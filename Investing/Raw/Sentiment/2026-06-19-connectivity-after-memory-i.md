After Memory, I Am Watching Connectivity — JP Insights (@Aktiehedonist), Jun 5 2026

Thesis: connectivity (everything that moves data between chips) is the next AI bottleneck after compute and memory — about to reprice from component to constraint. Evidence arrives from multiple chain layers simultaneously.

Key filter: margin expansion alongside revenue is the dividing line. Revenue-without-margin = capacity ramp, not pricing power.

**Architecture you cannot swap out**
- $AVGO: AI semi revenue $10.8B (+143% YoY), custom accelerator + networking driven. Stock fell on record quarter (no target raise) — priced for perfection.
- $ANET: Q1 revenue $2.71B (+35%); AI target raised to $3.5B (>2x); purchase commitments $8.9B from $6.8B — committing ahead of demand.
- $CSCO: Q3 FY26 record $15.8B (+12%); AI orders $5.3B YTD, full-year target ~$9B (~4.5x YoY). Silicon One + Acacia both pulled in.
- $ALAB: Q1 $308.4M (+93%), GM >76%. Scorpio fabric switch shipping; PCIe 6 already >1/3 of revenue.
- $CRDO: FY26 revenue >3x to $1.3B; Q4 alone $437M (>full prior year); GM ~68%. CEO: "connectivity is the limiting factor at hundreds-of-thousands-of-GPU scale."
- $AMD: Pushing UALink/Ultra Ethernet/Pensando — open-camp counter to Nvidia scale-up lock-in.

**Reaching the income statement**
- $LITE: Q3 revenue +90% YoY to record $808M; operating margin +~700 bps in one quarter. Revenue + margin together = constraint captured.
- $GLW: Optical comms +36% YoY (8th straight quarter); 2+ more hyperscaler multi-year deals (scale of ~$6B Meta deal); Nvidia partnership. Long-range plan toward $40B run rate by 2030.
- $APH: Q1 sales +58% to $7.62B; adj. operating margin 27.3%; orders record $9.4B, book-to-bill 1.24. IT datacom ~41% of revenue, +~80% organic.
- $COHR: Revenue $1.81B (+21%); datacenter/comms ~75% of company; order book into 2028; margins expanded.
- $FN: Revenue record $1.214B (+39%); datacom shipments held back by component shortages — supply, not demand, is the constraint.
- $AAOI: Q1 +51% to $151M; datacenter +154% on 800G ramp. GM stuck at ~29%, still losing money. Counter-example: inside the bottleneck ≠ capturing its economics.

**Constraint reaches past the rack**
- $CIEN: Q2 +40% to $1.57B; guidance raised to $6.3B; backlog record $7.7B. Stock -17% on the day — raise not large enough for a priced-for-perfection multiple.
- $NOK: AI/cloud +49%, €1B new orders; optical +20%. But AI/cloud still ~8% of group; company-wide margin ~6%. Re-rating requires margin proof.
- $MRVL: Q1 $2.42B (+28%); data center 76% of total; FY27/28 outlook raised on "exceptional AI bookings." Trap: $0.04 GAAP vs $0.80 non-GAAP gap shows acquisition overhang.

**Deeper than the obvious layer**
- $AXTI (AXT): Q1 $26.9M (+39%); InP >50% of sales for first time; InP backlog record $100M; non-GAAP GM 29.9% vs 21.5% prior quarter. $632.5M equity raise to ~double InP capacity in 2026 and again in 2027. Key risk: China export permits gate every shipment.
- AIX.DE (Aixtron): Revenue -47%, operating loss (batch-machine lumpiness). But order intake +~30% to ~€171M; >65% from optoelectronics; FY guidance raised to ~€560M. Lumentum placed multi-system order for AI optics tooling. Chain: GPU → switch → cable → module → laser → wafer → epitaxy tool.

**Five confirmation tests (and where each stands):**
1. Supply constraints at multiple chain points — ✓ (FN, CIEN, ANET all showing it)
2. Margin expansion alongside revenue — partial; LITE/COHR/APH/AVGO/ALAB/CRDO yes; AAOI no
3. Customer prepayments and long contracts — ✓ (GLW, COHR, CSCO)
4. Architecture lock-in — open; AVGO/ALAB sticky, but standards still contested
5. Theme surviving bad stock reactions — ✓ (CRDO, AVGO, CIEN sold off on strong prints; businesses intact)

**Disclosure — Best Ideas 2026 basket:** $NVDA, $AVGO, $CDNS, $VRT, POWL, $ETN, $HUBB, $ANET, $CRDO, $ALAB (10 equal-weighted). Holdings from this article: AVGO, ANET, CRDO, ALAB.

Memory comparison: HBM succeeded because memory stopped behaving like a cyclical component and became a gating function — investors shifted from asking "is it cheap?" to "is there enough?" Connectivity may be entering the same door, but the value will land unevenly across a wider, noisier supply chain.
