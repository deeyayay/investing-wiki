## Summary
nLIGHT's FY2025 10-K confirms the defense-first thesis with a dedicated Advanced Development segment focused exclusively on government directed energy and laser sensing programs. Key structural insight: nLIGHT's core laser chips are built on **gallium arsenide (GaAs)**, not InP — a different supply chain than the rest of the photonics folder, making them a standalone thesis. Market cap as of June 30, 2025 was ~$953.5M, confirming mid-cap status. The company has "multi-hundred-kilowatt" directed energy capability on the roadmap — significantly beyond the 70kW milestone cited in our thesis note. Three end markets (Aerospace & Defense, Industrial, Microfabrication) provide revenue diversification.

## Technology Highlights
- **GaAs-based semiconductor lasers** — core chip uses gallium arsenide wafers (NOT InP; different supply chain than LITE/COHR/AXTI)
- **Vertical integration** from semiconductor chip → fiber laser system; proprietary active fiber doped with rare-earth elements
- **Programmable beam shaping** — programmable fiber lasers with real-time beam size/shape/pulse control; single laser replaces multiple fixed-beam units
- **Multi-hundred-kilowatt DEW systems** — demonstrated "multi-hundred-kilowatt" directed energy weapons capability; 70kW milestone is a floor, not a ceiling
- **Easy field serviceability** — designed for rapid field repair, high uptime; important for defense deployments
