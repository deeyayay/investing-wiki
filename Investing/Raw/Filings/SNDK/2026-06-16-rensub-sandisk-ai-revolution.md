## Key Thesis

When people talk about the AI buildout, they talk about chips. NVIDIA. GPUs. That's Layer 5. But look one layer down — Layer 6 is Memory. Every GPU in existence is useless without it. The datasets AI trains on, the model weights it runs on, the caches that let it answer your question in milliseconds — all of it lives in NAND flash storage. Not RAM. Not the cloud. Physical chips, in physical servers, that hold data even when the power is off.

There is no AI without storage. And there is no storage at this scale without NAND. SanDisk is one of two companies in the world that can supply it at hyperscaler volumes. The other is their manufacturing partner, Kioxia.

## Section 1: Company Snapshot

SanDisk was founded in 1988 by Eli Harari, Sanjay Mehrotra, and Jack Yuan. They invented the consumer flash card industry almost from scratch.

In 2016, Western Digital acquired SanDisk for $19 billion and tucked it inside its hard drive empire.

Then, on **February 21, 2025**, SanDisk came back to life. Western Digital split into two companies: WD (hard drives) and SanDisk (flash). The company relisted on Nasdaq under the ticker SNDK at roughly $36 per share.

It now trades at $2,107. That is a +5,550% move in 16 months.

## Section 4: Product & Technology

SanDisk co-develops its NAND with Kioxia through **Flash Ventures** — a 25-year joint venture, 8 factories in Japan. SanDisk owns 49.9% of each venture entity. Not a supplier relationship — a co-creator relationship. JV extended through **2034** with $1.17 billion in guaranteed manufacturing payments.

**BiCS8** — 218-layer architecture. SanDisk's leading-edge NAND generation.

**128TB SSD** — built specifically for AI infrastructure. Q4 FY2026 launch.

## Section 5: The New Business Model (NBM)

The NBM is the single most important structural change in NAND in 30 years.

- **Multi-year contracts** with hyperscalers (Amazon, Microsoft, and others)
- Contracts lock in **volume**, **guaranteed minimum payments**, and **structured pricing**
- Customers who sign get **supply priority**. Customers who don't get whatever's left.
- **$42B in contracted minimum revenue** across five hyperscalers

For 30 years NAND was pure commodity — prices up, margins up; prices down, margins collapse. NBM is the attempt to permanently break that cycle. So far it's working.

## Section 7: Macro & Industry Backdrop

**Training is a phase. Inference is forever.**

Every time you use an AI — ask ChatGPT or Claude a question, generate an image, get a code suggestion — the AI has to *retrieve* its knowledge before it can answer you. That retrieval, at millisecond speed, at billions of queries per day, is called **inference**. And it runs on flash storage.

- Training: AI learns (happens once or a few times per year)
- Inference: AI thinks (happens billions of times daily, growing at 56% annually through 2031)

Flash is exactly in the middle: fast enough, dense enough, affordable enough to deploy at the scale AI requires. Not RAM (too expensive and volatile). Not hard drives (too slow).

### Competitive Landscape — Why SanDisk Wins

| Competitor | Distraction |
|-----------|------------|
| Micron | Splits engineering between DRAM and NAND |
| Samsung | Runs a consumer electronics empire |
| WDC | Still sells spinning hard drives |
| SK Hynix | Laser-focused on supplying HBM to NVIDIA |
| **SanDisk** | **Does one thing — flash — for 35 years** |

Kioxia is the exception: they share the same factories, co-develop the same NAND, then compete for the same customers. Whatever Kioxia sells, the underlying NAND was made in a factory SanDisk half-owns.

**Morgan Stanley** recently upgraded both Micron and SanDisk, arguing the AI memory upcycle is prolonged and NAND will stay supply-constrained while AI demand ramps.

## Section 8: Risks

| Risk | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Memory cyclicality** | NAND has gone boom-to-bust for 30 years. NBM contracts help but don't eliminate it. Margins could compress from 78% to 25% in a down cycle. |
| **Kioxia dependency** | All NAND wafers from Flash Ventures in Japan. Geopolitical tension, natural disasters, or operational failures have no quick fix. JV extension locks in supply — and locks in the dependency. |
| **Valuation** | 32.3x forward P/E at peak-cycle margins. No room for execution misses. One weak quarter reprices 20–30% immediately. |
| **Customer concentration** | Handful of hyperscalers drive Datacenter revenue. NBM sets a floor, but these buyers are the most sophisticated in the world. |
| **Technology disruption** | HBM, CXL-attached memory, new non-volatile architectures could shrink flash's role. SanDisk is developing HBF to stay ahead. |
| **Stock already up 5,500%** | Not a reason to sell or buy — only business fundamentals matter. Past returns tell you nothing about future returns. |
| **CXMT (China)** | ChangXin Memory Technologies — state-backed, subsidized, no-profit-needed entrant. Currently 3–5 years behind SanDisk's BiCS8. Cannot compete on enterprise AI performance yet. Competes on price in consumer markets. |

**Bear case:** You are paying 32.3x forward P/E for a semiconductor manufacturer whose 78.4% gross margins and 69.98% operating margins are at historic peaks, in an industry with a 30-year track record of violent cycles, where the biggest customers are also the most sophisticated buyers in the world.

## Section 9: Valuation

At 32.3x FY2026 forward earnings, looks priced for perfection. But the 32x drops to ~11.7x on FY2027 earnings — right in line with Micron (~11.3x) — once contracted NBM revenue shows up in the books.

The whole bet: **does the 2027 earnings estimate hold through the next NAND cycle?**

- If NBM deals hold and margins stay above 70%: 32x falls to ~12x on its own; stock looks cheap looking back.
- If margins drop as in every past NAND cycle: 2027 estimate gets cut; 12x was never real; you're stuck with 32x on smaller earnings.

**Analyst median target: $1,702** — below current price of $2,107. Median analyst consensus is underweight current price.
