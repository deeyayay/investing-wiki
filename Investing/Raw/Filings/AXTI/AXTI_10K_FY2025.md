## Summary
AXT's FY2025 10-K reveals critical context often missed in the bull thesis: **all manufacturing is in China**, the company had a market cap of just ~$84M as of June 30, 2025 ($2.09/share) before the photonics wave lifted it dramatically, and Tongmei (the Beijing wafer manufacturing subsidiary) is pursuing a STAR Market listing in Shanghai. FY2025 revenue was $88.3M — modest in absolute terms but supported by a record $60M InP backlog. The company is a smaller reporting company (non-accelerated filer), meaning less disclosure scrutiny. The China manufacturing concentration is the most critical under-discussed risk.

## Critical Risks
1. **ALL manufacturing in China (PRC)** — Fremont CA is HQ only; every wafer is made in Beijing/China subsidiaries. Geopolitical risk is structural, not marginal.
2. **China export permit dependency** — Q4 FY2025 revenue miss was directly caused by export permit delays; cited throughout filing as ongoing operational risk
3. **Tongmei STAR Market listing** — Complex ongoing process with Chinese government approvals; withdrawal or cancellation by private equity investors in China is a listed risk
4. **Tiny size** — ~$84M market cap before the photonics wave; limited analyst coverage, potential liquidity issues
5. **Beijing government relocation** — Beijing city government moving offices near Tongzhou manufacturing facility creates long-term operational uncertainty

## Thesis Validation Points ✅
- InP substrate applications explicitly include: "High-speed data transfer in data centers to support AI applications" — directly validates the demand thesis
- Record $60M InP backlog despite revenue decline = demand significantly exceeds current supply
- Capacity doubling underway: manufacturing expansion in progress
- Vertically integrated supply chain in China (gallium, InP base material, pBN crucibles) — cost advantage but also concentration risk
