## One-Line Thesis

MP Materials is the only company in the US mining, processing, and manufacturing rare earth permanent magnets — a vertically integrated monopoly in an input that China controls globally and that every EV motor, wind turbine, and defense system depends on.

## Investment Thesis

Rare earth permanent magnets (NdFeB — neodymium-iron-boron) are the strongest permanent magnets known, and they power every electric vehicle traction motor, every direct-drive wind turbine, and every missile guidance system. There is no viable substitute at current performance requirements. China controls ~85% of global rare earth mining, ~90% of processing, and effectively all magnet manufacturing outside Japan. In 2024, China began restricting rare earth magnet exports as trade leverage — the same strategic playbook as semiconductors and solar panels.

**Mountain Pass + Fort Worth = vertical integration:** MP Materials mines and separates rare earth oxides at Mountain Pass (California) — the only operating rare earth mine in North America. The Fort Worth magnet facility (Stage III) converts those oxides into NdFeB permanent magnets. The GM 20-year supply agreement is the anchor customer: every Ultium platform EV motor (Chevrolet, GMC, Cadillac) will run on MP magnets. This is the closed-loop rare earth supply chain that the DoD has prioritized for 15 years and never successfully built until now.

**Why the score is Unrivaled:** Mountain Pass has decades of ore left. The processing and magnet manufacturing IP took years to develop. No US competitor exists. The DoD has strategic interest in MP's success that effectively makes it a national security asset. China export restrictions are an ongoing demand accelerant. The GM relationship is a 20-year demand floor.

**The three-tier value chain:** Stage I (mining) generates positive cash flow at current REO prices. Stage II (separation) is operating and improving margins. Stage III (magnets) is ramping — the highest-value step that currently accrues to China. As Stage III scales, MP's margin per ton of rare earth extracted compounds dramatically.

**Risk:** REO prices are volatile and have been suppressed by Chinese overproduction. James Litinsky is a former hedge fund manager (not a mining operator) — execution risk on Stage III magnet manufacturing is real. The Fort Worth facility is first-of-kind in the US. DoD contracts are helpful but still a small revenue contributor.
