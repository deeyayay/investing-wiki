## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** May 31, 2026
> **Last validated:** May 31, 2026
> **Drift status:** On track — initial thesis established; monitor for post-IPO margin recovery and defense backlog conversion

The Elmet Group is a U.S. defense and critical materials company operating through two divisions. The Critical Materials Components (CMC) division is the only U.S.-owned, vertically integrated producer of engineered tungsten (W) and molybdenum (Mo) products — controlling the full process from powder production through machining to finished precision components. CMC serves aerospace, defense, medical imaging (Mo-99), semiconductor manufacturing (W sputter targets), nuclear energy, and industrial applications. The Engineered Microwave Products (EMP) division designs and manufactures high-power RF/microwave components and systems — waveguides, circulators, loads, pressure windows, automatic tuning systems — primarily for defense directed energy weapons, electronic warfare, and radar platforms. Combined, the company supports 100+ U.S. defense programs and 95+ national laboratory initiatives.

The structural bull case is a convergence of three macro tailwinds: (1) U.S. critical materials decoupling from China — China controls ~80% of global tungsten production; any executive-order-level supply chain mandate or NDAA provision for domestic-sourced refractory metals benefits ELMT directly as the only domestic vertical integrator; (2) defense spending acceleration — the EMP division's high-power microwave/directed energy backlog tracks DoD's sustained investment in electronic warfare and directed energy weapon platforms (a budget-protected, growing segment); (3) semiconductor and nuclear manufacturing onshoring — W sputter targets are consumed in chip fabs at every new wafer start; nuclear energy resurgence (SMR build-out) drives demand for W/Mo components in reactor hardware. Q1 2026 confirmed inflection: revenue +20.7% to $56.0M, gross margin expanded to 21.2% (from 18.6%), adjusted EBITDA doubled to $9.2M, and the order backlog hit a record $113.3M (up 51.7% vs. Q1 2025).

The moat is structural and difficult to replicate: (1) only domestic vertically integrated W/Mo producer — vertical integration from raw powder to finished parts is a capital-intensive, qualification-intensive process that took decades to build; no new entrant can replicate quickly; (2) 100+ defense program qualifications — each qualification is a 2–5 year process-approval barrier; the installed base of active qualifications is essentially a license to compete that others cannot rapidly obtain; (3) non-PRC tungsten/molybdenum sourcing — ELMT has locked strategic offtake agreements (including an A$30M tungsten concentrate agreement) that provide supply chain security precisely when buyers across defense and semis are mandated to de-risk PRC sourcing; (4) EMP niche — high-power microwave component design is a specialized engineering domain with few qualified vendors and multi-year customer co-development lock-in.

The risks are real and front-loaded. Net income collapsed in FY2025 from $15.4M to $5.5M on $201.6M revenue (+6%) — the margin compression story needs explanation and recovery evidence. The company is post-IPO ($125.5M raised April 2026 at $14/share) and will need to deploy capex to grow while absorbing public company costs. Revenue is concentrated in the Aerospace, Defense & Government segment, making results sensitive to DoD budget timing and contract award cycles. Tungsten and molybdenum are commodity inputs whose prices are volatile; ELMT's margin profile is exposed to raw material cost spikes if not fully passed through on contracts. The EPS for Q1 2026 was $(0.02) despite strong EBITDA, suggesting IPO costs and interest expense are near-term earnings headwinds.

**Key moat:** Only U.S.-owned vertically integrated W/Mo components producer; 100+ defense program qualifications; non-PRC strategic materials sourcing; EMP high-power microwave niche.

**Key risks:** FY2025 net income compression ($15.4M → $5.5M); post-IPO cost absorption; defense budget concentration risk; tungsten/molybdenum commodity price volatility; ELMT is a newly-public small-cap with limited sell-side coverage.
