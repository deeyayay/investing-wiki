## One-Line Thesis

Freeport-McMoRan is the world's largest publicly traded copper producer with a structural supply deficit, new leaching technology that adds 2B+ lbs at minimal capex, and copper positioned as the indispensable metal for electrification — at a cyclical discount.

## Investment Thesis

Copper is the metal of electrification. Every EV requires 80-100kg of copper (vs. 20kg for ICE). Every MW of offshore wind needs 8,000kg. Every AI data center adds copper wiring, busbars, and transformers. The copper supply deficit is structural: no meaningful new copper mines have been permitted in the US or Canada in decades, and greenfield projects in Chile and Peru face 15-20 year development timelines and permitting/community challenges.

**Grasberg's irreplaceable position:** The Grasberg mine complex in Indonesian Papua is one of the world's largest gold-copper deposits. The underground transition (Block Cave mining) is now complete after years of investment, and Grasberg is ramping to full production capacity — roughly 1.5B lbs of copper and 1.6Moz of gold per year from one site. The gold is a byproduct that dramatically lowers the net cost of copper production.

**The leaching technology unlock:** Freeport's new atmospheric leaching process can extract copper from previously uneconomic oxide ore deposits in existing mine footprints. The company has identified 2B+ lbs of incremental annual production potential from this technology — without new greenfield capex. If the technology scales as expected, FCX's production profile improves dramatically without committing billions to new mines.

**Why copper at this moment:** The AI data center buildout has been largely underestimated in its copper intensity. Each NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 rack requires ~50kg of copper busbars and wiring. A 200MW data center campus requires thousands of tons of copper transformers and power delivery equipment. The copper demand from AI infrastructure is in addition to the structural EV and grid demand.

**Risk:** Copper is exchange-priced — FCX has almost no pricing power. A global recession reduces copper demand and price. Grasberg faces persistent Indonesian regulatory and community relations risk. The leaching technology may not scale as projected. Kathleen Quirk is a new CEO with a CFO background — operational execution risk.
