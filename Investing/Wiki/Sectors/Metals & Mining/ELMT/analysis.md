## One-Line Thesis

Elmet is the only major US manufacturer of tungsten and molybdenum specialty products — a profitable, 80-year-old refractory metals franchise that sits at the intersection of defense onshoring and semiconductor manufacturing, with China controlling 85% of global tungsten supply.

## Investment Thesis

Tungsten is the densest common metal, with the highest melting point of any element. It is irreplaceable in applications requiring extreme hardness, temperature resistance, or radiation shielding: kinetic energy penetrators (armor-piercing ammunition), aircraft engine components, X-ray targets, cutting tool inserts, and — critically — semiconductor manufacturing. Every logic chip manufactured uses tungsten chemical vapor deposition (CVD) to fill the metal contacts that wire transistors together; every PCB drill bit is tungsten carbide. China produces ~85% of global tungsten and has imposed export restrictions.

**The defense demand driver:** The US Army is the world's largest consumer of kinetic energy penetrators (120mm rounds for Abrams tanks). These require dense tungsten alloy cores. Elmet is the DoD-qualified domestic supplier. As the US defense industrial base ramps following the Ukraine conflict and Pacific deterrence posture, tungsten demand for munitions is growing. Elmet has capacity and defense qualifications that new entrants would require years to replicate.

**The semiconductor demand driver:** Tungsten CVD is a critical process step in logic chip manufacturing. As AI chip demand drives wafer fab utilization higher and new fabs (Intel Ohio, TSMC Arizona, Samsung Texas) come online, tungsten target consumption grows. Elmet participates in this supply chain with its precision tungsten products.

**The moat:** 80 years of refractory metals manufacturing expertise, DoD qualifications, and established supply relationships with defense primes (Raytheon, L3Harris, General Dynamics) and semiconductor equipment makers. New entrants face a multi-year qualification process and require rare engineering expertise in high-temperature metals processing.

**Why not higher:** Elmet is a small specialty manufacturer ($190M revenue) with a professional management team that lacks founder-level ownership and vision. The business is stable and profitable but not a high-growth compounder. Tungsten is priced on London Metal Exchange — pricing power exists for specialty forms but is limited for commodity products.
