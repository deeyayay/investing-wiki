## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** May 31, 2026
> **Last validated:** May 31, 2026
> **Drift status:** On track — initial thesis established; monitor for first earnings validation

FormFactor (NASDAQ: FORM) designs and manufactures wafer probe cards — the interfaces between ATE and the wafer under test. Its probe cards are installed in semiconductor fabs to test DRAM, NAND, logic ICs, and RF devices at the wafer level. In 2024, revenue grew 15.2% to $764M with 40.3% gross margin. The stock surged from ~$3.4B market cap in early 2025 to ~$11.5B by May 2026 as the silicon photonics test opportunity was priced in.

The SiPh/CPO expansion is the primary re-rating driver. In November 2024, FormFactor and Advantest announced a joint development program for wafer-level SiPh/CPO test. In April 2025, the V93000-Triton system launched — combining Advantest's V93000 ATE with FormFactor's OptoVue Pro 9-axis optical alignment probe that achieves the lowest coupling loss of any production probe system. In December 2025, FormFactor acquired Keystone Photonics, a specialist in optical probing for SiPh/CPO, to accelerate the capability and consolidate IP.

The scale of the opportunity: FormFactor's OptoVue Pro systems are already in use at over 100 SiPh device manufacturers. As CPO moves from prototype to high-volume production (TSMC COUPE 2026, Samsung CPO 2029), every wafer requires opto-electrical test before packaging. FormFactor's probe hardware is the physical interface that makes this possible. There is no competing production-grade optical probe system for CPO test — the Advantest/FormFactor cell is the only announced solution.

The traditional wafer probe business continues to grow: AI SoC complexity drives probe card ASP higher as pin counts scale. Memory probe cards benefit from DRAM density increases (HBM, DDR5). FormFactor's installed base and software ecosystem (probe recipe management, yield analytics) create durable switching costs in customer fabs.

**Key moat (Semiconductors):** Largest probe card installed base globally; over 100 SiPh customer deployments; recipe management software creates switching costs; Keystone Photonics acquisition consolidates optical probe IP.

**Key moat (Photonics):** V93000-Triton partnership with Advantest creates the only production-grade SiPh/CPO opto-electrical test cell; first-mover timing advantage matches TSMC's 2026 CPO production schedule.

**Key risks:** Probe card business is semiconductor capex-correlated and cyclical. CPO test revenue is early-stage and dependent on CPO adoption timeline. Partnership risk with Advantest — if the V93000-Triton relationship weakens, FormFactor needs to develop standalone ATE capability or find alternative partners. Market cap has surged ~3x in 18 months; valuation risk if CPO timeline extends.
