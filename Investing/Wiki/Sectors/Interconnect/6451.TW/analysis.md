## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-05-31
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-18
> **Drift status:** On track, accelerating — CPO TAM $164M→$91B (Goldman Sachs, 2026–2028); FY2025 revenue +45% YoY confirms ramp; net margin collapse to 0.4% is expected trough before CPO economics kick in

ShunSin Technology is the optical packaging and assembly arm of the Foxconn industrial ecosystem, listed on the Taiwan Stock Exchange. FY 2025 revenue was NT$7.53B (+45% YoY, ~USD$232M), with net income collapsing to NT$27.5M (-36%) as margin compression hit 0.4% net — a classic transition-year pattern where volume ramps ahead of pricing power. Analysts project 1,240% EPS growth for 2026 as CPO manufacturing economics normalize.

The core bull case is a structural margin inflection. As an OSAT-style optical assembler, ShunSin historically operated at 15–16% gross margins — thin margins typical of packaging and test. The shift to CPO for AI datacenter networking changes the economics materially: CPO assembly requires FAU (fiber array unit) optical alignment — a precision optical coupling step that demands specialized process capability and is not interchangeable with standard chip packaging. ShunSin has established itself in NVIDIA's CPO supply chain for this harder alignment work, and the margin profile of this business is estimated at 30–35% gross margin. TSMC COUPE (mass production started early 2026) is the enabling foundry technology; ShunSin provides the system-level integration and alignment layer on top.

Goldman Sachs sized the CPO market at $164M in 2026 growing to $91B in 2028 — a 55× expansion in two years that is being driven by NVIDIA datacenter GPU deployments (H100, B200, and beyond) where each unit requires optical interconnects that pluggable transceivers cannot deliver at scale.

ShunSin's Foxconn/Hon Hai parentage is a double-edged strategic asset. It provides direct supply chain access via the world's largest electronics manufacturer and Hon Hai's ODM relationships with hyperscalers — effectively making ShunSin the "Taiwan NVDA CPO supply chain idea #1" per analyst consensus. However, Foxconn as controlling shareholder also means ShunSin's strategic priorities are subordinated to the parent, and related-party dynamics may limit independent capital allocation. FOCI (a TWSE-listed FAU leader) is the complementary company rather than a direct competitor — FOCI leads FAU fabrication, ShunSin leads system integration; the total CPO packaging TAM is projected at NT$40B+ by 2028.

**Key moat:** FAU optical alignment process capability in NVIDIA CPO supply chain; Foxconn/Hon Hai ecosystem access for hyperscaler relationships; TSMC COUPE as underlying enabling technology; precision MEMS and optical packaging expertise not easily replicated at scale.

**Key risks:** CPO adoption ramp timing risk; net margin trough (0.4%) may persist if CPO ASPs compress; Foxconn parent strategic control limits independent capital allocation; margin uplift thesis depends on CPO content growing to material revenue share; FOCI competitive dynamic needs monitoring as FAU market matures.
