## Investment Thesis

MACOM designs and manufactures high-performance analog and photonic semiconductors for data center optical interconnects (800G/1.6T), defense/industrial RF and radar, and telecom infrastructure. The company crossed $1 billion in annualized revenue in early FY26 — a scale inflection that unlocks operating leverage across its vertically integrated fab network. Data center revenue grew 48% in FY25 and is tracking above 60% growth in FY26, driven by design wins at all major optical module makers. Defense and industrial represent ~43% of revenue, growing 19% YoY with GaN product revenue up 50%, providing pricing power and cash flow stability while peers remain tethered to cyclical mobile RF.

The bull case rests on three reinforcing secular trends converging simultaneously: AI hyperscalers upgrading spine and fabric interconnects to 800G/1.6T (MACOM's InP-based optical components are in the critical path), defense modernization programs requiring GaN-on-SiC radar and electronic warfare upgrades, and 5G/6G densification demanding higher-frequency RF. Gross margins are expanding toward 59-60% as optical mix rises and fab utilization improves — a profile more comparable to analog leaders than to mobile-RF peers running in the low-40s. Operating cash flow is expected to exceed $300M in FY26, up from $235M in FY25.

The key moat is process breadth and performance leadership across compound semiconductor materials. MACOM holds proprietary GaN-on-SiC processes, Trusted Foundry status for classified defense programs, and an exclusive license to HRL's 40 nm T3L GaN process enabling operation above 40 GHz. This multi-material capability (GaAs, GaN, InP, SiGe) creates design-win stickiness: once a system architect qualifies MACOM at the highest-performance node, switching is a multi-year re-qualification. Commodity fabs cannot replicate this process portfolio.

Risks are real. Hyperscale optics orders are lumpy — a pause in AI capex or a shift in interconnect architecture could defer revenue ramps sharply. Defense contracts carry program timing uncertainty that creates quarterly revenue volatility. Valuation already prices in strong execution, leaving limited margin for error. Insider selling (net sellers in recent quarters) is worth monitoring as a sentiment signal even if it reflects planned diversification.

**Key moat:** Proprietary GaN-on-SiC and InP process technology with Trusted Foundry status; multi-material compound semiconductor breadth competitors cannot replicate without decade-scale investment.

**Key risks:** Hyperscale optics cyclicality; defense program timing lumpiness; rich valuation leaves little room for execution misses; net insider selling in recent quarters.
