## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** May 31, 2026
> **Last validated:** May 31, 2026
> **Drift status:** On track — initial thesis established; monitor for first earnings validation

Prysmian is the world's largest wire and cable group by revenue (~€16B), operating at the intersection of two infrastructure supercycles: AI datacenter fiber buildout and the offshore wind/grid interconnect expansion. Its four business segments — Transmission (submarine/HVDC), Power Grid, Electrification, and Digital Solutions — give it exposure to every cable-intensive capital expenditure theme running simultaneously through 2030.

In the optical fiber context, Prysmian's Digital Solutions segment develops ultra-high-density fiber cables (up to 6,912 fiber strands) for hyperscale datacenter inter-rack and campus connectivity — competing directly with Corning as the #2 global fiber maker. Its OVD preform process and global draw capacity place it in the structural duopoly that controls the bottleneck in fiber supply. As datacenter fiber demand grows from ~570M fiber-km (2025) toward 710M fiber-km (2030), Prysmian shares the capacity constraint with Corning.

The underappreciated bull case is Prysmian's order backlog: over €18B entered 2025, dominated by HVDC submarine cable contracts for offshore wind export systems and transatlantic/European grid interconnectors. Submarine cable installation is a multi-year, cost-plus EPC business with 3–5 year revenue visibility. Management guides FY2026 adjusted EBITDA of €2,625–2,775M and FCF of €1,300–1,400M — driven by margin expansion as higher-value subsea work displaces lower-margin distribution cable volumes.

The key risk is project execution — submarine cable contracts are complex turnkey builds where cost overruns can erode margin. Currency risk (EUR vs USD/JPY) affects competitive positioning in non-European markets. Capital intensity of capacity expansion (Arco Felice, Pikkala, Nordenham plant upgrades) creates near-term FCF headwinds.

**Key moat:** Global #2 optical fiber draw capacity alongside Corning; dominant submarine cable backlog with 3–5 year forward visibility; OVD and HVDC process IP not replicable at scale without $500M+ capex.

**Key risks:** Project execution risk on complex HVDC contracts; capex cycle for capacity expansion compresses near-term FCF; EUR currency headwinds in non-European revenue; Italian industrial governance risk (headquartered in Milan).
