## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-15 / **Last validated:** 2026-06-15 / **Drift status:** New

Monolithic Power Systems is a founder-led (Michael Hsing) fabless analog and power-management semiconductor company whose moat rests on a proprietary BCD process co-developed with foundry partners and a deep library of analog design IP. Analog/power parts are sticky: once a power-management IC is designed into a board, it is hard and risky to second-source, which gives MPS durable pricing power and high gross margins. The company has methodically expanded from consumer/industrial into automotive, enterprise data, and — most importantly now — AI data-center power.

The AI angle is the growth engine. As accelerators draw more power per node and racks move toward 800V DC architectures, power delivery becomes a board-level bottleneck. MPS's vertical power modules (power delivered directly beneath the processor) are designed into NVIDIA Blackwell and next-gen Vera Rubin board power, making MPS a direct shovel-seller to the GPU buildout. This wires it to the AI Buildout Stack's **Power Infrastructure rail → "Power Management ICs"** box. The risks are concentration (NVIDIA-linked demand cycles), competitive entry from larger analog incumbents, and a premium valuation that prices in continued share gains.

**Key moat:** Proprietary BCD process + analog IP + design-win lock-in (founder-led engineering culture; net-cash balance sheet).
**Key risks:** Demand concentration in NVIDIA-linked data-center power; valuation premium; CFO transition (interim Rob Dean after Blegen's retirement); larger analog rivals targeting the socket.
