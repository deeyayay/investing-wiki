## One-Line Thesis
The broadest US-listed passive- and discrete-components franchise — tantalum capacitors, MLCCs, film/electrolytic caps, resistors, inductors, diodes, and MOSFETs — and a quiet AI-infrastructure beneficiary as passive and power-discrete content per server multiplies.

## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-15 / **Last validated:** 2026-06-15 / **Drift status:** New

Vishay is the one-stop broad-line supplier of the unglamorous-but-essential components on every PCB. Its breadth — tantalum, ceramic (MLCC), film, and electrolytic capacitors plus resistors, inductors, diodes, and MOSFETs — is itself the moat: customers value single-source qualification across an entire bill of materials, and designed-in passives carry real switching cost. The franchise is cyclical and capital-intensive; the current "Vishay 3.0" program (CEO Joel Smejkal) is a multi-year internal-capacity expansion meant to capture the electrification and AI-content cycle rather than cede it to Asian competitors.

The AI relevance is indirect but structural: server power density and board complexity drive more capacitors, inductors, and power discretes per unit, and Vishay's **tantalum capacitor** line gives direct exposure to L12 critical-minerals (tantalum sourcing) while its MLCC/passive breadth maps to the Power Infrastructure rail's "MLCC and Passive Components." This is a content-growth story layered on a cyclical base, not a pure-play AI name. Q1 2026 results (revenue ~$839M) showed a return to profitability with a stronger Q2 guide (~$875–905M), suggesting the cycle is turning.

**Key moat:** Scale + portfolio breadth + qualification/switching cost across the full passive/discrete BOM.
**Key risks:** Component-cycle volatility; pricing pressure in commoditized lines; heavy capex during the 3.0 expansion; exposure diluted across many non-AI end markets.
