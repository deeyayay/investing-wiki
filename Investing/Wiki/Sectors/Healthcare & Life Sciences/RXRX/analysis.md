## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-22
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-22
> **Drift status:** On track — runway extended to 2028; cost discipline ahead of guidance

Recursion is building a vertically integrated AI drug discovery operating system: automated wet labs generate phenomics data at industrial scale, BioHive-2 trains biological foundation models on that data, and the resulting predictions feed both internal drug programs and high-value pharma partnerships. The core asset is the dataset — 65 petabytes of proprietary biological and chemical images spanning 300 million unique experiments — which no competitor can replicate without years of lab automation investment. The November 2024 acquisition of Exscientia (for $630M) added generative small-molecule chemistry to the platform and brought 20+ pipeline programs, including partnered and internal assets, making Recursion the most integrated AI-first drug discovery company by capability breadth.

The bull case is a data flywheel that compounds: more experiments → better models → better drug candidates → more pharma partnerships → more milestone funding → more experiments. Cumulative collaboration inflows exceed $500M (Roche/Genentech $213M, Sanofi $134M), validating the platform to large pharma. BioHive-2, the world's most powerful supercomputer wholly owned by a biopharma company, gives Recursion AI training capacity that most biotech companies must rent from hyperscalers. The 2025 Boltz-2 model release demonstrated biological foundation model capability competitive with AlphaFold on structure prediction tasks, signaling that the platform's AI layer is maturing.

The transition from founder-CEO Chris Gibson to Najat Khan (eff. Jan 2026) is a meaningful inflection: Khan is an operational leader from J&J's data science organization, signaling a shift from platform-building to clinical execution. The company ended 2025 with $753.9M cash (~$400M operating cash burn in 2025, guiding below $390M in 2026), giving a runway into early 2028. The path to value realization is milestone payments from partners and, ultimately, approved drugs from the internal pipeline.

Key risks: Recursion has no approved drugs and is burning ~$400M per year. Clinical success rates for AI-designed drug candidates remain unproven at scale. The Exscientia integration adds execution complexity. Roche and Sanofi partnerships have defined scopes — failure to expand or renew those contracts shrinks the revenue base. CEO transition risk is real: Khan must execute clinical programs while the platform continues to scale. At current cash burn, 2028 is a hard runway deadline that concentrates deal-making risk.

**Key moat:** Proprietary phenomics dataset at unmatched scale (65PB); BioHive-2 compute infrastructure; end-to-end wet-lab-to-AI platform; $500M+ in pharma validation via milestone payments.

**Key risks:** Pre-revenue from product sales; ~$400M annual cash burn; no approved drugs; integration execution risk post-Exscientia; CEO transition; 2028 runway deadline creates deal-making pressure.
