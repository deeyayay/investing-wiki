## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-22
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-22
> **Drift status:** On track — hosted transition and ACV growth on guidance

Schrödinger develops and sells the only commercially available physics-based molecular simulation platform at the rigor required for real drug candidate optimization. Its Free Energy Perturbation Plus (FEP+) engine is the de facto standard for binding affinity prediction at major pharmaceutical companies including Pfizer, Novartis, Bristol-Myers Squibb, and Bayer — each of which has invested years of proprietary data generation and workflow integration on top of Schrödinger's platform. This creates a rare switching cost profile in scientific software: changing platforms means abandoning accumulated internal benchmarks, trained ML models, and bespoke workflows, not just swapping a license.

The bull case runs on two tracks. The software segment (~$199M revenue in 2025, 74% gross margin) is transitioning from on-premise licensing to hosted cloud, with management guiding 10–15% ACV growth to $218–228M in 2026 and targeting high-70s gross margin as hosting scales. The drug discovery segment ($56M in 2025, doubled YoY) provides milestone and royalty optionality: Schrödinger takes equity stakes and milestone rights in collaborative programs with pharma partners, creating a free-option pipeline on top of the software business. Management targets positive adj. EBITDA by end of 2028.

The core moat is scientific. FEP+ accurately predicts small-molecule binding affinity at a level of physical rigor that ML-only approaches cannot match for novel chemotypes. The platform integrates deeply with medicinal chemistry workflows, and 218 PhD-level scientists maintain the scientific differentiation. With 87 active patents and no direct competitor offering the same physics-ML hybrid stack at commercial scale, Schrödinger occupies a defensible niche where accuracy — not speed — is the product.

Key risks: Schrödinger remains pre-EBITDA and burns cash while supporting a drug discovery pipeline with binary clinical outcomes. The hosted-model transition may create near-term revenue recognition lumpiness and ACV calculation changes. RXRX (having absorbed Exscientia's generative chemistry) is building a competing integrated platform. In-house computational biology expansion at major pharma could reduce outsourcing over time. At ~$832M market cap, any major pipeline failure or sustained ACV miss hits the multiple disproportionately.

**Key moat:** FEP+ physics-based simulation with deep pharma workflow integration; high switching cost; 87 patents; 218-PhD scientific staff maintain scientific edge.

**Key risks:** Pre-EBITDA cash burn; drug discovery binary risk; RXRX/Exscientia integration as emerging competitor; hosted-model transition lumpiness.
