## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-22
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-22
> **Drift status:** On track — 2025 revenue $1.27B (+83%); Q4 adj. EBITDA positive; 2026 guiding $65M adj. EBITDA

Tempus built what is likely the largest linked multimodal clinical dataset in oncology: 40 million connected patient records, 9 million de-identified and ingested, spanning genomic sequencing, imaging, pathology, and clinical outcomes, stitched together through 4,500+ EHR system integrations. This dataset is the durable asset — it is expensive to replicate (requires years of hospital partnership development and data licensing negotiation), improves continuously as more samples flow in, and enables AI applications that become more accurate as the dataset grows. The core insight is that better cancer treatment decisions require connecting the molecular (what gene mutations does this tumor carry?) with the clinical (what happened to patients with similar profiles?) — a combination that Tempus uniquely provides at commercial scale.

The business is a hybrid of two compounding revenue streams. Diagnostics ($955M in 2025, +111.5% YoY) is essentially a high-volume clinical lab for oncology and hereditary genetic testing — growing rapidly as oncologists adopt molecular profiling as standard of care. Data and Applications ($316M in 2025, +31% YoY) monetizes the dataset through analytics licenses, real-world evidence studies, and AI SaaS sold to biopharma and health systems — this segment carries ~80% gross margins versus ~60% for diagnostics. Net Revenue Retention of 126% demonstrates that data customers expand their spending each year as they integrate Tempus more deeply into their R&D workflows.

The AstraZeneca/Pathos partnership to build the largest oncology foundation model is the long-term platform bet: if Tempus data trains the best cancer AI models, those models pull in more clinical users, which generates more data, which trains better models. Tempus One (a GPT-backed clinical AI assistant integrated into Epic/Cerner workflows) is the access surface for this flywheel at the physician level. Founder-CEO Eric Lefkofsky owns ~27% of the company, creating unusually strong founder alignment at this scale.

Key risks: Tempus is still GAAP-loss-making (net loss $245M in 2025, though adj. EBITDA turned positive in Q4). Revenue growth is partly driven by acquisition-boosted diagnostics volume that may normalize. Epic and Cerner control the EHR moat that Tempus depends on for data access — any partnership disruption or Epic data-sharing restriction would impair the dataset. Google Health, Microsoft Health AI, and IQVIA all have competing data assets and distribution advantages. CFO and operational leadership depth below Lefkofsky is an open question.

**Key moat:** 40M+ patient records + 4,500+ EHR integrations create a data network effect reinforced by high switching costs; 126% NRR indicates strong data product stickiness.

**Key risks:** GAAP-loss-making; Epic/Cerner EHR dependency; big-tech healthcare AI competition; diagnostics revenue concentration in oncology; key-man risk (Lefkofsky ~27%).
