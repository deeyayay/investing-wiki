## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-26
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-26
> **Drift status:** On track — SiC leadership intact; gross margin trough appears to be in; data center (AWS) adds new growth leg

STMicroelectronics is a fully vertically integrated semiconductor IDM — designing, fabricating, packaging, and testing its own chips across three core franchises: SiC/GaN power devices, microcontrollers (STM32), and MEMS sensors. Unlike fabless or pure-play foundry models, ST owns manufacturing assets in Catania (Italy), Crolles (France), Agrate (Italy), and Singapore, which provides process control advantages critical to automotive-grade reliability. The company holds ~27.5% French-Italian government ownership through a joint holding, conferring strategic-industrial protection but adding governance complexity.

The bull case rests on two compounding platforms. The first is SiC power semiconductors: ST is the global #1 with ~32–35% market share and is the only IDM with a fully vertically integrated SiC campus (substrate → epitaxy → device → package) in Catania. Every EV traction inverter, onboard charger, and high-voltage DC-DC converter is a potential SiC socket; as EV penetration ramps through 2027–2030, that socket count compounds. ST's design-win pipeline predates most competitors', giving it a structural head start in automotive SiC that takes 3–5 years for an OEM to replace even if they wanted to. The second platform is the STM32 microcontroller family — the global #1 32-bit general-purpose MCU. With 1,800+ ecosystem partners, a mature STM32Cube IDE and HAL software stack, and millions of engineers trained on its pinout and peripherals, switching costs approach "must replace the whole design" territory. The STM32 franchise monetizes industrial IoT, edge AI inference, smart home, and medical device megatrends without requiring ST to compete directly in leading-edge logic. A third emerging leg — confirmed in Q4 2025 — is a multi-year, multi-billion USD collaboration with AWS for high-performance compute infrastructure, guiding data center revenue to "nicely above $500M" in 2026 and "well above $1B" in 2027.

The moat is layered. STM32's ecosystem creates customer lock-in analogous to platform software: the hardware is cheap; the switching cost is re-engineering the entire product. SiC moat is rooted in proprietary device IP (~21,000 patents), process know-how built over 15+ years, and the capital-intensive Catania campus that is essentially irreplicable without €2B+ and a decade of learning. MEMS uses the proprietary ThELMA process to produce sensors at performance-cost combinations that ST's specialized fabs enable but a standard CMOS foundry cannot match.

Key risks are real. Gross margins collapsed from ~47% (2023 peak) to ~34% (FY2025) on automotive inventory digestion and lower fab utilization — a structural headwind until SiC volumes recover and Catania capacity fills. onsemi is the most aggressive SiC competitor: with its EliteSiC 8-inch ramp and GTAT substrate self-sufficiency, it has climbed from #3 to #2 and is winning new automotive design wins on price. Government ownership creates industrial-policy entanglement (illustrated by Italy's April 2025 insider trading accusation against the CEO, unanimously rejected by the board, but emblematic of political risk). The China SiC JV with Sanan Optoelectronics adds geopolitical exposure as U.S./EU export controls tighten.

**Key moat:** STM32 platform ecosystem with multi-year switching costs; vertically integrated Catania SiC campus; 15+ years of SiC process IP.

**Key risks:** Gross margin recovery timeline (33–35% vs. 40%+ target); onsemi SiC share gain; government ownership constraints; China JV exposure to export control tightening.
