# Micron Technology — Investment Analysis

## One-Line Thesis

Only US-listed pure-play HBM maker ramping NVIDIA's Vera Rubin supply; structural margin expansion as HBM penetration grows.

## Investment Thesis

| Date | Status | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 2026-06-12 | Initial | Added to L06 Memory tier as HBM NVIDIA-qualified pure-play |

Micron sits in the most critical node of the AI memory supply chain: the sole US-based High Bandwidth Memory supplier for NVIDIA's next-generation AI accelerators. Unlike competitors SK Hynix and Samsung, Micron operates under US national security clearance, making it the preferred choice for US hyperscalers and defense/government AI infrastructure deployments.

The bull case hinges on three structural shifts: (1) NVIDIA's Vera Rubin (Blackwell) platform requires higher HBM4 density, driving Micron's already-ramped capacity to full utilization; (2) hyperscalers are diversifying away from single-source (SK Hynix) HBM suppliers, elevating Micron's negotiating position and ASP ceiling; (3) HBM's per-GPU content is increasing as model context windows expand, creating a multi-year secular tailwind independent of GPU unit demand.

The moat is defensible: Micron has invested $15B+ in advanced wafer capacity, secured NVIDIA qualification years ago, and maintains first-mover advantage in Vera Rubin supply contracts. Pricing power is structural — as HBM becomes a bottleneck, chipmakers will accept higher ASPs to secure supply.

Risks center on NVIDIA GPU demand cycles, yield ramp execution, and potential supply diversification to SK Hynix if geopolitical tensions ease. The company also faces legacy DRAM/NAND exposure that could dilute AI-driven valuation multiple re-rating.

**Key moat:** US national security clearance + NVIDIA first-mover advantage + capital-intensive HBM fab barriers.

**Key risks:** GPU demand cliff, competitive yield catches, geopolitical thaw enabling Korean competitors.
