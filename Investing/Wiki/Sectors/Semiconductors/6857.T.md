## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** May 31, 2026
> **Last validated:** May 31, 2026
> **Drift status:** On track — initial thesis established; monitor for first earnings validation

Advantest Corporation is a Tokyo-listed ATE company competing with Teradyne for semiconductor test equipment dominance. Its V93000 platform is the industry workhorse for logic IC test (SoC, AI accelerators, networking chips), and its T2000 platform serves DRAM and NAND memory test. Advantest's primary thesis is straightforward: as AI accelerator complexity grows and device-under-test pin counts scale, test time and ATE complexity grow, driving ASP and revenue per chip tested upward.

The new and differentiated angle as of 2025 is silicon photonics. In November 2024, Advantest and FormFactor announced a joint development agreement for a wafer-level SiPh/CPO test cell. In April 2025, they launched the V93000-Triton — a system that combines Advantest's V93000 ATE chassis (electrical test) with FormFactor's OptoVue Pro optical alignment probe (9-axis nano-precision fiber coupling). The result is the first production-grade system capable of testing both the electrical and optical functions of a silicon photonics chip or CPO module at the wafer level, in a single touchpoint.

Why this matters structurally: co-packaged optics cannot ship without per-wafer opto-electrical yield verification. As CPO ramps in AI GPU switch ASICs (TSMC targets COUPE production in 2026, Samsung targets 2029), every wafer needs to pass through a V93000-Triton equivalent. There is no production-proven alternative as of 2025. This makes the Advantest/FormFactor partnership a structural chokepoint in the CPO supply chain — the bottleneck for scaling CPO from prototype to high-volume manufacturing.

TrendForce (April 2026) explicitly identifies wafer-level SiPh/CPO testing as the critical bottleneck for CPO mass production. The V93000-Triton is the only announced solution meeting production-floor requirements.

**Key moat (Semiconductors):** V93000 platform installed base and software ecosystem lock-in; dominant AI accelerator test position (NVIDIA, AMD, Marvell test on Advantest); multi-year customer qualification cycles create high switching costs.

**Key moat (Photonics):** First-mover in production SiPh/CPO opto-electrical ATE; FormFactor partnership creates a combined optical+electrical test solution no single vendor can replicate alone; TSMC and Samsung CPO ramp schedules create a time-bounded demand event.

**Key risks:** Semiconductor ATE is cyclical — memory and logic capex can cut sharply in downturns. The CPO test revenue is currently pre-volume; if CPO adoption slips or TSMC COUPE schedule slips, the photonics upside is delayed. Teradyne and Keysight could both develop competing SiPh test capabilities.
