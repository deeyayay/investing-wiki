## One-Line Thesis

Infineon is the world's #1 automotive semiconductor and #1 SiC power manufacturer — the picks-and-shovels play on electrification, AI data center power, and grid decarbonization, at a European industrial valuation discount.

## Investment Thesis

Every electric vehicle needs power semiconductors in the traction inverter, on-board charger, DC/DC converter, and battery management system. Infineon's CoolSiC MOSFETs are embedded in 40+ OEM vehicle platforms for the 800V inverter architecture that enables 350kW fast charging. With ~30% global SiC revenue share and the Villach, Austria fab producing 200mm SiC wafers — a full generation ahead of US competitors still at 150mm — Infineon has a manufacturing cost advantage that compounds.

**The structural diversification moat:** Unlike pure-play power semi competitors, Infineon spans: (1) automotive (SiC inverters, ADAS power rails, EV charging), (2) data center power (48V AI server PSUs, power modules), (3) industrial (motor drives, renewable energy inverters), and (4) security chips (embedded secure elements in payment cards, passports, IoT). This diversification means no single end-market collapse can impair the whole business.

**200mm SiC advantage:** The transition from 150mm to 200mm SiC wafers reduces cost per die by ~40%. Infineon's Villach fab has been running 200mm SiC production since 2024. STMicroelectronics and Wolfspeed are 12-18 months behind. This cost advantage matters enormously when selling into cost-sensitive automotive OEMs.

**AI data center tailwind:** Every AI server rack requires a 48V power delivery bus with >97% efficiency conversion. Infineon's OptiMOS and CoolGaN family are designed for this application. Revenue is small today but the design-win pipeline is building.

**Why not Unrivaled:** Jochen Hanebeck is a professional manager with 0.1% ownership — low alignment. European industrial conglomerate structure means slower decision-making than US peers. The 2025 automotive semiconductor inventory correction is compressing near-term earnings. Competition from STMicro in SiC automotive, and from Chinese power semi vendors threatening lower-end market share.
