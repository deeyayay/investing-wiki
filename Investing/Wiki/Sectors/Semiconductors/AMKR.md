## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** May 19, 2026
> **Last validated:** May 19, 2026
> **Drift status:** On track — initial thesis established; monitor for advanced packaging revenue mix and US fab ramp

Semiconductor packaging has become as technically complex and value-added as chip design itself. As GPUs and AI ASICs push the limits of monolithic die integration, advanced packaging — 2.5D interposers, fan-out wafer-level packaging (FOWLP), and chiplet assembly — is the engineering bridge that connects multiple chiplets and HBM stacks into a single functional system. Amkor is one of only three or four companies globally capable of executing this at commercial scale (alongside TSMC captive, ASE Group, and a few others). Every AI chip that requires advanced packaging and cannot use TSMC's captive CoWoS capacity is a potential Amkor customer.

FY2025 revenue reached $6.71B (+6% YoY), with 83% from advanced packaging (9% CAGR from 2020–2025). Q1 2026 came in at a record $1.68B (+27% YoY) — a sharp acceleration suggesting the AI advanced packaging cycle is pulling forward. Amkor is investing aggressively: FY2026 capex is targeted at $2.5–$3.0B, including a new advanced packaging facility in Peoria, Arizona, co-funded with CHIPS Act investment — a strategic move to capture US-onshoring demand from hyperscalers and defense customers alike.

The Kim family controls ~49.4% of shares as of the 2026 proxy (down from 53.6% following a secondary offering in early 2026), meaning shareholders are effectively co-investing alongside a founding family that has built the business over five decades of semiconductor packaging experience.

**Key moat:** Global manufacturing footprint (27 facilities across Asia + US facility under construction), long-term customer co-development relationships with top fabless companies (Apple, Qualcomm, NVDA supply chain), advanced packaging IP, and CHIPS Act strategic positioning for US onshoring demand.

**Key risks:** Semiconductor cyclicality flows directly through (OSAT is a volume-leverage business), capital-intensive model creates high depreciation drag on margins, ASE Group is larger and more diversified, customer concentration risk (Apple believed ~20%+ of revenue), new CEO transition (Kevin Engel, January 2026) adds execution uncertainty.

**Execution track record:** Engel is a new CEO (January 2026) with deep Amkor institutional knowledge — he joined via the 2004 Unitive Electronics acquisition and has operated across Amkor US and Taiwan for 20+ years. The continuity in operating knowledge partially offsets the transition risk. Prior CEO leadership drove the advanced packaging mix from ~60% to 83% of revenue over 2020–2025, demonstrating consistent strategic execution toward higher-value services. FY2026 Q1 record revenue of $1.68B (+27% YoY) is Engel's first quarter as CEO — early positive signal.

**Insider ownership / alignment:** Kim family controls ~49.4% of outstanding shares as of March 2026 (down from 53.6% in 2025 after a secondary offering). Director Susan Kim holds ~6.1M shares individually. DEF 14A filed FY2026 (accession via SEC EDGAR, CIK 0001047127). Family control at nearly 50% is dual-edged: strong alignment with long-term value creation, but limits institutional governance and buyback flexibility. Capital allocation policy targets 40–50% FCF payout (dividends + $300M buyback program).
