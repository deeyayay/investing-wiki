## Investment Thesis
Apple Silicon — the M-series in Macs and iPads, and the A-series in iPhones — represents the most widely deployed custom AI silicon in the world. The Neural Engine cores baked into each chip run on-device inference for Apple Intelligence features (summarization, image generation, writing tools, Siri reasoning) without routing sensitive data to third-party servers. With the A19 Pro capable of trillions of neural operations per second and the M5 generation designed explicitly around AI workloads, Apple has a structural efficiency lead over x86 competitors (Intel, AMD) on performance-per-watt for inference at the edge.

The installed base is the moat. Roughly 2.2 billion active Apple devices create a captive inference platform that no cloud hyperscaler or PC chip vendor can replicate. Apple Intelligence launched on iPhone 15 Pro and iPhone 16 series and expanded to Macs and iPads; the iPhone 17 cycle (fiscal Q2 2026: $111.2 billion revenue, a March quarter record) confirms AI features are pulling upgrade demand. Private Cloud Compute (PCC) extends on-device privacy guarantees to server-side tasks using Apple-designed server chips, closing the loop on the full inference stack without ceding data to OpenAI or Google infrastructure.

From a semiconductor-competitive standpoint, Apple's silicon roadmap threatens Intel's Mac-segment remnants (effectively completed) and signals an ongoing threat to Qualcomm's modem monopoly as Apple internalizes 5G, Wi-Fi, and Bluetooth silicon. NVDA and AMD are not direct threats to Apple's edge inference position; Apple is a potential threat signal for them if on-device AI reduces cloud GPU inference demand at the consumer tier.

Key risks are regulatory (EU Digital Markets Act forcing sideloading and interoperability, potentially weakening Services revenue and the walled-garden moat), geopolitical (TSMC concentration in Taiwan for all leading-edge Apple Silicon; tariff exposure on China assembly), and execution (Apple Intelligence feature velocity has lagged Android AI rivals; Tim Cook succession uncertainty after COO Jeff Williams' retirement in 2025).

**Key moat:** Vertically integrated Silicon-to-OS-to-Services stack across 2.2 billion active devices; Neural Engine efficiency enables privacy-preserving on-device inference competitors cannot match at scale.
**Key risks:** TSMC/Taiwan geopolitical concentration; EU regulatory pressure on App Store and sideloading; AI feature rollout pace vs. Android; Cook succession overhang.
