# MU — Micron Technology
*Last updated: May 7, 2026*

---

## One-Line Thesis
The only US-based manufacturer of HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) — the memory stacked directly onto every NVIDIA Blackwell GPU — with a structural demand tailwind from AI that is unlike any prior memory cycle.

---

## Investment Thesis
Micron is a cyclical semiconductor company that has found a secular growth engine inside the AI capex supercycle. HBM3E is stacked directly onto NVDA's H200 and Blackwell GPUs — roughly 30% of the GPU's bill of materials — meaning every GPU shipped is a Micron revenue event. Historically memory was a brutal commodity business (DRAM, NAND), but HBM is different: it requires advanced packaging expertise, has a limited supplier base (Micron, SK Hynix, Samsung), and commands premium pricing with long-term supply agreements. Micron is gaining share vs. SK Hynix and is the only US-domiciled HBM supplier — giving it geopolitical tailwinds as the US pushes to onshore critical semiconductor supply. The thesis is that AI HBM demand is supply-constrained for years, and Micron's HBM ramp drives a structural re-rating of its earnings multiple.

**Key moat:** One of three HBM suppliers globally, US-domiciled (CHIPS Act funding), advanced packaging capability, DRAM/NAND vertical integration.

**Key risks:** Memory cycle downturn in commodity DRAM/NAND, SK Hynix HBM lead, Samsung price competition, CapEx intensity.

**Execution track record:** Mehrotra has executed one of the clearest strategic pivots in semiconductor history — repositioning Micron from a pure commodity memory supplier into a critical AI infrastructure vendor. FY2025 revenue grew ~50% YoY to a record $37.4B with gross margins expanding 17 percentage points to 41%. HBM revenue reached ~$2B in Q4 FY2025 alone (~$8B annualized run rate). HBM3E was sold out through 2024 and most of 2025 before product launched; HBM4 (>11 Gbps) is on track to ramp in Q2 CY2026, with full CY2026 supply already contracted. Murphy has managed capital discipline while committing to a 25%+ CapEx increase (>$25B) to fund the HBM ramp — a high-conviction bet on sustained AI infrastructure demand.

**Insider ownership / alignment:** DEF 14A filed November 25, 2025 (accession 0000723125-25-000038). CEO Sanjay Mehrotra's FY2025 total compensation was ~$30.7M, with ~$25.4M in equity awards — heavily stock-weighted, aligning incentives with shareholders. Institutional ownership dominates the float; named executive officer stock ownership details are in the proxy but specific share counts not extracted here. Compensation structure is majority performance-based equity, consistent with a management team incentivized on long-cycle execution.
