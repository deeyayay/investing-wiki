*Last updated: May 7, 2026*

## One-Line Thesis
Custom AI ASIC designer for hyperscalers (Google, Amazon, Microsoft) plus high-speed interconnect silicon — positioned to benefit whether hyperscalers buy NVDA GPUs or build their own custom chips.

---

## Investment Thesis
Marvell is one of the most interesting dual-angle plays in AI silicon. First, they design custom AI accelerators (ASICs) for hyperscalers who want to reduce NVDA dependency — Google's TPU, Amazon's Trainium, and potentially Microsoft's Maia all have Marvell silicon inside or adjacent. Second, Marvell makes the SerDes (high-speed interconnect) and PAM4 DSP chips that sit inside optical transceivers and switch fabrics — the plumbing that connects GPUs to each other and to storage. This means Marvell profits whether the AI compute layer is NVDA or custom silicon. CEO Matt Murphy has done a masterful job transforming Marvell from a commoditized storage chip company into an AI silicon platform. The thesis is that hyperscaler custom silicon spend grows from ~$10B to $100B+ over the next five years and Marvell captures a meaningful share.

**Key moat:** Deep co-design relationships with hyperscalers (multi-year design cycles = switching cost), SerDes IP leadership, optical DSP technology, TSMC advanced node access.

**Key risks:** Customer concentration (Amazon, Google are dominant), long design cycles mean lumpy revenue, AVGO competes in same custom ASIC space.

**Execution track record:** Murphy joined in July 2016 following activist pressure from Starboard Value and immediately pivoted Marvell away from consumer/storage toward data infrastructure. Key milestones: divested low-margin businesses, acquired Inphi (optical interconnect, ~$10B, 2021) and Innovium (Ethernet switching), reoriented R&D toward hyperscaler custom ASICs. Revenue grew from ~$2.3B in FY2017 to $8.2B in FY2026 (42% YoY in FY2026 alone). Data center now >70% of revenue. FY2025 operating cash flow hit record $1.68B with data center revenue +88% YoY. Recognized as Best CEO in semiconductors by Institutional Investor (2018). CFO Meintjes has been inside the transformation since 2016 — continuity is a positive.

**Insider ownership / alignment:** DEF 14A filed May 1, 2025 is on record with SEC. Director stock ownership guidelines set at 5× annual cash retainer; all directors met guidelines or are within compliance window. Executive compensation is heavily equity-weighted (RSU/PSU), aligning management with long-cycle hyperscaler design wins. Specific share counts for Murphy and Meintjes not retrieved from proxy detail — see DEF 14A at investor.marvell.com for current beneficial ownership table.
