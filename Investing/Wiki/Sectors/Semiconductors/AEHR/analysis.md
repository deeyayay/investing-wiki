# Aehr Test Systems — Investment Analysis

## One-Line Thesis

The only wafer-level SiC burn-in solution at commercial scale — monopoly position in EV semiconductor test with high switching costs; watching for expansion into AI chip and photonics test.

## Investment Thesis

| Date | Status | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 2026-05-07 | Initial | Flagged as SiC/EV test equipment play |
| 2026-06-13 | Scored | 6.5/10 Strong |

Aehr makes specialized test and burn-in systems for silicon carbide (SiC) wafers, a mandatory step in EV power module manufacturing. SiC is the material of choice for EV inverters (higher efficiency, higher temperature tolerance), and every major EV maker and Tier 1 supplier needs wafer-level burn-in testing before packaging. Aehr is the only company with a production-ready wafer-level burn-in solution (WaferPak technology), creating a monopoly-like position in a fast-growing niche.

The core thesis is a picks-and-shovels EV play — less binary than betting on a single EV maker, more levered to the overall SiC adoption curve. CEO Gayn Erickson built this position from scratch: he joined in 2012 when AEHR was subscale, repositioned around wafer-level burn-in, developed WaferPak, and secured the SiC design-ins that drove revenue from ~$20M to $80M before EV cycle softness created a reset.

The company is currently in a reset phase as EV production ramp timelines have extended. The thesis is intact but execution against the EV ramp timeline is the critical variable. Potential expansion into AI chip test (higher parallelism requirements) or photonics test is a long-term optionality.

**Key moat:** WaferPak proprietary technology; only production-grade wafer-level SiC burn-in; long qualification cycles (high switching costs); IP moat.

**Key risks:** EV production cycle sensitivity; customer concentration in small number of Tier 1 suppliers; limited diversification beyond SiC; founder director selling shares (Rhea Posedel sold ~17,700 shares April 2026).
