# FormFactor — Investment Analysis

## One-Line Thesis

Wafer probe market leader extending into silicon photonics — the V93000-Triton partnership with Advantest is the only production-grade CPO opto-electrical test cell, making FormFactor the hardware gate through which every CPO wafer must pass.

FormFactor's traditional wafer probe business — the largest installed base globally, 100+ silicon photonics customer deployments, recipe management software creating switching costs — provides a stable foundation. The re-rating driver is CPO.

In November 2024, FormFactor and Advantest announced joint development of a wafer-level SiPh/CPO test cell. In April 2025, the V93000-Triton system launched: Advantest's V93000 ATE + FormFactor's OptoVue Pro 9-axis optical alignment probe (lowest coupling loss of any production probe system). In December 2025, FormFactor acquired Keystone Photonics to consolidate optical probe IP. The result: FormFactor and Advantest have built the **only** announced production-grade opto-electrical CPO test cell — precisely timed for TSMC's COUPE CPO production ramp in 2026.

Every CPO wafer requires opto-electrical test before packaging. With no competing production solution, the V93000-Triton is the chokepoint. CEO Mike Slessor's open-market purchase of 5,908 shares in May 2026 (at elevated post-re-rating prices) is a strong alignment signal.

**Key moat:** Only production-grade CPO opto-electrical test cell (V93000-Triton); largest probe card installed base; recipe software switching costs; Keystone Photonics IP consolidation.

**Key risks:** Probe card business is semiconductor capex-correlated and cyclical; CPO test revenue is early-stage and timeline-dependent; valuation (3x market cap re-rating in 18 months) bakes in CPO adoption; Advantest partnership risk.
