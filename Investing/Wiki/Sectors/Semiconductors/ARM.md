## Investment Thesis
Arm does not fabricate chips — it licenses processor instruction set architectures (ISAs) and physical design blueprints to virtually every major semiconductor company. That asset-light model produced a ~97% gross margin and $1.14 billion in Q2 FY2026 revenue (+34% YoY). Because Arm collects a royalty on every chip shipped that carries its IP, revenue compounds with global silicon unit volume without proportional capex.

The architectural transition from v8 to v9 is the core near-term driver. Armv9 commands meaningfully higher royalty rates than v8, and by late 2025 v9 had already crossed 50% of royalty revenue. AI inference workloads — both at the edge (NPUs, vision processors, robotics) and in the data center (Neoverse server CPUs) — disproportionately favour Arm's power-efficient design ethos. Server CPU market share is forecast to rise from ~13% in 2025 to ~23% by 2028, and Arm launched its first proprietary "AGI CPU" data center chip in March 2026, signalling a move up the value chain toward compute solutions (CSS) alongside pure licensing.

Edge AI and physical AI represent the next wave. Boston Dynamics, NVIDIA Jetson, and autonomous vehicle platforms already run on Arm cores. The company reorganized around physical and edge AI at CES 2026, positioning its Cortex-A320/Ethos-U85 platform as the standard for billion-parameter-class on-device inference. Each new platform (cloud, edge, automotive, robotics) that standardizes on Arm extends the royalty surface area without requiring new chip design from Arm itself.

The key risk is concentration: SoftBank controls ~87–90% of shares outstanding, limiting float and creating headline overhang from any SoftBank liquidity needs. Arm's royalty rates can also face pushback from large licensees (Apple, Qualcomm) seeking architectural independence, and a prolonged smartphone unit decline would pressure legacy v8 royalties before v9 and data center fully offset them.

**Key moat:** Ubiquitous ISA standard — switching costs for the entire semiconductor ecosystem are prohibitive; decades of toolchain, OS, and developer ecosystem lock-in.
**Key risks:** SoftBank ~87-90% ownership overhang; large licensee rate negotiation risk (Apple, Qualcomm); smartphone volume cyclicality; China revenue concentration (~25% of revenue, geopolitical exposure).
