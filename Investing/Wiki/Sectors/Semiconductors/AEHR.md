# AEHR — Aehr Test Systems
*Last updated: May 7, 2026*

---

## One-Line Thesis
Niche semiconductor test equipment play riding the silicon carbide (SiC) wave in EV powertrains; dominant in wafer-level burn-in testing with no direct competitor at scale.

---

## Investment Thesis
Aehr makes specialized test and burn-in systems for silicon carbide wafers — a critical step in EV power module manufacturing. SiC is the material of choice for EV inverters (higher efficiency, higher temperature tolerance than traditional silicon), and every major EV maker and Tier 1 supplier needs to test their SiC wafers before packaging. Aehr is the only company with a production-ready wafer-level burn-in solution, giving it a monopoly-like position in a fast-growing niche. The thesis is an EV semiconductor picks-and-shovels play — less binary than betting on a single EV maker, more levered to the overall SiC adoption curve.

**Key moat:** Proprietary WaferPak technology, only wafer-level burn-in solution at scale, long customer qualification cycles (high switching cost), IP moat.

**Key risks:** EV production cycle sensitivity — if EV ramp slows, SiC wafer demand falls directly; customer concentration among a small number of Tier 1 suppliers; limited diversification beyond SiC.
