# Amkor Technology — Investment Analysis

## One-Line Thesis

World's #2 OSAT with 83% advanced packaging revenue, Kim family 49.4% founder control, and the only US-based CHIPS Act facility for AI chip advanced packaging at scale — the TSMC CoWoS overflow play.

Semiconductor packaging has become as technically complex and value-added as chip design itself. As GPUs and AI ASICs push the limits of monolithic die integration, advanced packaging — 2.5D interposers, FOWLP, chiplet assembly — is the engineering bridge that connects multiple chiplets and HBM stacks into a single functional system. Amkor is one of only three or four companies globally capable of executing this at commercial scale (alongside TSMC captive, ASE Group, and a few others).

The AI angle: every AI chip that requires advanced packaging and cannot use TSMC's constrained CoWoS capacity is a potential Amkor customer. Q1 2026 came in at a record $1.68B (+27% YoY) — a sharp acceleration from FY2025's modest +6% growth, suggesting the AI advanced packaging cycle is pulling forward aggressively.

Amkor's US positioning is strategic: the Peoria, Arizona CHIPS Act facility will be the first non-TSMC advanced packaging facility at commercial scale in the United States, directly positioned to capture onshoring demand from hyperscalers and defense customers. This is a multi-year capital investment with durable strategic value.

The Kim family's 49.4% ownership creates five decades of family-aligned capital allocation — they are building for the long term, not for the next quarter. Capital return policy (40-50% FCF payout + $300M buyback) rewards patient shareholders.

**Key moat:** Global manufacturing footprint (27 facilities); TSMC CoWoS overflow; Peoria AZ CHIPS Act US facility; Kim family 49.4% founder alignment; long-term co-development relationships with Apple, Qualcomm, NVDA supply chain.

**Key risks:** Semiconductor cyclicality; Apple ~20%+ customer concentration; ASE Group is larger and more diversified; new CEO transition risk (Kevin Engel, Jan 2026); high capex depreciation drag.
