## One-Line Thesis

Navitas pioneered the GaNFast integrated power IC that delivers 3x silicon's efficiency — a small-cap pure-play on the GaN transition in EV charging, data center PSUs, and industrial power, with execution risk and scale risk still to be proven.

## Investment Thesis

Wide-bandgap semiconductors (GaN and SiC) are displacing silicon in power conversion because physics demands it: AI data centers require ever-higher power density with ever-lower cooling overhead, and silicon MOSFETs are at their efficiency ceiling. GaN switches 3x faster than silicon, enabling smaller magnetics, fewer capacitors, and 97%+ conversion efficiency. Every kilowatt of data center power that migrates from silicon to GaN is a structural share gain.

**The GaNFast differentiation:** Conventional GaN discrete transistors require off-chip gate drive circuits, protection, and level shifters — adding cost, complexity, and board area. Navitas integrates all of this into a single GaNFast IC, dramatically reducing bill-of-materials cost. Deployed in Samsung, Dell, and Lenovo fast chargers, demonstrating mass-market manufacturing readiness.

**The SiC adjacency:** Via GeneSiC (acquired 2022), Navitas entered SiC modules for EV traction inverters — the $3B+ market segment where GaN's 650V/900V ceiling is insufficient. SiC handles 1,200V+ EV battery voltages. This gives Navitas both flanks of the wide-bandgap transition.

**Why the score is Strong, not Unrivaled:** Infineon, STMicroelectronics, ON Semiconductor, and Wolfspeed are all larger, better-capitalized, and rapidly closing the GaN gap. Navitas is pre-profitability at $82M revenue with -85% operating margin — survival depends on growing fast enough before incumbents commoditize. EV charging demand moderation in 2025 has created a near-term headwind.

**Risk:** Customer concentration (Samsung/Dell/Lenovo in consumer); EV production cycle uncertainty; insufficient scale to weather extended downturn; competing with Infineon which has billions in R&D and manufacturing scale.
