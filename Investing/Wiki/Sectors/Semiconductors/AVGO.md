## Investment Thesis

Broadcom has built two interlocking moats that position it as a structural beneficiary of AI infrastructure buildout. The semiconductor solutions segment supplies custom AI accelerators (XPUs) co-designed with the world's largest hyperscalers — Google TPUs, Meta MTIA, and Apple's silicon roadmap all rely on Broadcom's design capabilities and interconnect IP. This is not a commodity relationship: each custom program involves deep multi-year co-development, switching costs measured in years, and revenue visibility backed by long-term supply agreements. Management has cited an AI serviceable addressable market of $60–90B by 2027 across XPU and networking silicon, with Broadcom holding an estimated 70% share of custom AI ASIC design wins.

The networking silicon business (Tomahawk and Jericho product families) is equally defensible. Tomahawk 5 and Jericho3-AI control roughly 75% of high-end data center switching, the de facto standard for AI back-end fabric interconnecting tens of thousands of GPUs. Networking revenue grew 170% YoY in Q2 FY2025 and represented ~40% of total AI revenue in that period. The Q1 FY2026 AI revenue print of $8.4B grew 106% YoY, confirming the cycle is in mid-innings rather than peak.

The 2023 VMware acquisition added a third leg: a high-margin enterprise software business now being migrated toward a subscription/VCF bundled model. Early integration progress has been smoother than feared, with VMware contributing meaningfully to operating leverage. This software cash flow funds continued semiconductor R&D and M&A optionality, keeping the Hock Tan acquisition playbook active.

The strategic combination of recurring software revenue, long-cycle custom silicon programs, and commoditized-but-dominant networking chips produces exceptional free cash flow margins and predictable revenue even through semiconductor cyclicality.

**Key moat:** Custom ASIC co-design relationships (multi-year switching costs) + ~75% share in high-end data center switching silicon (Tomahawk/Jericho) + VMware enterprise software lock-in.

**Key risks:** Customer concentration (Google, Meta, Apple represent outsized revenue share — losing one would be material); China exposure (~32% of pre-VMware revenue, now reduced but still present with VMware facing Chinese regulatory headwinds); VMware integration execution risk if VCF migration stalls or customer attrition accelerates; gross margin pressure (management guided ~100bps dip in early 2026); Nvidia and AMD pursuing in-house networking silicon that could erode Tomahawk's switching dominance over a 3–5 year horizon.
