## Investment Thesis

Mobileye is the world's largest supplier of vision-based ADAS systems, embedded in vehicles from virtually every major global OEM. Its EyeQ system-on-chip is the de facto standard for front-facing camera ADAS across the industry — a position built over 25 years — and revenue reached $1.89B in FY2025, up 15% year-over-year. The business model is recurring and sticky: OEM design-wins lock in multi-year production contracts, and Mobileye generated $602M in operating cash flow in 2025 (up 51% YoY), demonstrating the underlying profitability of the core ADAS franchise even as it funds the autonomy roadmap.

The bull case rests on a stacked product ladder: base EyeQ ADAS (commodity-like volume at scale), SuperVision (hands-free highway and urban driving, ~50K units in 2025), and Chauffeur/Drive (full robotaxi stack). The robotaxi opportunity is approaching an inflection — Mobileye plans to remove safety drivers in its first U.S. city in 2026 and has an expanded robotaxi roadmap with Volkswagen. The February 2026 acquisition of Mentee Robotics (valued at ~$900M) extends the platform into humanoid robotics, placing Mobileye in the Physical AI category and opening an addressable market well beyond automotive. Surround ADAS is an additional emerging category with a major new U.S. OEM win disclosed in early 2026.

The primary risk is Intel's controlling ownership (~80% economic stake, ~96.9% of voting power via Class B shares). Intel is under financial pressure and has already sold portions of its Mobileye stake ($1.5B+ in secondary offerings since 2024); further stake sales create persistent overhang on MBLY's float and price. A full Intel divestiture — possible but unconfirmed — could be either a catalyst (new strategic owner) or disruptive. Second, the competitive landscape is intensifying: Tesla's in-house FSD, Waymo's robotaxi scale, and OEM in-house efforts (e.g., GM's ADAS consolidation) all erode Mobileye's pricing power at the top of its stack. Inventory digestion cycles (Q4 2025 was down 9% YoY) can cause revenue whipsaws despite healthy underlying design-win momentum.

**Key moat:** 25+ years of proprietary training data, EyeQ silicon optimized for mass-market cost/power targets, and OEM design-win lock-in that makes switching mid-generation prohibitively expensive for automakers.

**Key risks:** Intel overhang and potential stake sales; intensifying competition from Tesla FSD and Waymo at the autonomous stack level; OEM inventory digestion causing near-term revenue volatility; humanoid robotics bet is early-stage with no proven revenue.

**Execution track record:** Shashua guided Mobileye from a startup to the industry-standard ADAS platform with 230M+ cumulative EyeQ shipments, navigated the Intel acquisition and a successful Nasdaq re-IPO in 2022, and has since launched SuperVision and secured the Mentee Robotics deal — demonstrating product velocity alongside financial discipline ($602M operating cash flow in FY2025). Rojansky is an internal promote with deep institutional knowledge of Mobileye's cost structure and Intel relationship.

**Insider ownership / alignment:** Intel beneficially owned approximately 80% of Mobileye's economic interest and ~96.9% of voting power (via Class A + Class B shares) as of the 2026 DEF 14A. Intel has been reducing its stake through secondary offerings (including a $825M+ offering in mid-2025) and a $100M share repurchase by Mobileye itself, which has brought Intel below the 80.1% anti-dilution threshold. Management (Shashua and other executives) hold minority Class A positions; precise executive ownership percentages are disclosed in the 2026 DEF 14A (SEC filing available) but were not retrievable directly. Intel's majority control means MBLY minority shareholders have limited governance rights — a structural discount to apply to any valuation.
