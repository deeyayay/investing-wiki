## Investment Thesis

Rockwell Automation is the largest pure-play industrial automation company in the world, generating ~$8.3 billion in fiscal 2025 revenue across two segments: Intelligent Devices (hardware — PLCs, drives, sensors) and Software & Control (FactoryTalk MES/CMMS/SCADA, Plex, Fiix). The company sits at the intersection of two durable secular themes: U.S. manufacturing reshoring and the industrial AI transition. As factories automate to offset labor costs and tariff-driven supply chain restructuring pulls production back onshore, Rockwell is the default platform for greenfield and brownfield deployments across North America.

The bull case rests on software mix shift accelerating margin expansion. Software & Control grew 9% organically in FY2025 with segment margins of 29.7% — up 550 basis points year-over-year — and annual recurring revenue (ARR) now represents ~10% of total sales and grew 16% in FY2024, with 8% growth continuing into FY2025. As FactoryTalk SaaS subscriptions displace one-time license revenue, the revenue quality improves and incremental margins expand well above corporate average. Management's FY2026 guidance of 3–7% revenue growth and ~6–16% adjusted EPS growth reflects confidence in this trajectory. In parallel, Rockwell committed $2 billion over FY2025–2029 to expand U.S. manufacturing capacity, including a new 1 million+ sq ft greenfield facility in New Berlin, Wisconsin — directly positioning the company to capture reshoring capital expenditure.

The primary moat is the Allen-Bradley installed base combined with the proprietary Logix architecture. Rockwell holds >50% of the North American PLC market built over decades. More than 15 million installed controllers generate operational data that feeds FactoryTalk analytics, Plex MES, and Fiix CMMS, creating a closed-loop data flywheel. Replacing a Rockwell installation requires 12+ months of reengineering risk, retraining plant engineers, and rebuilding integrator relationships — switching costs that are structural, not just commercial. The company's integrator partner network is the densest in the industry and functions as a distribution moat competitors cannot easily replicate.

Key risks are real. First, Rockwell competes against much larger conglomerates — Siemens (Xcelerator), Schneider Electric (EcoStruxure), and Emerson — all of which are aggressively building industrial SaaS platforms and can outspend Rockwell in R&D indefinitely. Second, the Intelligent Devices segment (45% of revenue) remains cyclically exposed; FY2025 hardware sales declined 1% as customers worked down post-COVID inventory overbuilds. Third, valuation leaves little margin of error — at ~45× forward earnings, any guidance miss or macro softening (capex freezes, tariff escalation) risks a sharp de-rating. Fourth, the heavy North American concentration (~70% of revenue) is both a moat and a geographic risk.

**Key moat:** Entrenched Allen-Bradley/Logix installed base with >50% North American PLC market share, proprietary data flywheel across 15M+ controllers, and a dense integrator network — together creating multi-year switching costs and recurring software attach rates.

**Key risks:** Larger competitors outspending on R&D; hardware segment cyclicality; premium valuation with limited error tolerance; North American revenue concentration.

## Management & Leadership

| Role | Name | Since | Background |
|------|------|-------|------------|
| Chairman & CEO | Blake Moret | July 2016 | Rockwell lifer; joined 1985 as sales engineer; held commercial and operational leadership roles across multiple business units before CEO appointment |
| CFO | Christian Rothe | August 2024 | 13 years at Graco Inc., including CFO/Treasurer (2015–2018) and subsequent global business division leadership; brings manufacturing-sector finance and corporate development experience |

**Execution track record:** Under Moret's tenure Rockwell has pivoted from a hardware-centric PLC vendor to an integrated hardware-software platform company, completing acquisitions of Plex (MES, $2.2B, 2021) and Fiix (CMMS) while expanding Software & Control margins from the mid-teens to nearly 30%. Rothe is new to the CFO seat (Aug 2024) and has not yet been tested through a full cycle at Rockwell.

**Insider ownership / alignment:** Total insider and director ownership is approximately 0.17% of shares outstanding (DEF 14A, Feb 2026). CEO Blake Moret held ~89,749 shares as of late 2025 — a modest stake relative to his tenure, with recent open-market sales signaling limited personal accumulation. Institutional ownership dominates the register; alignment is primarily compensation-driven (performance-linked equity) rather than owner-operator.
