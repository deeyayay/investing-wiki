## Investment Thesis

Analog Devices designs and sells high-performance analog, mixed-signal, and digital signal processing (DSP) semiconductors that translate real-world signals — temperature, pressure, motion, sound, current — into digital data that systems can act on. This signal-chain function sits at the physical interface of every industrial robot, EV battery management system, medical instrument, and communications base station. As the physical world accelerates its integration with AI and automation, demand for ADI's precision sensing and data-conversion products grows structurally: more actuators, more sensors, more edge compute, more ADI content per system.

The bull case rests on two converging cycles. First, industrial inventory destocking that suppressed ADI revenue through 2023–2024 has reversed; fiscal 2025 revenue rose 17% year-over-year to $11.0 billion, with industrial (46% of revenue) up 34% and automotive (28%) up 19%. Second, the Maxim Integrated acquisition — fully integrated by 2024 with over $1 billion in synergies realized — expanded ADI's power management and automotive product depth, particularly for EV battery management and ADAS sensor fusion. These are long-duration design-win opportunities: once an ADI chip is qualified into a platform, it typically ships for 10–20 years with minimal re-qualification pressure.

ADI's gross margin (~63%) and operating margin (~33%) reflect the stickiness of its business model. The company runs a hybrid fab model — proprietary precision nodes in-house for differentiated processes, TSMC for high-volume digital content — which protects its process IP while keeping capital intensity manageable. Capital returns have been consistent: ADI has increased its dividend for 21 consecutive years and supplements with buybacks.

The primary risks are macro-driven. Industrial capital expenditure is cyclical, and the 2023–2024 inventory correction demonstrated how sharply ADI's revenue can contract when customers pause orders. Automotive is growing but exposed to EV adoption pace uncertainty. China represents a meaningful revenue concentration that carries geopolitical risk. Competitive pressure from Texas Instruments in broad-market analog remains persistent, though ADI's differentiation in high-precision, high-complexity applications limits direct overlap.

**Key moat:** 20,000+ product catalog, decades of proprietary analog process IP, and entrenched design wins with 10–20 year revenue tails that competitors cannot economically displace.

**Key risks:** Industrial capex cyclicality (demonstrated 2023–2024 downturn), China revenue concentration and export-control exposure, EV adoption pace risk in automotive, and ongoing TI competition in commoditized analog segments.

## Management & Leadership

|------|------|--------|------------|
| CEO | Vincent Roche | CEO since 2013; at ADI since 1988 | 35+ year ADI lifer; led acquisitions of Linear Technology (2017), Hittite (2014), and Maxim Integrated (2021); grew revenue from $2.7B (FY2012) to $12B+ under his tenure; elected Board Chair 2022 |
| CFO | Richard Puccio | CFO since January 2024 | Joined from AWS (CFO); prior 29 years at PricewaterhouseCoopers, partner for 20+ years serving semiconductor and technology clients; AB Economics Harvard, MBA Boston University |

**Execution track record:** Under Roche, ADI executed three transformative acquisitions while expanding gross margins above 60% and sustaining 21 consecutive years of dividend growth — a track record that reflects disciplined capital allocation alongside inorganic growth. Puccio brings large-scale cloud-company financial rigor from AWS to a business now operating at $11B+ revenue scale.

**Insider ownership / alignment:** Directors and officers collectively own approximately 1.4% of shares outstanding (per FY2026 DEF 14A filed January 2026); co-founder Ray Stata holds the largest individual insider stake at ~0.9%. Institutional ownership is ~86%, dominated by Vanguard (10.0%), BlackRock (7.9%), and State Street (4.6%). Insider ownership is low in percentage terms but board members hold ~$310M in shares at current prices, providing dollar-denominated alignment.
