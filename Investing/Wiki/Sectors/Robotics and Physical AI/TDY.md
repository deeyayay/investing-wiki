## Investment Thesis

Teledyne Technologies is a disciplined acquirer and operator of highly specialized sensors, digital imaging systems, instrumentation, and engineered systems for defense, space, and industrial customers. The 2021 acquisition of FLIR Systems for ~$8 billion transformed Teledyne into the world's largest commercial infrared imaging company, layering FLIR's thermal camera franchise onto Teledyne's existing strength in scientific-grade visible and infrared detectors. Together the combined entity supplies sensors into unmanned systems, surveillance platforms, space-based tracking layers, subsea robotics, and laboratory instrumentation — markets where performance requirements are extreme and switching costs are high.

The company's competitive moat rests on three pillars. First, proprietary detector fabrication: Teledyne's focal plane arrays and infrared detectors are used by NASA, space primes, and DoD platforms where there are few or no qualified substitutes. Second, government clearances and program incumbency: once designed into a defense or space program, Teledyne components are notoriously difficult to displace given qualification cycles, supply chain certification requirements, and export-control constraints. Third, scale in a fragmented market: Teledyne can afford the R&D investment to stay at the performance frontier across dozens of niche sensing modalities that a smaller player cannot justify. FY2025 saw record sales and non-GAAP operating margins, with digital imaging (led by Teledyne FLIR) posting particular strength in unmanned and surveillance systems, including the company's first production-rate loitering munition contract and selection as infrared detector supplier for the Space Development Agency's Tranche 3 Tracking Layer program.

Teledyne runs a portfolio model — over 40 operating companies — that trades headline growth for resilience and margin consistency. Shorter-cycle commercial businesses (industrial machine vision, test and measurement) provide a recovery lever when defense budgets pause, while long-cycle defense and space programs provide revenue visibility. Management has demonstrated consistent margin expansion through organic efficiency and post-acquisition integration, compounding non-GAAP EPS at a mid-teens rate over the prior decade.

**Key moat:** Proprietary infrared and scientific detector IP, deep government program incumbency, export-controlled technology, and FLIR brand scale in thermal imaging — collectively creating high barriers to displacement once designed into a program.

**Key risks:** Integration complexity across 40+ operating companies; FLIR acquisition premium must be earned through sustained defense demand; civilian/commercial imaging revenue is cyclical and susceptible to macro slowdowns; rising competition in lower-end thermal from Asian manufacturers could pressure FLIR's commercial segment margins; executive transition risk following Robert Mehrabian's move to Executive Chairman and George Bobb's relatively short tenure as CEO.

**Execution track record:** Robert Mehrabian compounded revenue and non-GAAP EPS at a mid-teens rate over two decades through serial bolt-on acquisitions and disciplined integration. The $8B FLIR acquisition in 2021 was the largest deal in company history; by 2025, Teledyne FLIR was delivering record results in defense surveillance. FY2025 represented record quarterly orders, sales, and non-GAAP EPS in company history, with operating cash flow of $1.07B. George Bobb's tenure as CEO is new as of April 2025; execution track record under his sole leadership is limited but early indicators are positive.

**Insider ownership / alignment:** Insiders collectively hold approximately 5% of shares outstanding. Robert Mehrabian is the largest individual insider shareholder with ~442,000 shares (~0.95% of shares outstanding), a position accumulated over his multi-decade tenure. George Bobb holds approximately 10,400 shares as of mid-2025. Institutional ownership dominates at ~95%, consistent with a large-cap defense/technology compounder.
