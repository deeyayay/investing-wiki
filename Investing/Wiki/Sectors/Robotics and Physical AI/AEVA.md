## Investment Thesis

Aeva Technologies develops 4D LiDAR sensors based on Frequency-Modulated Continuous Wave (FMCW) technology — a fundamentally different sensing architecture than the time-of-flight LiDAR used by most competitors. FMCW simultaneously captures depth and instantaneous velocity per pixel without the need for multi-frame processing, enabling safer and faster object detection in automotive, industrial, and defense applications. The technology matured at exactly the right time: regulatory pressure for hands-free highway systems and accelerating factory automation are creating production-volume demand for sensing that legacy approaches cannot adequately address.

The bull case rests on two structural anchors. First, Aeva holds an exclusive long-term LiDAR supply agreement with a major European passenger OEM for a Level 3 global automation program with planned start-of-production in 2028 — a deal that locks in revenue visibility well beyond the current development period. Second, its partnership with SICK AG, a €3B+ industrial sensing leader, gives Aeva distribution leverage into factory and warehouse robotics that would take years to build organically. Combined, these partnerships validate the technology and de-risk the path to volume production. Revenue doubled to $18.1M in FY2025, with management guiding to $30–36M in FY2026 — 70–100% YoY growth — as industrial sensor shipments scale.

The primary moat is the FMCW sensor architecture itself. Building production-grade FMCW LiDAR requires deep photonics integration and years of system-level tuning; Aeva co-founders spent years on sensing systems at Apple before launching the company in 2016. Time-of-flight competitors cannot retrofit velocity measurement into their existing designs without restarting the silicon stack. This gives Aeva an estimated 5-year architecture lead in any application where velocity-per-pixel matters — particularly Level 3+ autonomy and high-speed industrial robotics.

Key risks are real: the company burned $119.7M gross cash in FY2025 against $18.1M in revenue. Non-GAAP operating losses remain near $102M annually. With $246.9M in liquidity (cash plus an undrawn $125M facility) at year-end 2025, the runway is approximately two years at current burn — enough to reach the 2028 automotive production ramp, but only if costs compress meaningfully as shipments scale. Any slip in OEM program timing or industrial rollout would extend the dilution risk substantially.

**Key moat:** FMCW LiDAR architecture that measures instantaneous velocity per pixel — technically non-replicable by time-of-flight competitors without full silicon redesign, locked in via exclusive OEM supply agreements.

**Key risks:** Pre-profitability with ~$120M/yr gross cash burn; runway dependent on OEM program timing; 2028 automotive SOP leaves a multi-year execution window where delays could force dilutive capital raises.
