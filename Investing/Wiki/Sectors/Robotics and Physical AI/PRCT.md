## Investment Thesis

Procept BioRobotics makes the AquaBeam Robotic System, which delivers Aquablation therapy — an image-guided, heat-free waterjet ablation for benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH, enlarged prostate). BPH is one of the most prevalent urological conditions globally, affecting roughly 50% of men over 60, and surgical intervention has historically been dominated by TURP (transurethral resection), a decades-old technique with meaningful complication rates. AquaBeam is the first robotic system to combine real-time ultrasound imaging with autonomous waterjet tissue removal, enabling anatomy-independent resection that eliminates heat-related nerve damage and preserves sexual function at rates superior to TURP in randomized trials.

The bull case rests on a large underpenetrated surgical market, a compounding razor-and-blade revenue model, and accelerating guideline adoption. The U.S. install base reached 718 systems at end of 2025 (+42% YoY) and 765 by Q1 2026 (+40% YoY), each system generating recurring high-margin handpiece revenue. Full-year 2025 revenue came in at approximately $325.5 million (+45% YoY), and 2026 guidance is $390–$410 million at ~65% gross margin — evidence that the business is scaling toward profitability (targeted by end of 2027). International expansion is an incremental growth layer; European Association of Urology guidelines now strongly recommend Aquablation, validating the clinical story outside the U.S.

The primary moat is the clinical data package and guideline endorsement that competitors cannot easily replicate. The WATER, WATER II, and WATER III randomized controlled trials constitute a multi-year body of evidence. Combined with the integrated ultrasound-imaging-plus-robotics hardware stack — which no direct competitor currently replicates — and increasing ASPs (34% ASP growth noted in recent coverage), switching costs rise with each successive handpiece purchase and hospital protocol integration.

**Key moat:** First-and-only robotic Aquablation platform with RCT-backed guideline endorsement (EAU) and a growing locked-in install base generating recurring consumable revenue at ~65% gross margin.

**Key risks:** Execution risk following CEO transition (Larry Wood appointed September 2025, replacing founder Dr. Reza Zadno); 2026 guidance reset was below prior expectations and triggered a BofA downgrade to Underperform; procedure volume growth dependent on hospital adoption pace and reimbursement stability; tariff headwinds ($5–6M guided impact in 2026); path to profitability remains multi-year; concentrated single-product, single-indication exposure.
