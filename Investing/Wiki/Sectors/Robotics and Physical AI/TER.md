## Investment Thesis

Teradyne holds a structural leadership position in semiconductor ATE through its UltraFLEXplus platform, which has become the de facto standard for testing high-performance compute and AI chips. The semiconductor test segment generates gross margins consistently above 55% and benefits from deep customer switching costs: rewriting test software and requalifying a competing vendor is expensive enough that most fabs stay on-platform for years. With AI-driven chip complexity accelerating — larger dies, heterogeneous packaging, higher pin counts — test time per chip grows, expanding the total addressable market without requiring Teradyne to win new customers.

Universal Robots (acquired 2015 for $285M) is the founding brand of the collaborative robotics category, and its PolyScope software layer runs on more than 75,000 deployed cobots worldwide. That installed base creates a durable ecosystem moat: third-party developers build UR+ certified accessories and software exclusively for PolyScope, and customers train workforces on UR arms, compounding switching friction over time. The MiR autonomous mobile robot line extends the robotics platform into factory logistics. Revenue from this segment is hardware-led but increasingly includes software subscriptions and multi-year service contracts, improving margin quality.

AI infrastructure build-out is a secular tailwind for both divisions simultaneously. Hyperscaler demand for GPU and custom ASIC test capacity drives semiconductor ATE orders; the same factories adding AI compute capacity are also adding automation, benefiting Universal Robots and MiR. The 2025 acquisition of TestInsight — a semiconductor test development and pattern conversion software specialist — deepens Teradyne's design-to-test software stack, shortening customer time-to-market on complex AI chips and strengthening platform stickiness. FY2025 revenue reached $3.19B (+13% YoY), with Q1 2026 EPS of $2.56 beating consensus of $2.11 by a significant margin.

Key risks include cyclicality in semiconductor capex, customer concentration in a handful of large foundries and fabless leaders (TSMC, Samsung, Broadcom, NVIDIA), and robotics segment execution — Universal Robots growth has been lumpy and faces intensifying competition from low-cost Chinese cobot vendors. Tariff and trade policy shifts affecting TSMC's Taiwan operations or Teradyne's own supply chain are an emerging macro risk.

**Key moat:** UltraFLEXplus platform lock-in via test software switching costs; Universal Robots' 75,000-unit PolyScope ecosystem with a certified third-party developer network.

**Key risks:** Semiconductor capex cyclicality; customer concentration in ATE; Chinese cobot competition pressuring UR pricing and growth; trade/tariff disruption to the TSMC/Taiwan supply chain.

**Execution track record:** Smith is a 19-year Teradyne insider who built the semiconductor test and industrial automation businesses from within, giving him deep operational credibility with both divisions. Under his leadership the company delivered FY2025 revenue of $3.19B (+13% YoY) and a Q1 2026 beat across EPS and revenue. The TestInsight acquisition and the deepened Flex manufacturing partnership reflect a disciplined capital deployment posture: bolt-on software to tighten platform stickiness rather than dilutive large-scale M&A.

**Insider ownership / alignment:** Executive and director ownership is modest relative to institutional float, which is typical for a large-cap industrials/semis company. The 2026 proxy notes all non-employee directors are on track to meet ownership guidelines (newer directors — Maddock, van Kralingen, Henry, Sayiner — are within their accumulation window). Teradyne's insider trading policy prohibits hedging (short sales, collars, equity swaps) and pledging of shares, aligning executives with long-term price performance. All executive officers and directors trade under Rule 10b5-1 plans. Institutional ownership is dominated by index and long-only managers that hold through cycles, providing a stable shareholder base.
