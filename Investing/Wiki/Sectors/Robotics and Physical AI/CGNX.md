## Investment Thesis

Cognex holds a structural position at the sensing layer of industrial automation: its cameras, vision systems, and software allow machines to inspect, identify, and guide objects at production-line speeds. With over 4.5 million image-based products shipped since 1981 and more than $11 billion in cumulative revenue, Cognex has built the reference brand in machine vision — a market where accuracy, reliability, and application expertise compound into high switching costs.

The company's near-term recovery story is playing out through logistics, where e-commerce operators are upgrading existing facilities with vision-based conveyor and sortation systems. Logistics revenue grew double-digits for seven consecutive quarters through Q1 2026. Simultaneously, the industrial segment is recovering from a deep capex drought (2023–2024) correlated with semiconductor and EV battery investment cycles. When factory capex returns at scale, Cognex is positioned as a first-mover beneficiary.

The longer-term lever is the OneVision Platform (launched 2025), which allows non-expert operators to upload images of defective parts to the cloud, where AI models are trained automatically and pushed back to factory-floor cameras. This shifts Cognex from a hardware-centric box-seller toward a recurring software and services model, expanding addressable market to customers previously unable to deploy machine vision without specialists. Q1 2026 revenue grew 24% YoY with Adjusted EBITDA margins of 26.9%, up 1,010 bps YoY — the seventh consecutive quarter of margin improvement.

**Key moat:** Proprietary vision algorithms (PatMax, Deep Learning), decades of application-specific training data, and installed base of 4.5M+ devices create compounding software and switching-cost advantages that generalist camera vendors cannot replicate.

**Key risks:** Revenue is highly cyclical and tied to semiconductor, EV, and logistics capex cycles; leadership transition (new CEO June 2025) introduces execution risk during a critical AI product build-out; competition from low-cost Asian machine vision vendors (Keyence, Hikrobot) is intensifying; hardware commoditization could pressure gross margins if software transition stalls.
