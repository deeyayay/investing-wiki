## Investment Thesis

Intuitive Surgical (ISRG) designs, manufactures, and sells robotic-assisted surgical systems — primarily the da Vinci platform and the Ion endoluminal system for lung biopsy. The company sells capital equipment (systems), then earns recurring revenue from single-use instruments, accessories, and service contracts tied to each procedure performed. Recurring revenue represented 86% of Q1 2026 total revenue, making the business model more annuity-like than hardware-driven.

The bull case centers on the consumables flywheel: every system placed becomes a captive annuity. With 11,395 systems installed as of Q1 2026 (up 12% year-over-year) and each da Vinci procedure generating $800–$3,600 in consumable revenue, top-line growth is structurally linked to procedure volume — which itself grows as surgeons trained on da Vinci now expect it at any hospital they work. Medical residents increasingly train on da Vinci from day one, cementing the next generation of demand before they enter practice. Da Vinci 5, featuring force feedback and advanced digital integration, is driving higher per-system utilization and accelerating upgrade cycles. Worldwide da Vinci procedures grew 16% in Q1 2026; management guides 13.5–15.5% for full-year 2026.

The key moat is multi-layered: a 30-year IP portfolio, FDA clearance depth across dozens of procedure types, surgeon training lock-in (surgeons with 13+ cases in 90 days show >90% retention), hospital capital investment lock-in, and a brand so dominant that hospitals decline to hire urologists without da Vinci credentials. Competitors exist — J&J's Ottava, Medtronic's Hugo, and Chinese domestic players — but none match ISRG's installed base, procedure breadth, or clinical evidence library. Hugo is currently cleared only for urologic procedures in the U.S.

Key risks include China, where domestic robotic surgery players compete aggressively on price and benefit from government procurement preferences for local suppliers; reimbursement uncertainty in China through at least 2027; tariff headwinds on margins; Japanese public hospital capital constraints; and macro-driven hospital CapEx deferrals. Valuation is a persistent risk — ISRG consistently trades at a significant premium to the market, embedding high expectations for continued procedure growth.

**Key moat:** Surgeon training lock-in + hospital capital investment + 11,000+ installed base generating captive consumables revenue + 30-year IP portfolio across procedure types.
**Key risks:** China competitive pressure and reimbursement uncertainty; hardware pricing pressure in Asia; CapEx-sensitive hospital spending; premium valuation leaves little margin for growth deceleration.
