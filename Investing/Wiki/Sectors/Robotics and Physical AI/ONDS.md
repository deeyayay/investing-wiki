## Investment Thesis

Ondas Holdings (now branded Ondas Inc.) operates two segments: Ondas Autonomous Systems (OAS) and Ondas Networks. OAS is the growth engine — a portfolio of AI-powered drone and counter-drone platforms purpose-built for defense, border security, and critical infrastructure. The Networks segment provides private wireless connectivity (900 MHz) for the U.S. rail sector. The company matters now because the U.S. government's Blue UAS and Green UAS requirements are actively shutting out Chinese-origin drone hardware (DJI and peers), creating a secular demand shift toward NDAA-compliant domestic suppliers at exactly the moment Ondas has achieved the regulatory credentialing to fill that gap.

The bull case rests on scale from a tiny revenue base into a large pipeline. FY2025 total revenue was ~$50.7M (up ~600% year-over-year, largely driven by acquired assets). Management has guided $170–180M in revenue for FY2026 — a figure that requires near-term ramp of the Optimus System, Iron Drone Raider, and border-protection contracts. OAS's flagship Optimus 1-EX became the first small UAS to receive FAA Type Certification for automated aerial security, unlocking DoD procurement at scale and BVLOS operations over people and moving vehicles. Sentrycs (acquired), integrated into OAS, provides Cyber-over-RF counter-drone technology deployed across 25+ countries with 200+ installations — giving a detect-to-defeat stack that few competitors replicate. A major government border-protection tender, expected to culminate in thousands of drone deployments, represents a multi-year revenue anchor if executed.

The primary moat is regulatory: FAA Type Certification for the Optimus system and expansive BVLOS waivers are multi-year barriers that cannot be easily replicated. NDAA-compliant domestic manufacturing is a second enforced moat, particularly as DoD procurement rules tighten. The Sentrycs Cyber-over-RF layer adds proprietary technology depth that complements the hardware portfolio. Gross margins reached ~49% in Q1 2026, consistent with a software/systems-of-systems business model rather than a pure hardware play.

Key risks are substantial for a micro-cap at this stage. Revenue is backlog-dependent and highly lumpy; execution on $170M+ guidance from a $50M base requires near-flawless operational delivery. Insider ownership is low (~0.79% total), so management's financial alignment with shareholders is thin. Cash is not an immediate concern — the company raised ~$960M in net proceeds in January 2026 and held ~$840M in pro forma cash — but dilution risk from future raises is real. OAS EBITDA profitability is not expected until Q1 2027. Any contract delay, certification setback, or procurement freeze in defense budgets could impair the thesis materially.

**Key moat:** FAA Type Certification + BVLOS waivers on Optimus System; NDAA-compliant domestic UAS manufacturing; Sentrycs Cyber-over-RF counter-drone IP deployed in 25+ countries.

**Key risks:** Lumpy backlog-driven revenue; execution risk on aggressive 2026 guidance (~3.5× scale-up); low insider ownership; pre-EBITDA profitability until 2027; defense budget sensitivity.
