## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-26
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-26
> **Drift status:** Early watch — Akoya integration and gross margin recovery are the key execution tests in 2026

Quanterix built its business on Simoa (Single Molecule Array) — a proprietary detection technology that finds proteins in blood at femtomolar concentrations, roughly 1000x below what standard immunoassays can see. This sensitivity threshold enables biomarker discovery and clinical testing that is simply not possible on competing platforms, creating a defensible position wherever detecting low-abundance proteins matters. More than 3,600 peer-reviewed publications now cite Simoa data, cementing it as the reference standard in neurodegeneration and oncology research.

The bull case is a two-lever transition story. Lever one is the 2024 acquisition of Akoya Biosciences, which adds spatial biology (tissue-level protein mapping) to Simoa's blood-based platform. The combined ~2,300-instrument install base creates substantial cross-sell opportunity, and management projects ~$40M in annual cost synergies by end of 2026. Lever two is the move into in-vitro diagnostics: Quanterix's five-biomarker Alzheimer's multiplex test, targeting 90%+ sensitivity/specificity, is in FDA clearance process. NY State clinical lab permit (granted August 2025) removes a geographic barrier to commercial testing. The 2026 revenue guide of $169–174M (+22% at midpoint vs. 2025) reflects the first full year with Akoya included.

The moat rests on three reinforcing elements: (1) patent protection for Simoa chemistry, (2) reagent lock-in on an installed base that grows as citations accumulate and new instruments place, and (3) the network effect of 3,600+ publications creating a self-reinforcing literature standard that drives new instrument purchases.

Key risks: The Akoya integration is messy — GAAP gross margin fell from 60.5% to 46.8% in 2025, and the path to 49–53% adjusted gross margin in 2026 requires cost-out execution in a complex integration. Quanterix remains deep in net loss ($107M in 2025); while management guides for cash flow breakeven in H2 2026, any revenue miss resets that timeline. Competition from Luminex, Olink (now part of Thermo Fisher), and bespoke microfluidic platforms is real, even if no competitor matches Simoa sensitivity at scale. And the IVD/diagnostics pivot introduces regulatory risk and a different commercial motion than the company's traditional research-tools playbook.

**Key moat:** Simoa's sensitivity floor creates sole-source status for low-abundance blood biomarker work; instrument install base locks in high-margin consumables.

**Key risks:** Gross margin recovery post-Akoya; cash burn pace vs. H2 2026 breakeven guide; diagnostics regulatory execution; competition from Olink/Thermo Fisher.
