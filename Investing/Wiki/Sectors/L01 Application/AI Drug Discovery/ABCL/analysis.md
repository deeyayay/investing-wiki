## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-26
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-26
> **Drift status:** On track — ABCL635 Phase 1 interim positive; transitional revenue period manageable with $700M liquidity

AbCellera built its business around a differentiated antibody discovery platform that combines proprietary microfluidics hardware, single-cell functional screening, paired heavy/light chain sequencing, and AI/ML models trained on 14 years of proprietary data. The platform's defining advantage is native B-cell diversity: it screens millions of real immune cells rather than synthetic libraries, recovering rare high-affinity antibodies that competitors' methods miss. The commercial proof was Bamlanivimab (LY-CoV555), the COVID-19 antibody discovered for Eli Lilly in 11 months — a speed record enabled by the platform — which generated hundreds of millions in royalties at the pandemic peak.

The bull case is a royalty-to-pipeline compounding story. Lever one is the 40+ partnered programs now advancing toward clinical stages; as partners reach approval milestones, royalty streams diversify and compound without additional discovery capex. Lever two is the internal clinical pipeline: ABCL635 is a first-in-class NK3R antibody for non-hormonal treatment of vasomotor symptoms (menopausal hot flashes) — a $50M+ US sufferer population with no good non-hormonal option. Q1 2026 interim Phase 1 data showed strong NK3R target engagement, favorable safety, and once-monthly subcutaneous dosing feasibility; Phase 2 efficacy readout is the key binary catalyst in Q3 2026. ABCL575 (anti-OX40L, half-life extended) in atopic dermatitis is a second owned asset competing in the Dupixent-validated $13B+ market. A clinical manufacturing facility in development could add a CDMO revenue stream independent of partner milestones.

The moat rests on three reinforcing elements: (1) 14 years of proprietary single-cell data create an AI training advantage that is non-replicable on a short timeline; (2) the patent estate, validated by a $44.9M settlement payment in Q4 2025, creates defensive IP barriers; (3) the integrated stack (hardware + software + biology + AI) means a competitor must replicate the full system, not just one component. FY2025 revenue of $75.1M overstates the baseline — strip out the settlement and organic discovery + milestone revenue was ~$30M — but the company is debt-free with ~$700M liquidity, providing 3+ years of runway through the critical pipeline readouts.

Key risks are binary and timing-driven. ABCL635's Q3 2026 Phase 2 efficacy data is the primary value inflection: a clean readout could reset the stock toward analyst price targets in the $9–10 range; failure eliminates the primary re-rating catalyst and forces reliance on partner royalties and ABCL575 alone. Revenue visibility remains poor — milestone payments are lumpy and non-recurring, and post-COVID royalties are in a trough while the 40+ partner programs mature toward approvals. Competition from Adimab (private), Absci (ABSI), and in-house big-pharma discovery teams is real, though no competitor yet matches the full-stack single-cell native B-cell approach.

**Key moat:** 14-year integrated AI+microfluidics platform creates a non-replicable single-cell discovery advantage; IP estate validated by 2025 patent settlement.

**Key risks:** Binary Phase 2 efficacy readout ABCL635 (Q3 2026); revenue lumpiness (patent settlement was 60% of FY2025); post-COVID royalty trough; capital consumption vs. runway.
