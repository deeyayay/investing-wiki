# Arista Networks — Investment Analysis

## One-Line Thesis

The dominant Ethernet switching platform for hyperscaler AI clusters; EOS software lock-in means every Meta/Microsoft/Google AI build is a recurring Arista order.

Arista sells high-speed Ethernet switches and its EOS (Extensible Operating System) software to the world's largest data centers. The AI angle: NVIDIA GPU clusters originally ran on InfiniBand, but hyperscalers at scale are shifting to Ethernet because it's cheaper, more flexible, and their engineering teams already know it. Arista dominates high-speed Ethernet switching (400G/800G) with deep relationships at all five major hyperscalers.

As AI cluster sizes balloon to hundreds of thousands of GPUs, the networking layer becomes as critical as the compute layer. Arista's software moat (EOS) means customers don't just buy switches — they build their entire network automation stack around Arista's APIs. Switching costs are extremely high. Jayshree Ullal has run this company with exceptional operational discipline: 29% revenue growth at 48% non-GAAP operating margin at $7B+ scale is genuinely exceptional.

The AI-Ethernet thesis creates a multi-year secular tailwind: larger clusters → more switching infrastructure → faster product cycles (400G → 800G → 1.6T) → more ASP per cluster.

**Key moat:** EOS network OS ecosystem lock-in + hyperscaler relationship depth + 400G/800G switching technology leadership.

**Key risks:** Cisco competitive response; InfiniBand resurgence (NVDA Quantum-X); customer concentration (Meta ~15% of revenue); hyperscaler custom silicon programs.
