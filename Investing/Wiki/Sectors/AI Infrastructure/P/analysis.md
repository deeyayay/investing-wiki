## One-Line Thesis

Everpure is the all-flash storage platform that AI training data gravity makes mandatory — Evergreen subscription lock-in, DirectFlash IP, and Portworx Kubernetes storage combine to create a durable enterprise storage franchise as AI data volumes compound.

## Investment Thesis

AI creates an irreversible shift in enterprise storage requirements: training datasets are 10-1,000x larger than traditional enterprise workloads, require ultra-low latency random I/O, and need to persist across model iterations. Pure Storage / Everpure's DirectFlash architecture — which bypasses commodity SSD controllers and communicates directly with NAND at the software layer — delivers 4-6x the density and performance of competing NVMe SSD arrays at similar or lower total cost of ownership.

**The Evergreen moat:** The subscription model eliminates the #1 objection to all-flash (hardware becomes obsolete). Customers pay a subscription, get hardware refreshes included, and never face a disruptive fork-lift upgrade. This creates 90%+ renewal rates and growing average contract values as AI workloads expand on existing installations. Once a customer's AI pipeline is designed around FlashArray or FlashBlade, switching means re-architecting the entire data path.

**Portworx for AI:** Kubernetes-native persistent storage (acquired 2020, $370M) positions the company directly in the AI MLOps stack. Every AI training job on Kubernetes needs persistent volume claims. Portworx is the enterprise-grade answer: data locality, snapshot, replication, disaster recovery — all Kubernetes-native. Growing 40%+ YoY as AI pipeline adoption accelerates.

**Why AI is a structural tailwind:** Hyperscalers build their own storage; Everpure is the enterprise/neocloud play. NVIDIA DGX BasePod reference architectures certify Everpure storage. Every enterprise AI deployment that isn't on a hyperscaler is a potential Everpure customer.

**Risk:** Hyperscaler competition builds all their own storage (no direct revenue). NetApp and Dell EMC are serious competitors in enterprise storage. Subscription model creates deferred revenue recognition complexity. Rebranding from Pure Storage to Everpure creates short-term brand awareness cost.
