## Investment Thesis
Microsoft has repositioned from enterprise software vendor to the essential infrastructure layer of the AI economy. Azure is growing at ~39% year-over-year, with roughly 14 percentage points of that growth attributed directly to AI services. Microsoft's AI revenue crossed a $37 billion annual run rate in early 2026, up 123% YoY, while Microsoft 365 Copilot alone reached an $8 billion annual run rate with 15 million paid users across over 90% of Fortune 500 companies.

The Maia AI chip program is the key differentiator from a pure hyperscaler play. The Maia 100 (deployed at scale in Azure) and the Maia 200 (ramping in early 2026 for inference workloads including OpenAI's GPT models) reduce Microsoft's dependence on NVIDIA for inference compute and compress cost-per-token over time. Paired with the Cobalt 100 ARM CPU, Microsoft is building a heterogeneous silicon stack that allows margin recovery as the infrastructure matures — the same path AWS took with Graviton.

The investment in OpenAI and the Anthropic partnership provide dual model coverage: Azure OpenAI Service ties enterprise customers to Azure for frontier model access, while the broader multi-model strategy hedges against any single model provider losing ground. This creates a network effect where enterprise data, fine-tuned models, and workflows increasingly lock customers into the Azure ecosystem.

**Key moat:** Enterprise distribution (Office 365 installed base), proprietary silicon reducing inference COGS, and exclusive/preferred cloud hosting for OpenAI and other frontier labs.
**Key risks:** Cloud gross margins contracted to ~68% as capex scales to ~$80B+ annually; AI revenue must convert from trial to durable high-margin workloads before the market reprices growth; antitrust scrutiny of OpenAI relationship; competition from AWS and Google Cloud on price and model quality.
