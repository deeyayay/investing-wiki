## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-05-07
> **Last validated:** 2026-05-14
> **Drift status:** On track — NVIDIA $3.4B cloud deal + $2.1B warrant + Microsoft $9.7B contract de-risk buildout; execution on GPU ramp is the remaining variable

Iris Energy is a vertically integrated AI data center operator that controls power, land, and cooling directly — eliminating the landlord markup and cloud intermediary layers that compress margins for most HPC infrastructure providers. The company operates three segments: AI Cloud Services (managed GPU clusters), AI Data Centers (colocation and build-to-suit), and Bitcoin Mining (retained as a dynamic capacity buffer that can be reallocated to GPU workloads in real time based on ROI). That mining-to-GPU arbitrage capability is structurally unique — no pure-play AI infra competitor has it.

The bull case rests on two anchor contracts that de-risk the entire buildout. NVIDIA: a 5-year managed GPU cloud services agreement (~$3.4B), a $2.1B warrant investment (5-year right to purchase 30M shares at $70/share), and designation of IREN's Sweetwater Campus (Texas, 2GW) as the flagship NVIDIA DSX architecture deployment. Microsoft: a $9.7B, 5-year contract at 85% EBITDA margin. These are not LOIs or early-stage pilots — they are multi-year contracted revenue commitments from the two dominant AI infrastructure spenders. The AI Cloud segment is already proving the model: $33.6M in Q3 FY2026, +94% QoQ. Management targets 460 MW utilization, 140,000 GPUs, and $3.4B ARR by end-2026.

The structural edge is the 3GW permitted power pipeline — grid-connected land in renewable-rich regions across North America, Europe, and APAC. Securing permitted power at this scale takes years and regulatory relationships that cannot be replicated quickly. Combined with NVIDIA preferred partner status and direct GPU allocation agreements, IREN can densify facilities faster than any greenfield competitor. Co-founders hold ~13.2% combined with no recent selling — strong alignment at a pivotal execution moment.

**Key moat:** 3GW permitted power pipeline (unmatched among non-hyperscaler HPC operators), vertical integration (no landlord/cloud markup), dynamic mining-to-GPU capacity reallocation, NVIDIA DSX preferred partner status, $2.6B cash to fund buildout.

**Key risks:** Execution risk on 20x ARR expansion in 12 months, share dilution (+124% shares outstanding YoY to 272M), long-term debt $963M, high net losses driven by impairments and capex ($247.8M net loss in Q3 incl. $140.4M impairments), hyperscaler capex cycle sensitivity.
