## Investment Thesis
Everpure (formerly Pure Storage, rebranded February 2026; ticker changed PSTG → P in April 2026) is a Santa Clara-based enterprise data infrastructure company repositioning as an AI-era data management platform. The core product suite includes FlashArray/FlashBlade (all-flash storage), Evergreen//One & Flex (storage-as-a-service ARR driver), Portworx (market-leading Kubernetes data management), Everpure Cloud (hybrid cloud block storage), and Everpure Fusion (SaaS management plane). The pending 1touch acquisition adds data intelligence and governance tools — embedding data discovery and provenance tracking directly into the storage layer.

**The hyperscaler flash design win** is the near-term catalyst lever. A win that meaningfully exceeded internal expectations in FY2026 ramps significantly in H2 FY2027. Hyperscalers deploying all-flash for AI training/inference need Everpure's power density and performance. Management noted the revenue is "not linear" — high-ceiling and lumpy, potentially underweighted by consensus.

**The ARR flywheel** is the long-term compounder. Subscription ARR at $1.9B (+16% YoY), RPO at $3.7B (+40% YoY). Evergreen//One converts storage from capex to an auto-renewing operating expense. Net Promoter Score has been best-in-class for a decade; churn is negligible and expansion is the primary growth vector across 14,500+ enterprise customers.

**The AI platform repositioning** is the optionality layer. The rebrand and 1touch acquisition are a deliberate move toward governing and serving enterprise AI data. Portworx is already the market leader in Kubernetes data management — credible cloud-native footprint alongside the traditional enterprise base.

**Financial quality:** FY2026 FCF $616M (~17% FCF margin) on $3.7B revenue. $343M+ in buybacks. Gross margins near 70%. NAND procurement shifted to customers — structural change reducing margin volatility.

**Key moat:** Installed base of 14,500+ enterprises, storage-as-a-service lock-in (Evergreen subscriptions), Portworx Kubernetes leadership, 70% gross margins, best-in-class NPS.

**Key risks:** Hyperscaler concentration (lumpy, non-linear revenue; build-out timing risk), macro/enterprise IT spend sensitivity, 1touch integration execution risk, insider selling activity (early May 2026), valuation premium (~25x forward earnings vs. ~22x sector).
