# Super Micro Computer — Investment Analysis

## One-Line Thesis

NVIDIA's primary GPU rack ODM with 14-15% founder ownership and liquid-cooling first-mover advantage — discounted to "Strong" not "Unrivaled" due to unresolved governance overhang.

| Date | Status | Notes |

Super Micro builds and ships the actual GPU servers that go into AI data centers: DGX-compatible rack systems, NVL72 GPU rack solutions, and high-density compute nodes. They are NVIDIA's closest hardware partner with first-mover advantage in liquid-cooled AI server design. At peak AI buildout, SMCI captures enormous revenue leverage — every large GPU order tends to include SMCI rack infrastructure.

The bull case is structurally sound: AI server TAM grows from ~$30B to $300B+, and SMCI maintains 10-15% share via NVIDIA partnership and design speed. The company ships faster than Dell or HPE on new GPU architectures — a genuine competitive advantage in a market where hyperscalers want infrastructure the week NVIDIA announces it.

However, SMCI has well-documented governance issues: 10-K filing delays (2024), Hindenburg short report, multiple auditor resignations, and an ongoing DOJ investigation. A new auditor has been engaged and filings are current, but the governance scar tissue suppresses the multiple and forces a "Strong" rather than "Unrivaled" conviction.

Key differentiation from pure ODM: Charles Liang's 14-15% founder ownership and NVIDIA preferred status creates real moat. The company is not commodity — it's the fastest-to-market AI server builder. But until governance cloud clears, position sizing should reflect the uncertainty.

**Key moat:** NVIDIA preferred ODM status + fastest time-to-market on GPU architectures + liquid cooling IP + founder Charles Liang 14-15% alignment.

**Key risks:** DOJ investigation resolution uncertain; CFO succession limbo; governance scar tissue cap on multiple; thin ODM margins (~12% GM) limit downside protection; Dell/HPE competition.
