## Investment Thesis
Google Cloud is Alphabet's fastest-growing segment and its clearest AI infrastructure narrative. Revenue has scaled rapidly, powered by enterprise adoption of Gemini models and a $240 billion signed-but-undelivered contract backlog that provides multi-year revenue visibility. Alphabet's 2026 capex commitment of $75–$90 billion (full-year guidance) underscores how seriously management is competing against AWS and Azure for AI workloads.

The TPU program is the structural differentiator. Now in its seventh generation (Ironwood), Alphabet's custom silicon delivers 42.5 exaflops per 9,216-chip superpod — purpose-built for inference rather than training — and has already secured external customers including Anthropic and Meta. TPU 8 is on the roadmap for later 2026. A decade of internal deployment gives Google a unit-economics advantage: Gemini serving costs fell 78% in 2025, a compounding cost moat that external GPU-dependent cloud rivals cannot easily replicate.

DeepMind and Google Research anchor the model layer. Gemini 2.x spans consumer (Search AI Overviews, Assistant), developer (Vertex AI), and enterprise (Workspace) surfaces, giving Alphabet distribution leverage that pure-play infrastructure providers lack. The co-location of a world-class research lab, proprietary chips, and a hyperscale cloud under one roof is rare and hard to reproduce.

Key risks are real. The DOJ antitrust case targeting Google Search's default agreements represents an existential regulatory overhang — a structural remedy could impair the advertising cash engine that funds AI capex. Additionally, Alphabet's $75–$90B capex cycle is demand-backed today but carries execution risk if Cloud revenue growth decelerates. Competition from AWS Trainium and Microsoft Azure's NVIDIA partnership is intensifying.

**Key moat:** Vertically integrated AI stack — proprietary TPU silicon, DeepMind research, Google Cloud distribution, and Search/YouTube cash generation funding the flywheel.

**Key risks:** DOJ antitrust structural remedy risk to Search; capex cycle execution risk if Cloud growth slows; TPU adoption still small relative to NVIDIA's installed base.
