## Investment Thesis
Amazon Web Services is the dominant public cloud platform with ~30% global market share and is accelerating into AI infrastructure as the primary growth driver. AWS grew 28% year-over-year in Q1 2026 — the fastest pace in 15 quarters — and its AI revenue run rate exceeded $15 billion in that quarter alone, with the broader chips portfolio (Graviton, Trainium, Inferentia, Nitro) surpassing a $20 billion annual run rate. AWS also reported a $244 billion committed backlog in early 2026, up 40% year-over-year, reflecting durable enterprise demand.

The strategic bet on custom silicon is central to the AI Infrastructure thesis. Trainium2 instances run at roughly half the cost of comparable NVIDIA H100 instances, giving AWS a structural pricing advantage for training and inference workloads at scale. Project Rainier — nearly 500,000 Trainium2 chips across a dedicated 1,200-acre Indiana facility — is already powering Anthropic's Claude model training. OpenAI has committed to 2 gigawatts of Trainium capacity through AWS, and Anthropic has secured up to 5 gigawatts of current and future Trainium generations. These anchor partnerships validate custom silicon as commercially viable at hyperscale. Amazon has committed ~$200 billion in 2026 capex, the majority directed at AI chips and data centers, signaling long-term infrastructure lock-in.

Key moats are multi-layered: (1) AWS's breadth of services creates deep enterprise switching costs; (2) proprietary silicon at scale lowers per-unit AI compute cost, compressing margins for NVIDIA-dependent rivals; (3) the Anthropic and OpenAI partnerships serve as both revenue and R&D anchors; (4) Amazon's retail and logistics data flywheel gives AWS unique training datasets unavailable to pure-play cloud competitors.

**Key moat:** AWS enterprise switching costs + proprietary Trainium/Inferentia silicon enabling 50% cost reduction vs. NVIDIA H100 equivalents, backed by anchor AI partnerships with Anthropic and OpenAI.
**Key risks:** NVIDIA CUDA software ecosystem lock-in limits Trainium adoption outside captive workloads; free cash flow compressed 71% TTM (to ~$11 billion) as capex surges to $125B+ annually; AWS market share erosion from Azure/Google Cloud in AI-native workloads; regulatory scrutiny of Anthropic investment.
