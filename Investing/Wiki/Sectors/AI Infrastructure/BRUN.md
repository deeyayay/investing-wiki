## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** June 4, 2026
> **Last validated:** June 4, 2026
> **Drift status:** On track — initial thesis established; monitor Q2 2026 results for ARR ramp and 10-Q filing resolution

Boost Run is a pure-play neocloud operator providing GPU compute, managed Kubernetes, and shared storage infrastructure for enterprise AI and HPC workloads. The company went public via SPAC merger with Willow Lane Acquisition Corp. on May 11, 2026, entering Nasdaq with $940M in contracted customer revenue at average three-year terms. Unlike hyperscalers that treat GPU allocation as a commodity add-on, BRUN built its platform on a compliance-first foundation — SOC 2 Type II, HIPAA, ISO 27001, and ISO 27701 certifications at the operator level — targeting enterprise and government customers for whom regulatory compliance is a procurement gating requirement, not an afterthought.

The bull case is execution on contracted backlog before competition catches up. BRUN projects $375M+ in annualized recurring revenue by year-end 2026, rising from $10.96M in Q1 2026 (+165% YoY) — a ramp driven primarily by the $471.7M multi-year contract with Thinking Machines Lab Inc. (5,000 NVIDIA B300 GPUs, 36-month term). Projected adjusted EBITDA margins of 75–80% are among the highest in the neocloud sector, suggesting the revenue model converts cleanly to cash once contracted deployments ramp. The company operates 6 US data center locations with 5 additional in development, expanding accessible capacity to over 125 MW.

The primary moat is the TierPoint partnership: access to 45 US data center locations gives BRUN geographic flexibility that single-site operators cannot match, enabling rapid deployment to meet customer proximity and latency requirements without the capital intensity of building owned facilities. Compliance certifications create meaningful procurement barriers for healthcare, finance, and government customers who cannot use un-certified GPU cloud providers regardless of price. NVIDIA and Lenovo partnerships provide priority B300 GPU allocation in a still-constrained supply environment.

The critical risks are revenue concentration and governance. A single $471.7M contract with Thinking Machines Lab represents a large share of the $940M backlog — customer default, renegotiation, or workload migration to a hyperscaler would materially impair revenue visibility. BRUN announced on May 26, 2026 that it cannot file its 10-Q by the SEC deadline, a governance signal that warrants scrutiny in a newly public company. The $134.5M SPAC proceeds must fund GPU acquisitions at a pace that matches contracted deployment timelines — execution gap between contracted and deployed capacity is the key tracking metric. Competition from CoreWeave (CRWV), Nebius (NBIS), and hyperscalers is intensifying in every neocloud sub-segment.

**Key moat:** TierPoint 45-location network enabling rapid US geographic deployment; HIPAA/SOC 2/ISO certifications gating regulated-industry procurement; NVIDIA + Lenovo hardware partnerships for B300 GPU priority allocation.

**Key risks:** Revenue concentration in Thinking Machines Lab ($471.7M / $940M backlog); 10-Q filing delay signals governance immaturity; SPAC balance sheet requires disciplined capex pacing; hyperscaler and CoreWeave competition compressing neocloud pricing.
