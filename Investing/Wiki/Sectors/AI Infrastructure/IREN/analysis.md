# Iris Energy — Investment Analysis

## One-Line Thesis

Co-founder-led AI data center operator with NVIDIA ($3.4B) and Microsoft ($9.7B) anchor contracts, 3GW permitted power pipeline, and a dynamic Bitcoin→GPU reallocation moat no independent competitor can replicate.

Iris Energy is vertically integrated — it controls power, land, and cooling directly, eliminating the landlord markup and cloud intermediary layers that compress margins for most HPC infrastructure providers. The Bitcoin mining segment is not a distraction: it's a dynamic capacity buffer that can be reallocated to GPU workloads in real time based on ROI. This mining-to-GPU arbitrage capability is structurally unique — no pure-play AI infrastructure competitor has it.

The bull case rests on two anchor contracts that de-risk the entire buildout. NVIDIA: $3.4B, 5-year managed GPU cloud services agreement + $2.1B warrant investment + designation of Sweetwater Campus (Texas, 2GW) as the flagship NVIDIA DSX architecture deployment. Microsoft: $9.7B, 5-year contract at 85% EBITDA margin. These are multi-year contracted revenue commitments from the two dominant AI infrastructure spenders.

Q3 FY2026 AI Cloud segment grew +94% QoQ to $33.6M — proof the GPU monetization model is working, not just contracted. Management targets 460 MW utilization, 140K GPUs, and $3.4B ARR by end-2026. The structural edge is the 3GW permitted power pipeline — securing permitted grid-connected land in renewable-rich regions takes years and regulatory relationships that cannot be replicated quickly.

The bear case is financial health: net loss $(247.8)M in Q3 (including $140.4M impairments), share dilution +124% YoY to 272M shares, $963M long-term debt + $3B convertible notes. This is a high-conviction pre-profitability story that requires a long time horizon.

**Key moat:** 3GW permitted power pipeline + NVIDIA DSX partner status + vertical integration (no landlord markup) + dynamic mining→GPU capacity reallocation + $13.2% co-founder ownership.

**Key risks:** Execution risk on 20x ARR expansion in 12 months; share dilution; $963M debt; hyperscaler capex cycle sensitivity.
