## One-Line Thesis

ASP Isotopes sits at the intersection of three exponential trends — nuclear renaissance (HALEU for SMRs), quantum computing (silicon-28 substrates), and medical isotopes — using a proprietary aerodynamic separation process that could make it the only non-governmental HALEU supplier in the West.

## Investment Thesis

The nuclear renaissance has a fuel problem: advanced reactor designs (Oklo Aurora, X-energy Xe-100, TerraPower Natrium) require High-Assay Low-Enriched Uranium (HALEU, 5-20% enrichment) rather than the low-enriched uranium (LEU, <5%) used in conventional reactors. Russia's TENEX was the only commercial HALEU supplier. The 2024 US ban on Russian nuclear fuel imports created an acute supply chain crisis — no advanced reactor can operate without HALEU, and the only current alternative is costly DOE enrichment programs with limited capacity.

**The ASP process:** The Aerodynamic Separation Process uses aerodynamic and potentially laser-based principles to separate isotopes without the massive infrastructure of conventional centrifuge cascades. If successful at scale, ASP could produce HALEU at 40-60% lower cost than centrifuge enrichment, making it the preferred pathway for commercial HALEU supply. The pilot plant in South Africa is commissioning to demonstrate technical viability.

**The three-leg stool:** ASPI is hedged across three isotope markets: (1) HALEU — the strategic prize, potentially worth billions in long-term supply contracts; (2) silicon-28 — isotopically pure silicon is required for high-performance quantum computing qubits (Intel, IBM, academic labs need this now); (3) carbon-13 and other medical isotopes — diagnostic tracers that provide current revenue.

**The thesis in one sentence:** If the ASP process works at scale, ASPI becomes the critical link between nuclear fuel supply and the AI nuclear power renaissance — a structural monopoly position in an indispensable strategic input.

**Risk:** The process has not been demonstrated at commercial scale. South African pilot plant is still commissioning. NRC licensing for a US facility would take years. The company is burning cash at $8M revenue. Any competitor who cracks the enrichment cost problem first (centrifuge expansion, SBK laser) eliminates the moat.
