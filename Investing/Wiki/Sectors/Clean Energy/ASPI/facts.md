## Company Overview

ASP Isotopes is developing the Aerodynamic Separation Process — an enrichment technology that uses aerodynamic and potentially laser techniques to separate isotopes far more efficiently than conventional gaseous diffusion or centrifuge methods. The primary strategic application is HALEU (High-Assay Low-Enriched Uranium, 5-20% enriched) — the fuel required by advanced reactor designs including Oklo's Aurora, X-energy's Xe-100, and TerraPower's Natrium. Russia's TENEX was the dominant HALEU supplier until the 2024 US-Russia nuclear fuel import ban. ASPI's pilot plant in South Africa is commissioning; a US domestic facility would require NRC licensing. Near-term revenue comes from silicon-28 (quantum computing substrates) and carbon-13 (medical diagnostics).
