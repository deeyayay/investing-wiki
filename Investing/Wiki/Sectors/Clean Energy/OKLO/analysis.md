## One-Line Thesis

Oklo is the nuclear renaissance pure-play that sits at the intersection of AI's insatiable power demand and bipartisan nuclear policy support — pre-revenue with exceptional management and 20+ GW of power purchase LOIs, but entirely dependent on NRC licensing execution.

## Investment Thesis

AI data centers are the most power-intensive infrastructure build in human history. A single GB200 NVL72 rack draws 120kW. A hyperscale campus of 100,000 GPUs requires ~500MW of always-on power — the equivalent of a small city. Grid-connected power from wind/solar has intermittency problems. Natural gas has emissions and price volatility. Nuclear microreactors solve both: always-on baseload at ~$0.05/kWh with zero carbon.

**The Aurora proposition:** Oklo's Aurora micro-fission reactor (1.5-50 MWe) is factory-built, delivered by truck, and fueled by HALEU or recycled spent nuclear fuel. The metal fuel design allows the reactor to burn waste from conventional nuclear plants — addressing the spent fuel problem while creating a domestic fuel supply chain not dependent on Russian/Chinese enrichment. A single Aurora unit could power a 5,000-GPU data center cluster continuously for 20 years.

**Why Sam Altman matters:** OpenAI's CEO sitting on Oklo's board is the most credible signal in the AI infrastructure space that nuclear microreactors will be part of the AI power solution. Altman's ~12% ownership means this isn't a casual endorsement — it's a strategic bet. The AI industry is the primary demand driver for Oklo's LOI pipeline.

**20+ GW of LOIs:** Letters of intent from data center operators, defense installations, and industrial facilities. LOIs are non-binding but signal serious customer intent. Converting even 5% of this LOI pipeline to executed PPAs would represent $5B+ of long-term contracted revenue.

**Why not higher conviction:** No deployed reactor. The NRC license application was rejected in 2022 for insufficient safety documentation — the revised application must pass. The regulatory path for a novel reactor design has historically taken 5-10 years and cost hundreds of millions. DeWitte and Cochran have deep technical credentials but zero prior experience bringing a nuclear reactor to commercial operation. The $4B market cap represents enormous optionality premium on an unproven technology.
