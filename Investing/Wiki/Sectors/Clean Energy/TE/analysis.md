## One-Line Thesis

T1 Energy is a Texas utility-scale solar developer benefiting from IRA tax credits and AI data center power demand, but the business lacks a technology moat — it competes on project finance and land relationships in a highly competitive EPC market.

## Investment Thesis

The Texas ERCOT grid is experiencing the fastest power demand growth in the US — driven by data center expansion, semiconductor fab construction, and LNG export terminal buildout. Utility-scale solar with battery storage is the fastest pathway to adding generation capacity in Texas (18-month build vs. 5+ years for gas peakers). T1 Energy is positioned as a developer/EPC with a Texas-centric pipeline and IRA Investment Tax Credit monetization expertise.

**IRA as a business accelerator:** The Inflation Reduction Act's 30-40% ITC (Investment Tax Credit) dramatically improves project economics for solar + storage. T1 Energy structures projects to maximize ITC capture — both for own-account projects and as a pass-through to tax equity investors. This is a learnable skill that creates some stickiness with repeat financial partners.

**Why the score is Average:** Solar EPC and development is a commodity business. T1 Energy's competitors include NextEra, Intersect Power, Ørsted, and dozens of regional developers. The competitive differentiation is Texas land relationships and ERCOT market expertise — real, but not a durable moat. Revenue is lumpy (project completions), margins are under compression (solar panel prices fell 60% but EPC labor/interconnection costs rose), and the business requires ongoing project finance access to grow.

**The honest risk:** A solar developer without a technology moat is a capital allocator, not a compounder. If the company can consistently identify, develop, and sell projects at good risk-adjusted returns, it is a good business. But it is not an Unrivaled franchise.
