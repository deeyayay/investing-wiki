## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-19
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-19
> **Drift status:** On track — hybrid bonding order inflection confirmed in Q1 2026

BESI (BE Semiconductor Industries N.V.) makes the equipment that bonds semiconductor dies together inside a chip package. Unlike AMAT, KLAC, or LRCX — which serve the wafer fab with deposition, etch, and metrology — BESI operates entirely in back-end packaging assembly. It makes die attach bonders, flip chip systems, and hybrid bonding equipment: the machines that physically join chiplets, stack HBM memory onto interposers, and integrate logic and memory into 2.5D/3D packages. This focus makes it the only publicly-traded pure-play on advanced packaging *equipment* at scale.

The structural bull case is hybrid bonding. Conventional packaging connects dies via micro-bumps (solder balls 5–20μm pitch), which limit interconnect density and require copper pillars that add resistance and inductance. Hybrid bonding eliminates bumps entirely: it bonds copper pads directly to copper pads at pitches below 1μm through chemical-mechanical planarization and annealing. The result is an order-of-magnitude improvement in bandwidth density and a material reduction in power per bit — directly addressing the bandwidth wall that constrains AI accelerator performance. TSMC's SoIC-X, Samsung's X-Cube, SK Hynix's HBM5/6 roadmap, and Intel's Foveros all depend on hybrid bonding. BESI is the #1 equipment supplier for this process.

Q1 2026 validated the inflection: revenue of €184.9M (+28.3% YoY), orders of €269.7M (+104.5% YoY at a record) with hybrid bonding unit orders more than doubling sequentially. The customer base for hybrid bonders expanded to 20 — up from a narrow initial cohort — signaling that adoption is broadening from early movers (TSMC/memory) into a multi-customer wave. Q2 2026 guidance of +30–40% QoQ revenue and 64–66% gross margins signals the ramp is structural, not a pull-forward.

The founder-CEO Richard Blickman has run BESI since 1995 (~30 years) with 1.94% personal ownership and 88.5% performance-linked compensation. Gross margins consistently above 60% (and guided to rise to 64–66% as hybrid bonding mix increases) reflect both pricing power and operating leverage on a high-IP product. The risk is primarily valuation (forward P/E ~80x as of June 2026) and cycle timing: BESI went through a painful FY2025 down cycle (revenue −2.7%, net income −27.7%) before the hybrid bonding ramp began. In a sustained slowdown, multiple compression could be severe.

**Key moat:** ~75% market share in advanced die attach; #1 position in hybrid bonding equipment with 20 customers and a technology lead that took 10+ years to build; long-term co-development relationships with TSMC, Samsung, SK Hynix, and Intel mean any switch carries years of qualification risk.

**Key risks:** Valuation at ~80x forward P/E offers little margin of safety; semiconductor equipment cycles are violent (FY2025 demonstrated -27% earnings on a modest -3% revenue drop); ASMPT (ASM Pacific Technology) has its own hybrid bonding roadmap and is well-funded; customer concentration on a small number of leading-edge packaging fabs; foreign-listed (Euronext Amsterdam) — FX (EUR/USD) headwind for USD-denominated investors.
