## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-05-07
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-17
> **Drift status:** On track, evolving — AI pivot expanding TAM; SiC/EV softness is the near-term drag; $37.2M Q3 bookings (3.5x BTB) validates the AI thesis ahead of revenue

Aehr Test Systems builds specialized test and burn-in systems for compound semiconductors and advanced chips. Its core product — the FOX-NP wafer-level burn-in (WLBI) system with proprietary WaferPak contactors — is the only production-ready WLBI solution at scale. WaferPak tests an entire wafer's devices simultaneously before dicing, dramatically reducing cost vs. package-level burn-in. This is critical for SiC EV power modules (high infant mortality requires burn-in), and increasingly for AI processors, HBM memory stacks, and heterogeneous chiplet packages (CoWoS) that require burn-in at wafer scale.

**AI pivot (2025–2026):** The original EV/SiC thesis softened as EV production ramp slowed globally. AEHR leveraged the same WaferPak platform for a new market: AI processor burn-in. In November 2025, AEHR partnered with ISE Labs (backed by ASE Group, the world's largest OSAT) to co-market and deploy WLBI for HPC/AI processors, heterogeneous integration, CoWoS, and optical interconnect. AEHR delivered the world's first production WLBI systems for AI processors to a premier global OSAT — proof-of-concept now generating production orders. Q3 FY2026 bookings hit $37.2M (3.5x book-to-bill on $10.3M revenue), the strongest booking quarter in company history, driven by AI demand. This is a picks-and-shovels play on AI chip manufacturing quality — a test/burn-in tax on every advanced AI chip regardless of which GPU or ASIC wins.

**Key moat:** WaferPak is sole-source — no direct competitor has a production-ready WLBI system at scale. Qualification cycles for semiconductor test equipment are 12–18 months minimum, creating structural switching costs. The IP moat is reinforced by 35+ years of know-how under CEO Gayn Erickson (repositioned the company from irrelevant to category leader during his tenure).

**Key risks:** Customer concentration — historically a small number of SiC customers drove majority of revenue; AI pivot is still early and concentrated in ISE Labs. Revenue recognition lags bookings by 1–3 quarters (equipment books on shipment), so the current backlog signals FY2027 recovery but near-term revenue remains muted. SiC/EV demand recovery timeline depends on EV production ramp at Ford, GM, BMW, BYD — uncertain in 2026.
