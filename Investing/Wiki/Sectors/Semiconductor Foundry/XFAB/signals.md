- **2026-02-06** — CEO Rudi De Winter stepped down; COO Damien Macq appointed CEO. **Why it matters:** De Winter was the architect of the $1B capex expansion and the now-abandoned $300-350M SiC 2026 target. Macq's mandate appears to be operational efficiency and cost control rather than growth, signaling the company is in harvest/optimization mode for the capex already deployed.

- **2026-02-17** — New CEO Damien Macq purchased 5,500 shares at ~€4.39 ($24K). **Why it matters:** Small buy but directional signal — new CEO putting personal capital in within 11 days of taking the role is a minor alignment flag.

- **2025 Q3/Q4** — SiC revenue -60% YoY, book-to-bill 0.38; management abandons $300-350M FY2026 SiC target. **Why it matters:** The SiC ramp was the primary growth narrative for 2024-2026. Its collapse is the reason the stock has de-rated. The analog/automotive/medical core was always the base; SiC was the optionality premium. That premium has evaporated.

- **2025 FY** — $1B capex build-out (2023-2025) largely complete; 2026 capex guided ~$100M. **Why it matters:** Foundry capex is lumpy and front-loaded. The heavy spending phase is over. FCF should improve materially in 2026-2027 assuming any utilization improvement. This is the inflection point thesis.

- **2026-04-29** — Q1 2026 results: revenue $195.6M (beat $190-200M guide), EBITDA margin 17.5%; microsystems ATH $33.7M (+42% YoY, +35% QoQ); wide-bandgap $15.1M (+152% YoY, +49% QoQ); industrial +32% YoY; medical +39% YoY; automotive soft; Q2 guided $190-200M / 17-20% EBITDA; automotive recovery expected H2 2026.
  **Why it matters:** Revenue beat above guidance despite automotive headwind — the non-automotive revenue base (microsystems, industrial, medical, wide-bandgap) is growing rapidly and diversifying XFAB away from the Melexis (largest automotive client) inventory overhang. Wide-bandgap +152% YoY signals the SiC and GaN demand recovery is underway in non-EV applications (industrial power, energy storage). The automotive correction appears to be a timing issue, not structural loss of share.

- **2026-06-19** — photonixFAB deep-dive (via @serenity_invests thread, fact-checked): X-FAB leads the €48M EU KDT-funded "photonixFAB" consortium for European sovereign silicon photonics manufacturing. Key consortium partners: NVIDIA (evaluating for CPO transceivers/switches; hosted 5th meeting June 2025), Nokia (system specifications/assembly), imec, SMART Photonics (InP chiplets via MTP). Architecture uses micro-transfer printing (MTP) of InP chiplets and thin-film lithium niobate (TFLN) onto X-FAB's 200mm silicon photonics platform. Risk production readiness target: 2027. Mass production: 2028.
  **Why it matters:** This is a dimension entirely absent from the initial thesis write-up. XFAB is not just an analog/SiC foundry — it is the leading EU silicon photonics foundry for CPO applications with NVIDIA as a design-in partner. If NVIDIA (or Nokia) convert the evaluation to volume commitments, this re-rates XFAB from a depressed automotive/SiC play to a silicon photonics infrastructure asset. EU and US government backing (€48M photonixFAB + €128M MEMS Erfurt + $50M CHIPS Act Lubbock SiC) provides meaningful capex de-risking.

- **2026-06-19** — US CHIPS Act $50M PMT confirmed: December 6, 2024 — US DOC/NIST signed preliminary memorandum of terms with XFAB for up to $50M to expand Lubbock TX SiC foundry. NIST confirmed XFAB Lubbock is "the only high-volume SiC foundry in the U.S." — verbatim "critical infrastructure" designation.
  **Why it matters:** Government recognition of XFAB as critical infrastructure is strategically significant. The $50M PMT partially de-risks the SiC capex recovery thesis — the US government has explicitly underwritten the strategic importance of XFAB's SiC capacity.

- **2026-06-19** — Foundry customer confirmations: (a) POWI (Power Integrations): SEC EDGAR file POWI-EX10.2 is a wafer supply agreement with X-FAB; PCN added XFAB Lubbock as qualified fab for power-switch die. (b) NVTS (Navitas): uses XFAB Texas for SiC manufacturing. Note: NVTS also announced GaN partnership with GlobalFoundries (Nov 2025) and PSMC (Jul 2025) — XFAB is NVTS's SiC foundry specifically.
  **Why it matters:** POWI is an established, multi-year customer relationship (not speculative). NVTS SiC demand flows directly to XFAB Lubbock — the wide-bandgap +152% YoY in Q1 is partly driven by these customer ramps.
