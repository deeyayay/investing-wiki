## One-Line Thesis

Robinhood is the retail fintech platform with a real recurring revenue stream (Gold, >3M subscribers) and Bitstamp crypto optionality, but PFOF regulatory risk and crypto cyclicality keep it a tactical trade more than a compounder.

## Investment Thesis

Robinhood disrupted retail brokerage by making commissions zero and UX great — 24M funded accounts is a real platform. The evolution from pure PFOF broker to multi-product financial platform is the right strategic direction: Gold subscription ($10/month) is recurring, margin-expanding revenue; Bitstamp acquisition adds a regulated global crypto exchange; international expansion (UK, EU) opens new TAM.

**What works:** Vlad Tenev (co-founder, ~8% ownership, super-voting shares) has strong founder alignment and has navigated the company from meme-stock controversy to GAAP profitability. FY2025 was the first full GAAP profitable year. Q1 2026 at $927M (+50% YoY) shows the crypto/trading volume leverage.

**The Gold thesis:** Gold subscribers exceeded 3M growing ~25% YoY. At $120/year, 3M subscribers = $360M ARR — defensible recurring revenue independent of trading volumes. Gold includes 5% APY cash sweep, 3% IRA match, margin at 6%, and research — genuine value at $10/month for active investors.

**Risk:** PFOF (payment for order flow) is 30-50% of non-Gold revenue and faces SEC regulatory scrutiny — an SEC ban would materially impair the business model. Crypto revenue is highly cyclical (crypto winter = revenue crater). Competition from Schwab, Fidelity, and new entrants is intensifying. Super-voting structure means Tenev controls the company regardless of public shareholder votes.

**Why not higher:** The business is good but the moat is not deep. Commission-free trading is now table stakes — every broker has it. The Gold subscription is real but faces competition from bank sweep accounts. PFOF risk is a structural overhang that rational investors discount.
