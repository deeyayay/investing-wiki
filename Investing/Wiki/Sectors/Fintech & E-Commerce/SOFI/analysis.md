## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-05-16
> **Last validated:** 2026-05-18
> **Drift status:** On track — Q1 2026 verified: record $1.1B rev, 10th consecutive GAAP quarter, LPB $3.6B expansion confirmed; Tech Platform weakness known and already priced; thesis executing on all core pillars

SoFi Technologies is the first U.S. fintech to build a full-spectrum banking product suite on top of a national bank charter, then monetize that same technology infrastructure as a B2B platform to third-party banks and fintechs (Galileo). This two-sided architecture — consumer banking + B2B tech licensing — is what separates SoFi from every other digital bank. Without the charter, there's no deposit gathering. Without Galileo, there's no tech platform revenue. Together they create a compounding moat that improves with scale.

The bank charter, secured in 2022, is the structural differentiator. Deposits hit $37.5B in Q4 2025, enabling SoFi to fund its own loan book at near-zero cost rather than depending on expensive wholesale funding. This is the mechanism behind the margin expansion: as the deposit base grows, net interest margin expands. Ten consecutive quarters of GAAP profitability (through Q1 2026) is the proof point.

The bull case is the Financial Services Productivity Loop (FSPL): 75% of new SoFi Plus subscribers add a second product within 30 days. A member enters for a personal loan, adds a SoFi Money account (4.6% APY), adds SoFi Invest, adds a credit card. Each addition increases switching cost and lifetime value. With 14.7M+ members and 90% retention, the platform is compounding engagement at scale. Management targets $4.7B adjusted net revenue in 2026 (+30% YoY) and ~$1.6B adjusted EBITDA (~34% margin). The Loan Platform Business (LPB) — originating loans for third-party balance sheets, earning fee income without credit risk — is the highest-quality growth driver. CEO Anthony Noto's open-market buying (39 purchases, 0 sales over 5 years; purchased ~$1M in shares around the May 2026 earnings report) is the strongest insider alignment signal in the portfolio.

**Key moat:** National bank charter enabling deposit gathering at scale ($37.5B), Galileo B2B tech platform (158.4M global accounts), FSPL cross-sell engine (75% 30-day second product adoption), 90% member retention, capital-light Loan Platform Business.

**Key risks:** Valuation premium (~33x 2026 EPS vs. 10–12x for traditional banks — punishes any miss), consumer lending book credit quality in macro downturn (personal loans are unsecured), regulatory compliance burden of bank charter, competitive pressure from incumbent banks building digital-first products, LPB growth dependent on third-party balance sheet appetite which is rate-cycle sensitive.
