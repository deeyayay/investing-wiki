# MELI — MercadoLibre Inc.
*Last updated: May 7, 2026*

---

## One-Line Thesis
The dominant e-commerce and fintech compounder in Latin America; two massive TAMs (retail + financial services) with a defensible network effect moat and a founder still at the helm.

---

## Investment Thesis
MercadoLibre is the closest thing to an Amazon + PayPal combination in Latin America, a region of 650M+ people with massive under-penetration in both e-commerce and formal banking. Mercado Libre (marketplace) and Mercado Pago (fintech) reinforce each other — payments fund logistics, logistics drives GMV, GMV grows the lending book. The compounding is structural. Founder Marcos Galperin has been CEO since 1999, giving this rare long-term alignment. The thesis is simply that LatAm's digital economy inflects over the next decade and MELI captures a disproportionate share of that value creation.

**Key moat:** Network effects (buyers/sellers), proprietary logistics (Mercado Envíos), payments ecosystem (Mercado Pago), credit flywheel, brand dominance.

**Key risks:** Brazil/Argentina macro — FX volatility and inflation impacts, regulatory risk across multiple LatAm jurisdictions, local competition from Shopee and Amazon LatAm, credit book quality in a macro downturn.

**Execution track record:** Under Galperin's 26-year tenure MELI compounded revenue at sustained high rates; FY2025 net revenue reached $28.9B (+39% YoY), capping 27 consecutive quarters of 30%+ growth. Q1 2026 revenue accelerated to +49% YoY. The company successfully expanded from marketplace into logistics (Mercado Envíos), fintech (Mercado Pago), and credit — each reinforcing the others. Margin compression in early 2026 reflects deliberate investment rather than competitive deterioration. CEO transition to Szarfsztejn was planned and orderly; Galperin retains strategic oversight as Executive Chairman.

**Insider ownership / alignment:** Galperin Trust holds ~7% of shares outstanding (classified as institutional but family-controlled). Institutional ownership ~82% overall. MELI is a foreign private issuer (incorporated in Delaware, listed on NASDAQ) and files 20-F annual reports rather than DEF 14A proxy statements; granular proxy-level ownership disclosures are not available via EDGAR DEF 14A search.
