## One-Line Thesis

MercadoLibre is the Amazon + PayPal of Latin America — 27 consecutive quarters of 30%+ growth with GAAP profitability across a region of 650M people with massive e-commerce and fintech under-penetration.

## Investment Thesis

Latin America is the most under-penetrated large e-commerce and fintech market on Earth. E-commerce penetration sits at ~10% vs. 20%+ in the US and 30%+ in China. Formal banking penetration is below 50% in Brazil and Mexico — the two largest markets. MercadoLibre is not just capturing this penetration; it is creating the infrastructure that makes penetration possible.

**The flywheel:** Mercado Pago (payments) enables Mercado Libre (marketplace) to accept digital payments → GMV growth funds Mercado Envíos (logistics) investment → faster delivery drives more GMV → larger GMV creates the credit data to build Mercado Crédito (lending) → lending profits fund the next cycle. Four self-reinforcing loops running simultaneously at continental scale.

**Why unrivaled:** Amazon entered LatAm and retreated (Brazil exit 2021). No single competitor can replicate all four flywheels simultaneously — marketplace, payments, logistics, credit each require years of regulatory relationships, warehouse footprint, and credit bureau integration. MELI has 27 consecutive quarters of 30%+ growth because the market is still early and the moat compounds.

**New leadership:** Ariel Szarfsztejn became CEO January 1, 2026 — internal promotion, ran the Commerce division. Marcos Galperin (26-year CEO) remains Executive Chairman with ~7% stake. This is the best kind of succession: domain expert with institutional continuity, not a hired gun.

**Risk:** LatAm FX volatility (Brazilian Real, Argentine Peso) creates reported USD revenue noise. Deliberate investment phase compressing near-term margins. Argentina macro instability (though Argentina is a small revenue contributor). Competition from local fintechs (Nubank, PagSeguro) in payments.
