## Structural Gaps

### Gap: SpaceX / Starlink — Dominant Actor, Not Investable
SpaceX controls >60% of commercial launch by mass and is the only company with a functioning LEO broadband mega-constellation at scale (Starlink, >6,000 satellites). It is private with no near-term IPO pathway. This creates a structural gap in the investable universe: the dominant LEO connectivity provider and launch supplier cannot be directly owned. **Watch for:** Any Starlink partial IPO filing or secondary share offering; indirect exposure via SpaceX suppliers (RKLB sells to SpaceX competitors; KTOS makes satcom ground systems components).

### Gap: GaN T/R Module Supply for Phased Array Payloads
The gallium nitride MMIC at Ka/Ku-band frequencies is the core performance element in flat-panel phased array satellite payloads. Production is concentrated at foundries with compound semiconductor capability (WIN Semiconductors, Qorvo, Wolfspeed/RFMD heritage, and classified government fabs). No pure-play GaN MMIC satellite payload foundry is publicly listed. **Watch for:** MACOM (MTSI), Qorvo (QRVO), or WIN Semi gaining satellite payload customer wins.

### Gap: Radiation-Hardened Electronics Supply
Space-grade rad-hard FPGAs (Microchip RTG4, BAE FPGA) and solar cells (Spectrolab, a Boeing subsidiary; Azur Space, private) are produced by a small number of qualified suppliers. These are long-qualification-cycle components with no commercial equivalent. The supply chain is government-program-paced and not well represented by public pure-plays. **Watch for:** Any fabless rad-hard chip startup going public; Microchip Technology's space revenue segment (MCHP).

### Gap: Optical Inter-Satellite Link (ISL) Terminals
Free-space optical ISL (laser communication terminals at 1550 nm coherent) is the technology enabling Starlink Gen 2 and Telesat Lightspeed's mesh routing. The key supplier (Mynaric, Germany-listed) and Tesat-Spacecom (Airbus subsidiary) are either foreign-listed or private. There is no US-listed pure-play optical ISL terminal company. **Watch for:** Mynaric (MYNA on NASDAQ/Frankfurt) volume ISL production ramps; any US optical ISL startup IPO.
