## One-Line Thesis

AST SpaceMobile is the only satellite constellation that connects directly to unmodified smartphones — if the BlueBird architecture scales, every MNO on Earth becomes a distribution partner and 3.5B unconnected people become potential subscribers.

## Investment Thesis

Mobile network operators have a coverage problem they cannot solve with ground-based towers: oceans, mountains, rural areas, and developing markets cost too much to wire. Starlink solves this with a terminal (cost: $300-600). AST SpaceMobile's BlueBird satellites use massive phased-array antennas — some spanning hundreds of square meters of aperture — to amplify cellular signals directly to existing phones using the same frequencies MNOs already license. No terminal. No app. No hardware. The subscriber's existing smartphone just works.

**The MNO wholesale model:** AT&T, Verizon, Vodafone, Rakuten, and others pay ASTS a wholesale fee (per-subscriber, per-MB, or capacity-based) to add satellite coverage to their existing plans. This means ASTS doesn't need to acquire retail subscribers — the MNOs do that. ASTS is infrastructure, not consumer retail. The addressable market is every MNO subscriber who travels outside tower coverage, plus every MNO in markets with poor rural infrastructure.

**Why BlueBird works when others failed:** Previous direct-to-device attempts (Iridium, Globalstar) required purpose-built phones and narrowband data. BlueBird uses massive aperture (the satellite antenna is 100x larger than any previous LEO satellite) to achieve the link budget required for broadband to a standard LTE/5G handset. The physics worked in testing; Block 1 satellites confirmed commercial viability.

**Abel Avellan's alignment:** 18.5% equity ownership + super-voting Class C shares means Avellan controls the company regardless of dilution. He has demonstrated remarkable capital efficiency getting from startup to commercial service with limited capital.

**Risk:** The capital requirement to scale is enormous — 100+ Block 2 satellites at $10M+ each plus launch costs. Dilution risk is severe. SpaceX is developing direct-to-cell Starlink (partnership with T-Mobile), which competes directly. ITU spectrum disputes could emerge. The $10B market cap represents enormous execution risk.
