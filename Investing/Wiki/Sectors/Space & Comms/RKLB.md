*Last updated: May 18, 2026*

The only vertically integrated launch provider with proven mission cadence outside SpaceX, compounding into a space systems business that serves as the DoD's indispensable second source.

## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** May 18, 2026
> **Last validated:** May 18, 2026
> **Drift status:** On track — Q1 2026 record revenue and $2.2B backlog confirm Space Systems durability; Neutron secured first commercial commitments pre-launch

Rocket Lab is a full-stack space company with two revenue engines: Launch Services (Electron small-lift rocket; Neutron medium-lift in development) and Space Systems (spacecraft buses, satellite components, propulsion systems, and mission management). Its vertical integration — designing and manufacturing most components in-house — gives it cost control and mission assurance that competitors buying commodity parts cannot replicate. Space Systems contributes ~71% of revenue, providing recurring component income independent of launch cadence and reducing binary dependence on launch outcomes.

The bull case rests on structural demand for a second reliable provider. Rocket Lab executed 21 Electron missions in 2025 with a 100% mission success rate — a record no other small-launch competitor approaches. The U.S. government (DoD, NRO, NASA) has a categorical need to avoid sole dependence on SpaceX for national security payloads, and RKLB is the only entity currently positioned to fill that role at commercial cadence. This creates a strategic premium and a durable floor under government contract awards. With 57% of backlog from government sources, the launch business is effectively partially underwritten by national security imperative.

The Neutron medium-lift rocket is the thesis accelerant. If Neutron reaches commercial operations in 2026–2027 as targeted, it expands RKLB's addressable launch market from a small-sat niche into a segment competitive with SpaceX Falcon 9, enabling significantly larger government and commercial constellation contracts. Neutron transforms RKLB from a credible niche launcher into a full-spectrum launch provider — a step-change in revenue ceiling.

The critical risk is Neutron execution. The current valuation prices in meaningful Neutron success; a multi-year delay compresses the story back to Electron-only fundamentals at a premium multiple. Blue Origin's New Glenn is now operational and actively competing for the same government medium-lift missions Neutron targets — first-mover displacement risk is real and growing. RKLB remains cash-burning pre-Neutron profitability, requiring disciplined capital markets access to avoid dilutive raises that erode per-share value.

**Key moat:** Mission trust with DoD/NRO/NASA; vertical integration enabling cost control and rapid iteration; sole proven non-SpaceX commercial launch provider at scale.

**Key risks:** Neutron delay or failure rewrites thesis; Blue Origin New Glenn pre-empts medium-lift market; ongoing cash burn requires capital markets access; government backlog concentration creates contract renewal risk.
