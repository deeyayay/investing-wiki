## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** May 19, 2026
> **Last validated:** May 19, 2026
> **Drift status:** On track — initial thesis established; monitor for ARR growth sustaining >25% and machine identity TAM expansion

SailPoint is the enterprise identity governance platform — the system that decides who (and what) gets access to which corporate resources, and audits that access continuously. It sits at the intersection of two compounding trends: the explosion of machine and non-human identities (service accounts, AI agents, bots, APIs) and the regulatory mandate for organizations to prove they know who has access to what. As AI deployments proliferate, the ratio of machine to human identities is projected to reach 45:1 by 2026 — each of those machine identities needs to be provisioned, governed, and audited. SailPoint's platform is built to handle this at enterprise scale; most point solutions are not.

The business crossed $1B ARR with 28% YoY growth, with ~50% of Fortune 500 companies as customers and a net revenue retention rate of ~120% — meaning existing customers expand their spend annually. The $250K+ ARR customer cohort grew 27% YoY to 1,090 customers; the $1M+ cohort grew 48% YoY to 185 customers — the upper tier accelerating faster signals an enterprise-grade upsell motion that is working. Full-year FY2026 revenue guidance of $1.069B represents 24% growth.

The agentic AI angle is structurally important: every AI agent deployed inside an enterprise needs identity credentials, access permissions, and an audit trail. SailPoint's platform — identity provisioning, access certification, policy enforcement, and logging — maps directly onto what enterprises will need to deploy autonomous systems safely. The tweet that surfaced this name frames it precisely: CRWD/PANW/FTNT are the "first order" perimeter defense; SailPoint is the "second order" identity infrastructure layer that becomes essential immediately after.

**Key moat:** 150+ deep enterprise connectors (AWS to legacy COBOL mainframes — switching cost is enormous), AI-powered identity modeling patents, data moat from the identity graph built across Fortune 500 deployments, ~120% net revenue retention (customers expanding, not churning), Thoma Bravo backing (operational discipline, SaaS expertise).

**Key risks:** Microsoft Entra and Okta bundle IAM into broader enterprise agreements and compete in the mid-market; Thoma Bravo controlling ownership creates governance overhang and potential secondary offering risk; premium SaaS multiple in a rate-sensitive market; execution risk on machine identity expansion (new product surface, not core legacy IGA); 23% short float signals meaningful skepticism.
