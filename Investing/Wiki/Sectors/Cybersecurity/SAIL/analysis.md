## One-Line Thesis

SailPoint is the identity governance control plane that every AI agent, bot, and human employee must pass through — machine identities growing 45:1 vs. humans makes this the fastest-expanding TAM in enterprise security.

## Investment Thesis

Identity governance is the permission system for the enterprise — SailPoint decides who (and what) gets access to which resource, certifies that access continuously, and revokes it instantly. This is unglamorous but mission-critical: compliance mandates (SOX, HIPAA, GDPR) make IGA non-discretionary spend. Embedded in ~50% of Fortune 500 companies with 150+ deep enterprise connectors reaching from AWS to 40-year-old COBOL mainframes.

**The machine identity thesis:** By 2026, machine identities (AI agents, service accounts, bots, APIs) outnumber human identities 45:1 in enterprise environments. Every AI agent needs: provisioning (who is this agent?), governance (what can it access?), and audit (what did it do?). SailPoint's platform governs both human and machine identities on a single data model. This is not a feature add — it's a structural TAM expansion from ~$13B IGA to $40B+ unified identity security.

**Moat:** 150+ deep connectors represent years of integration engineering that cannot be replicated quickly. Fortune 500 customer identity graphs (who accessed what, when, with what justification) become proprietary AI training data for anomaly detection. $1B+ ARR at 28% growth, 120% NRR, and 185 customers >$1M ARR (+48% YoY) proves enterprise lock-in.

**Thoma Bravo:** Controlling shareholder brings operational discipline from private equity — cost structure optimization post-re-IPO. Governance risk (controlled company) is balanced by strategic patience that public markets rarely provide.

**Risk:** Mark McClain (co-founder CEO) with only 2.5% ownership creates some alignment gap. Thoma Bravo overhang as PE exits over time. Competition from Microsoft (Entra), Okta, CyberArk in adjacent spaces.
