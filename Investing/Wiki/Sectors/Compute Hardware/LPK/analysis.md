## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-19
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-19
> **Drift status:** On track — CPO waveguide equipment already shipped to customer; SDAX inclusion confirmed; adjacent TAM materializing ahead of schedule

LPKF Laser & Electronics SE makes the LIDE (Laser-Induced Deep Etching) system — currently the only commercially viable method for drilling precision through-glass vias (TGVs) in glass core substrates. Glass substrates are the next frontier in AI chip packaging: superior electrical performance, dimensional stability, and thermal properties vs. silicon interposers make them the preferred substrate for co-packaged AI accelerators and co-packaged optics. With the supply chain moving from R&D into commercial production in 2026-2027, LPKF is the sole gatekeeper to this transition.

The bull case is a classic sole-source capital equipment story: one qualified supplier, no proven substitute process, and a customer base that has already committed to the technology direction. 80%+ of major global players — Intel, Samsung, LG Innotek, Absolics, Corning — have selected LPKF equipment for process validation. LPKF booked its first production-scale order in Q1 2026, with multiple additional buyers reportedly in pipeline (4-5 buyers; 3-10 machine orders each, per corroborated investor meeting notes). CEO Klaus Fiedler has publicly stated a 70% LIDE market share target. Long-term TAM has expanded well beyond former internal estimates toward a market analysts size at €5-10B of equipment opportunity by 2030. The NEXAR platform extends revenue beyond LIDE to glass singulation, multilayer bonding, ABF ablation, and co-packaged optics integration.

The moat is LPKF's IP in the LIDE process itself — laser-based drilling of micron-precise holes in thick glass without cracking it, a feat that remains technically non-trivial for competitors. This is reinforced by installed-base lock-in: customers who validate on LPKF equipment face high switching costs to qualify an alternative. The NEXAR full-line platform deepens the moat across the entire glass packaging process chain, not just the drilling step.

Near-term financials are pressured: 2026 guidance is €105-120M revenue with a −3% to +4.5% EBIT margin, as solar (formerly a material revenue source) has collapsed (Q1 2026 solar revenue −32% YoY due to industry pause for perovskite transition). If the advanced packaging ramp takes longer than expected — industry HVM targeted 2027, TSMC mass production 2028 — LPKF could face multi-year cash burn. A competing glass drilling technology could also emerge, though no incumbent has a production-ready LIDE equivalent. Third risk: glass substrates lose the substrate race to advanced silicon or organic alternatives, though current momentum strongly favors glass.

**Key moat:** Sole-source LIDE process IP with installed-base lock-in and NEXAR full-line platform expansion.

**Key risks:** Timing of HVM ramp (gated by downstream metalization/plating qualification, not LIDE); solar segment in cyclical trough; balance sheet pressure until packaging revenue materializes; potential for competing substrate technology.
