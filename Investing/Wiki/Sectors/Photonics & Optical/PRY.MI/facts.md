## Company Overview

Prysmian is the world's largest cable manufacturer, spanning optical fiber cables, power transmission cables, submarine cables, and specialty cables. In the photonics context, its optical cable division supplies the physical fiber infrastructure that carries all data center interconnect, FTTH broadband, and telecom backbone traffic. Prysmian does not make the glass fiber itself (that is Corning's domain) but manufactures the finished cables — wrapping, protecting, and engineering the fiber for specific deployment environments. The company's submarine cable business (HVDC cables for offshore wind farms) is a separate but important structural growth driver tied to energy transition.
