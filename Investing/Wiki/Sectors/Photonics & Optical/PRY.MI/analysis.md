## One-Line Thesis

Prysmian is the physical infrastructure layer of the data economy — the world's largest optical fiber cable and submarine power cable manufacturer, with a €10B+ offshore wind backlog and growing data center fiber cable demand that makes it a picks-and-shovels play on both AI infrastructure and energy transition.

## Investment Thesis

Every optical fiber network — hyperscaler data center, FTTH broadband, 5G backhaul, subsea cable — requires Prysmian's finished optical fiber cables. Corning may make the glass fiber, but Prysmian wraps it, armors it, connectorizes it, and delivers the cable ready to install. As the world's #1 optical cable and #1 submarine power cable manufacturer, Prysmian sits at the intersection of two of the largest infrastructure buildout cycles of the decade: AI data center fiber and offshore wind power.

**Optical fiber cable demand:** AI data center campuses require massive amounts of inter-building fiber — trunk cables, MPO/MTP backbone cables, and armored outside plant cables. Every new hyperscaler campus or cloud region represents a Prysmian sales opportunity. The company has supply agreements with multiple hyperscalers and the optical cable lead times have extended as demand outpaces manufacturing capacity.

**Offshore wind submarine cables — the bigger opportunity:** Prysmian's HVDC (high-voltage direct current) submarine power cables are the enabling infrastructure for offshore wind farms. A single offshore wind project (Dogger Bank, Hornsea, Vineyard Wind) requires 100-400 km of submarine HVDC cable at $500K+ per km — a single project can be a €100-200M cable contract. Prysmian's backlog exceeds €10B, providing 2-3 years of revenue visibility.

**Why 7.0 Strong, not higher:**
- P:3 — Prysmian is a manufacturer, not an IP company; the moat is scale and specialized ships, not patents
- PP:3 — pricing power is real (backlog extending = market tightening) but limited by European competition (Nexans, NKT)
- L:3 — Battaini is competent but not an exceptional founder-type leader
- FP:3 — growth is tied to infrastructure buildout cycles, which eventually slow; not compounding like software
- FH:4 — strong: €16B revenue, consistent FCF, investment-grade credit
