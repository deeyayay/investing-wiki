## One-Line Thesis

Viavi is the quality gate for every fiber network build — its OTDR test equipment must touch every fiber link before it goes live — but this steady-eddy franchise trades at a deserved discount to its optical peers because it lacks the AI transceiver or chip-level growth story.

## Investment Thesis

Viavi's value proposition is unglamorous but durable: you cannot commission a fiber optic network without test equipment. Every fiber cable installed by AT&T, Verizon, Openreach, or any carrier must be tested with an OTDR (optical time domain reflectometer) to verify the splice quality and identify faults before traffic runs. This makes Viavi's NSE (Network & Service Enablement) division an infrastructure tax on network buildout — not the growth story of a transceiver or chip company, but a steady recurring revenue stream.

**Two distinct businesses:**

**NSE — Network Test:** OTDR instruments + optical spectrum analyzers + network performance management software (Observer Platform). The equipment side is capex-tied to carrier network buildout cycles; the software side is more recurring. Switching costs come from embedded field workflows — Viavi-trained technicians, Viavi-calibrated test procedures, Viavi software integrated into NOC dashboards. This is an "installed base" business, not a growth story.

**OSP — Optical Security:** Anti-counterfeiting optical thin films for currency, pharmaceuticals, government IDs. Contracts with central banks (US Federal Reserve, ECB, Swiss National Bank) provide multi-year, low-churn revenue. This is a specialty manufacturing franchise with limited TAM but very stable cash flows.

**Why this is a 6.0 Strong, not higher:**
- No AI chip/transceiver exposure — the test business benefits from fiber capex, not GPU capex
- Telecom carrier capex cyclicality creates revenue volatility
- IPO Photonics, Spirent, EXFO, Keysight all compete in network test — no dominant market position
- Limited growth catalysts: 5G network build + fiber-to-home are positive but not accelerating

**Why this is not below 6.0:**
- 54%+ gross margins from software + optical film mix
- Installed base moat in carrier field test
- OSP anti-counterfeiting = stable recurring cash flows
- JDSU/Viavi institutional franchise with deep carrier relationships
