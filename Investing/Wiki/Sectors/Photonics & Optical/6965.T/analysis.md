## One-Line Thesis

Hamamatsu Photonics holds a global monopoly in photomultiplier tubes and silicon photomultipliers — the invisible photon detectors inside every PET scanner, every particle physics experiment, and every high-sensitivity scientific instrument — with the most defensible IP position in all of photonics.

## Investment Thesis

Hamamatsu Photonics is one of the most exceptional industrial monopolies in the world that most investors have never heard of. The company holds a genuine global monopoly in photomultiplier tubes (PMTs) — ultra-sensitive photon detectors that amplify a single photon into a measurable electrical pulse. This is not a monopoly by regulatory fiat or network effect but by technology leadership sustained over 70 years: Hamamatsu's PMT technology requires an intimate combination of glass-blowing expertise, cathode material science, vacuum processing, and dynode engineering that no other company has replicated at commercial scale.

**Why PMT monopoly matters:** PMTs are essential in medical imaging (every PET/CT scanner uses them to detect gamma ray coincidences), nuclear and particle physics (Super-Kamiokande used 11,129 Hamamatsu PMTs; Nobel Prize in Physics 2015 used Hamamatsu detectors; IceCube neutrino detector in Antarctica), scientific instruments (chromatography fluorescence detectors, bioluminescence readers, photon-counting spectroscopy), and nuclear plant monitoring. Hamamatsu has no meaningful competitor for high-sensitivity PMT applications. When Siemens or GE HealthCare designs a new PET scanner, there is no PMT vendor to evaluate besides Hamamatsu.

**Silicon photomultipliers (SiPM/MPPC):** The next-generation solid-state equivalent of the PMT, SiPMs are replacing PMTs in some applications where size, magnetic field tolerance, and lower voltage operation matter. Hamamatsu leads the SiPM market — they are not disrupting themselves, they are leading the transition to the replacement technology they invented.

**R&D reinvestment is the moat maintenance mechanism:** Hamamatsu invests ~15% of revenue in R&D — extraordinary for a manufacturer — which is why the company has maintained its technology leadership for seven decades. This culture of reinvestment is not an expense; it is the mechanism by which the monopoly is continuously extended.

**Why 8.5 Unrivaled:**
- PMT monopoly is genuine — no competitor at scale
- SiPM/MPPC leadership extends the monopoly into next-gen technology
- 22%+ operating margins sustained through all market cycles
- Net cash balance sheet; no leverage risk
- Medical imaging + particle physics + scientific instruments = three recession-resistant end markets
- Macro environment 4 (not 5) because PMT/SiPM TAM is large but not AI-scale; growth is structural, not explosive
