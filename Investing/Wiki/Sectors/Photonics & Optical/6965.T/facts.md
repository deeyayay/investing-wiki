## Company Overview

Hamamatsu Photonics is the most important company in photon detection — a world most people have never heard of, supplying the detectors that make medical imaging, particle physics, and scientific instruments possible. The company holds a global monopoly in photomultiplier tubes (PMTs): when a photon needs to be detected with extreme sensitivity (single-photon counting), Hamamatsu makes the component. Every PET scanner (Positron Emission Tomography) in the world uses Hamamatsu PMTs or silicon photomultipliers. The Super-Kamiokande neutrino detector (which won the Nobel Prize) uses over 11,000 Hamamatsu PMTs. CERN's LHCb detector uses Hamamatsu silicon photomultipliers. Hamamatsu's culture of reinvesting ~15% of revenue into R&D — extraordinary for a manufacturer — has maintained its technological leadership for 70 years.
