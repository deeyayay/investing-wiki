## One-Line Thesis
The structural chokepoint nobody talks about — indium phosphide (InP) wafer substrates are the base material for every high-speed optical laser, NVIDIA has been pre-allocating capacity from top suppliers since late 2025, and AXTI has a record $60M InP backlog with plans to double capacity by end-2026.

## Investment Thesis
AXT manufactures compound semiconductor substrates — primarily indium phosphide (InP), gallium arsenide (GaAs), and germanium — that serve as the foundation for the lasers and detectors inside high-speed optical transceivers. InP is irreplaceable: silicon can't generate light efficiently, and every EML laser starts with an InP wafer. NVIDIA's aggressive pre-allocation of EML capacity in late 2025 pushed lead times past 2027, cascading a supply shortage back to the substrate layer. AXTI sits at that chokepoint. FY2025 revenue: $88.3M (down YoY due to Chinese export permit timing), but record $60M InP backlog. Plans to double InP manufacturing capacity by end-2026 are the key execution milestone.

**Key moat:** One of only a handful of InP substrate suppliers globally, decades of compound semiconductor crystal growth expertise, multi-substrate capability (InP + GaAs + Ge).

**Key risks:** China export permit volatility, capacity doubling execution risk, lumpy demand tied to EML/transceiver production schedules, stock may have run significantly.
