## One-Line Thesis

POET's optical interposer monolithically integrates what every competitor bonds together — if commercialized, it eliminates a fundamental cost and loss bottleneck in photonic chip manufacturing, but it's a pre-revenue binary risk like LWLG at one supply chain layer deeper.

## Investment Thesis

POET Technologies is solving a problem at the heart of silicon photonics manufacturing: how to integrate III-V active devices (lasers, modulators, photodetectors) with passive optical waveguides and CMOS electronics without the losses and complexity of hybrid bonding. Every optical transceiver made by Coherent, Lumentum, or InnoLight today requires bonding or wire-bonding separate chips together — a process that introduces optical coupling loss, mechanical reliability risk, and manufacturing yield constraints. The POET Platform fabricates all these components in a single monolithic process on an optical interposer.

**Why this matters for AI infrastructure:** Co-packaged optics (CPO) is the architecture that integrates optical transceivers directly with switch ASICs — eliminating pluggable transceiver modules entirely. CPO requires laser sources, modulators, and detectors co-located on the package. POET's monolithic approach could enable the most compact, lowest-loss CPO implementation possible. The partnership with InnoLight — the world's largest transceiver ODM supplier — validates that a major commercial partner believes in the technology.

**The commercialization path:** POET is further along than pure academic research: InnoLight and Mitsubishi partnerships provide real commercial qualification pipelines. If InnoLight qualifies a POET-based AI data center transceiver for hyperscaler deployment, POET's royalty or component revenue could be substantial relative to its current $110M USD market cap.

**Why this is Average (5.5) not Strong:**
- Pre-revenue; zero commercial revenue to date
- Technology must demonstrate foundry-scale CMOS compatibility (Singapore NTU fab is not a production foundry)
- InnoLight partnership = customer interest, not a purchase order
- FH:1 — cash burn with dilution risk; TSX Venture listing limits institutional participation
- Leadership (FP:5 — highest score): Suresh Venkatesan brings GLOBALFOUNDRIES commercialization credibility, but even he cannot collapse the foundry qualification timeline
