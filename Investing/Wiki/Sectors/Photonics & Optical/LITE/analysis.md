## One-Line Thesis

Lumentum holds the most defensible positions in photonics — Apple VCSEL monopoly + telecom ROADM duopoly + coherent transceiver — but the Apple concentration risk and telecom capex cyclicality keep this permanently undervalued relative to its technology quality.

## Investment Thesis

Lumentum is the most technology-focused pure-play photonics company, with the highest gross margins (48–50%) in the sector. The portfolio has three pillars: (1) VCSEL arrays for Apple Face ID and LiDAR — sole-source supplier with multi-year product qualification cycles creating deep switching costs; (2) ROADM systems for telecom optical networks — #2 globally with proprietary tunable laser technology; (3) coherent optical transceivers — expanded via NeoPhotonics acquisition (2022) adding InP chip capability for 400G/800G data center interconnect.

**Apple VCSEL: the anchor.** Lumentum supplies the VCSEL arrays that power Face ID on every iPhone. The relationship dates to iPhone X (2017) and has survived supply chain reviews — a testament to the qualification lock-in. This creates a near-monopoly revenue stream tied to iPhone volumes, but it also creates concentration risk (~30% of total revenue) that keeps the stock at a discount to peers.

**ROADM recovery:** Telecom carriers overbought optical equipment in 2022–2023 and then paused. The inventory correction is largely done, and network capex is normalizing. ROADM revenue should recover to prior levels as carriers upgrade to 400G backbone capacity.

**NeoPhotonics integration:** The 2022 acquisition added InP-based coherent chips, enabling Lumentum to compete in 400ZR/800ZR data center interconnect. This is the growth vector most aligned with AI infrastructure buildout.

**Risk — Apple cycle:** Any softness in iPhone unit volumes or Apple's decision to reduce Face ID prominence in lower-cost models directly impacts 30% of revenue. Long-term, Apple's move toward silicon photonics in-house (for LiDAR sensing) is a structural displacement risk.

**Risk — telecom capex:** ROADM revenue is tied to carrier network investment cycles, which are lumpy and often correlated with macro conditions.
