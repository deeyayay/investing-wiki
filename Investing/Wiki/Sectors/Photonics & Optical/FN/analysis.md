## One-Line Thesis

Fabrinet is the TSMC of optical components — the world's only scaled precision photonic assembly platform, directly in the path of the AI transceiver supercycle with 15% operating margins that defy contract manufacturing norms.

## Investment Thesis

Fabrinet has built the most defensible position in photonics manufacturing that most investors overlook: the company is the sole-source precision assembly partner for every major optical component OEM. Coherent, Lumentum, Ciena, and MACOM all outsource their most complex optical assemblies to Fabrinet because optical alignment — bonding fiber to chip at sub-micron precision, hermetic sealing, lens assembly — requires specialized cleanroom expertise that is economically impossible for any individual OEM to replicate at scale.

**The TSMC analogy holds exactly:** Just as semiconductor OEMs (AMD, Qualcomm, Apple) outsource chip fabrication to TSMC rather than building their own fabs, optical OEMs outsource complex module assembly to Fabrinet. The switching cost is the process qualification — re-qualifying a new assembler takes 12–18 months and risks product performance. Once qualified, Fabrinet is embedded in the product bill of materials.

**NVIDIA certification = AI supercycle direct exposure:** Fabrinet is NVIDIA-certified for AI transceiver assemblies, meaning the 800G transceivers that connect GPU clusters in NVL72 and GB200 rack architectures are assembled at Fabrinet. As NVIDIA's data center TAM grows, Fabrinet's volumes grow in lockstep — without the R&D and IP risk of an OEM.

**The margin paradox:** Contract manufacturers typically earn 3–8% operating margins. Fabrinet earns ~15% because the assembly is not commodity assembly — it is precision photonic alignment requiring specialized capital equipment, cleanroom environments, and technician expertise that cannot be replicated in standard contract manufacturing facilities. This is a true moat embedded in the process capability.

**Clean balance sheet:** Net cash position, no leverage, consistently GAAP profitable. Fabrinet can weather industry cycles without dilutive equity raises — a rare advantage in the volatile optical sector.

**Risk — Thailand concentration:** A single major natural disaster (flood, political disruption) in Thailand could disrupt all manufacturing. The 2011 Thailand floods disrupted hard drive supply chains globally — optical assembly faces the same geographic risk.

**Risk — customer concentration:** Top 3 customers (~50% of revenue) means Coherent alone is a significant exposure. If Coherent insourced assembly or moved to a competitor, revenue impact would be severe.
