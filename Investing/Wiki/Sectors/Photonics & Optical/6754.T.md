## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** May 31, 2026
> **Last validated:** May 31, 2026
> **Drift status:** On track — initial thesis established; monitor for first earnings validation

Anritsu Corporation (~$779M TTM revenue, ~$3.34B market cap) is a Japanese test and measurement company focused on digital communications testing: mobile networks (5G/6G), optical communications, and microwave/RF. In the photonics context, Anritsu's Network Master Pro (MT1040A/MT1000A series) is the portable coherent optical network tester used to commission and troubleshoot coherent DWDM links in the field and in production.

The AI datacenter thesis for Anritsu centers on the proliferation of coherent ZR/ZR+ optics. As the datacenter interconnect market shifts from CWDM4/FR4 pluggables to 100ZR/400ZR/800ZR coherent transceivers for DCI (datacenter interconnect) and long-haul backhaul, operators need field instruments that can read the FEC error counters, OSNR values, and coherent DSP telemetry inside these modules. The MT1040A added 100ZR coherent evaluation in 2025 and demonstrated interoperability with multiple 100ZR vendors at ECOC2025. This positions Anritsu's portable tester as the commissioning tool of choice for coherent link deployment — a market growing with every DCI buildout.

Anritsu also has a mobile test business (network emulators, protocol analyzers for 5G/6G) and a food/pharmaceutical inspection business (metal detectors, X-ray inspection systems) which together represent a significant revenue mix that is not optical. The photonics T&M angle is a meaningful growth vector, not the whole company thesis.

The key moat is the combination of protocol depth (Anritsu has historically been strong in telecom standard compliance testing — OIF, ITU-T, IEEE) and portable form factor (the MT1040A is battery-powered and field-deployable, unlike rack-mount lab instruments). Network operators and their contractors are trained on Anritsu instruments, creating switching costs in field commissioning workflows.

**Key moat:** Protocol compliance depth for coherent ZR/ZR+ standards; portable field-deployable form factor with industry-standard interfaces; established relationships with telecom operators and system integrators who qualify vendor equipment on Anritsu instruments.

**Key risks:** Coherent T&M revenue is a portion of a diversified company; 5G/6G test market is highly competitive (Keysight, Rohde & Schwarz, Spirent); Japan exposure and yen dynamics; shift to software-defined test platforms could commoditize instrument hardware.
