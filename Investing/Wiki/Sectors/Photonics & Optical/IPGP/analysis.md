## One-Line Thesis
The world's dominant vertically-integrated fiber-laser manufacturer; its CW and pulsed fiber lasers are the high-power "external light source" of the industrial economy, with growth optionality in directed-energy weapons and adjacency to the photonic-interconnect light-source role in the AI buildout.

## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-15 / **Last validated:** 2026-06-15 / **Drift status:** New

IPG Photonics built a structural cost moat by vertically integrating the entire fiber-laser stack — it fabricates its own semiconductor pump diodes, draws its own specialty fibers, and assembles complete laser systems in-house. That integration let it commoditize the high-power CW fiber-laser market and displace legacy CO₂ and lamp-pumped lasers across cutting, welding, and marking. The franchise is cyclical and currently working through a trough as Chinese materials-processing demand normalizes and domestic Chinese competitors pressure the low end.

The forward call is mix-shift: management (CEO Mark Gitin, ex-Coherent/MKS) is steering toward higher-value applications — medical, micromachining, and **directed energy**. The CROSSBOW counter-UAS high-energy laser line and IPG Defense (Huntsville, AL) won a ~$10M Lockheed Martin order in early 2026, validating the defense optionality amid drone-warfare demand. Separately, IPG's high-power CW laser IP is adjacent to the **L07 Interconnect "External Light Sources"** role that co-packaged and coherent optics need at scale — an emerging, unproven datacenter angle, but real optionality if external laser sourcing becomes a bottleneck.

**Key moat:** In-house diode→fiber→system vertical integration (hard to replicate; net-cash, no-debt balance sheet funds the down-cycle).
**Key risks:** Deep cyclicality tied to China + auto/EV capex; Chinese fiber-laser commoditization at the low end; directed-energy and datacenter-light-source optionality are early and lumpy.
