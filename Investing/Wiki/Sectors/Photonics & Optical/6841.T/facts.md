## Company Overview

Yokogawa Electric is a Japanese industrial technology company primarily known for its DCS (Distributed Control System) process automation franchise — the backbone control systems in oil refineries, chemical plants, pharmaceutical manufacturing, and food processing facilities. The switching costs in DCS are among the highest in any industrial technology: once a plant is built around a Yokogawa CENTUM VP DCS, replacing it requires a complete control engineering redesign during a planned plant shutdown. This creates a 20-30 year lock-in lifecycle. The test & measurement business (oscilloscopes, power analyzers, precision instrumentation) is the photonics-adjacent segment, used for optical fiber measurement and electronics testing, but it is a minor part of overall revenue. Yokogawa's inclusion in the Photonics & Optical sector reflects its fiber measurement tools, but the primary investment thesis is the DCS automation franchise.
