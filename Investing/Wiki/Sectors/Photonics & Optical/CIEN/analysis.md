## One-Line Thesis

Ciena's WaveLogic coherent ASIC is the backbone of the internet's capacity expansion — as hyperscalers extend coherent optics inside their own data centers, Ciena transitions from a telecom cycle stock to a structural AI infrastructure compounder.

## Investment Thesis

Ciena has spent 25 years building the most sophisticated coherent optical networking platform in the world. The WaveLogic ASIC — designed entirely in-house — achieves the highest spectral efficiency in DWDM systems, meaning networks built on WaveLogic can carry more data on existing fiber than any competitor's equipment. This is a genuine technology moat: fiber is the most expensive, slowest-to-deploy component of any network, and Ciena's technology lets operators avoid laying new fiber by extracting more capacity from what exists.

**The carrier-to-cloud shift:** Ciena was historically a telecom carrier equipment supplier, subject to the lumpy capital expenditure cycles of AT&T, Verizon, and global telcos. That business remains important but is now being complemented by web-scale cloud operators (Google, Microsoft, Amazon) who need the same coherent optical technology to connect their own data centers. Google alone runs one of the world's largest coherent optical networks — and Ciena is a preferred supplier. This mix shift toward cloud operators is improving revenue visibility and margin quality.

**800ZR coherent pluggable — the AI data center opportunity:** Traditional data center interconnect (DCI) used gray optics (simple, short-range, cheap). AI cluster buildout is forcing hyperscalers to connect data centers 10–100 km apart with coherent optics — using 800ZR pluggable transceivers in standard router/switch ports. Ciena's WaveLogic technology is directly applicable to 800ZR, creating a new addressable market inside data centers for a company historically only in carrier networks.

**Gary Smith's cycle expertise:** 24 years as CEO means Smith has navigated every optical capex cycle since the dot-com era. He manages inventory and supplier relationships through downturns better than any other management team in the sector, and has consistently rebuilt the company after each trough stronger than before.

**Risk — telecom concentration:** Despite the cloud shift, carrier customers still represent ~60% of Ciena revenue. Any macro recession that freezes carrier capex = revenue risk.

**Risk — Nokia competition:** Nokia Optical Networks (including Infinera, now acquired) is the #1 DWDM competitor with comparable technology and deep carrier relationships. The duopoly is stable but competitive.
