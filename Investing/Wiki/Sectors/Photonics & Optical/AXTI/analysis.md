## One-Line Thesis

AXT is the micro-cap supplier of the InP and GaAs substrates that every photonic device is built on — a 40-year compound semiconductor crystal growth franchise riding the AI transceiver and 5G tailwind, constrained by China manufacturing concentration and scale economics.

## Investment Thesis

InP (indium phosphide) wafers are the bedrock of high-speed optical transceivers. The laser diodes and photodetectors inside every 400G/800G transceiver are fabricated on InP substrates — there is no alternative material for these specific wavelength/speed combinations at commercial scale. AXT has been growing compound semiconductor crystals since 1986, accumulating process expertise in InP, GaAs, and Ge crystal growth that is not easily replicated. As AI data center transceiver demand drives 800G/1.6T volumes, InP substrate demand scales directly.

**The three substrate markets:**
- **InP:** Optical transceivers (AI data center), 5G power amplifiers, photonic integrated circuits — highest growth vector
- **GaAs:** Power amplifiers for mobile phones and base stations, space solar panels — stable volume
- **Ge (Germanium):** Space solar cells for satellite constellations (Starlink, OneWeb), high-efficiency terrestrial solar — growing with satellite buildout

**The China problem:** AXT manufactures through Chaozhou Sanjing, a 75%-owned Chinese subsidiary. This gives cost competitiveness (significantly lower labor costs than Japanese or German substrate suppliers) but creates geopolitical concentration risk. Any escalation in US-China trade restrictions on compound semiconductors — already a sensitive area given III-V substrates' dual military-commercial use — could disrupt supply chains or impose export controls.

**Why this is Average (5.5), not Strong:**
- Small scale ($80M revenue) gives no pricing power vs. Sumitomo Electric or Freiberger
- China manufacturing concentration is a binary geopolitical risk
- Gross margins (~30%) are low for a specialty materials company
- Competition from much-larger Coherent/II-VI for InP substrates

**Speculative long:** If US government mandates US-sourced III-V substrates for defense/telecom, AXT's Fremont CA HQ + ability to shift production creates optionality. But this is a tail scenario.
