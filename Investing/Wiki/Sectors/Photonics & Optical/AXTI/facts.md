## Company Overview

AXT Inc. is one of the few publicly traded III-V compound semiconductor substrate manufacturers. The company grows single-crystal compound semiconductor boules — indium phosphide (InP), gallium arsenide (GaAs), and germanium (Ge) — and slices them into wafers that are the raw material for optical devices, power amplifiers, and space solar cells. InP is the critical substrate for high-speed optical transceivers (used in 800G AI data center interconnect) and 5G power amplifiers. The manufacturing is done primarily through AXT's Chinese subsidiary (Chaozhou Sanjing), giving it cost competitiveness but significant geopolitical exposure. Morris Young co-founded the company in 1986 and has operated it for 40 years.

- China manufacturing: Chaozhou Sanjing subsidiary (~75% owned) — geopolitical risk
- Competing with Sumitomo Electric, Freiberger, Coherent for substrate supply
