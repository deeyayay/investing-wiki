## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** May 21, 2026
> **Last validated:** May 21, 2026
> **Drift status:** On track — initial thesis established; monitor for H2 2026 FAU mass-production ramp and TSMC COUPE adoption timeline

FOCI Fiber Optic Communications designs and manufactures the fiber optic components at the heart of co-packaged optics (CPO) modules. Founded in 1995 as an ITRI (Industrial Technology Research Institute) spinout in Hsinchu, Taiwan, the company has spent three decades building precision fiber interconnect expertise. Its current strategic focus is the fiber array unit (FAU) — the component that couples optical fiber to photonic chips in CPO assemblies. FAU is not glamorous, but it is the tolerance-critical interface that determines whether a CPO module works or fails: sub-micron alignment between fiber core and silicon photonic waveguide is required at scale, and FOCI's proprietary ReLFACon (Reflowable Lensed Fiber Array Connector) technology is designed to meet that specification in high-volume automated production.

The bull case is concentrated on TSMC's COUPE (COmpact Universal Photonic Engine) architecture, which is the de facto CPO standard for NVIDIA's next-generation Spectrum-X and Rubin platforms. FOCI has been identified by Citrini Research and Hunterbrook Media as a key FAU supplier within COUPE, alongside Himax Technologies — which took a 5.3% equity stake in FOCI via private placement, explicitly to unite FOCI's CPO technology with Himax's wafer-level optics (WLO) for TSMC COUPE integration. That equity investment is the most concrete supply-chain validation signal available for a Taiwanese OTC-listed company with limited English disclosure. FOCI plans to begin small-scale FAU production in 2025 and mass-produce 1.6T and 3.2T FAUs in H2 2026, targeting a "revenue surge within 3 years."

The company's 2025 revenue was TWD 1.89B (~$58M USD) growing +38.7% YoY — the growth rate accelerating as CPO design-wins convert from sampling to initial volume. At a ~TWD 87B (~$2.7B USD) market cap, the market is pricing in the full CPO ramp thesis with limited margin of safety: the implied EV/Revenue is approximately 46x on 2025 revenue. The valuation requires H2 2026 FAU mass-production to materialize on schedule and TSMC COUPE to become the dominant CPO architecture as NVIDIA scales Spectrum-X and Rubin across hyperscaler clusters.

**Key moat:** ReLFACon precision technology for high-yield FAU manufacturing; 30-year compound semiconductor fiber expertise; strategic equity position in Himax partnership for TSMC COUPE; ITRI heritage gives ongoing access to Taiwan's deep photonics research network.

**Key risks:** Extreme valuation (~46x EV/Revenue on 2025 revenue) prices in flawless CPO execution; TSMC COUPE adoption timeline uncertainty; grating-coupled CPO architectures could displace FAU requirement (structural substitute risk); limited English disclosure and TPEX listing creates information asymmetry; FAU commoditization if multiple suppliers qualify; single-architecture concentration on COUPE.
