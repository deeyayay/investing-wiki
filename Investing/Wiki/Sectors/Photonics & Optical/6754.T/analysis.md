## One-Line Thesis

Anritsu's 5G protocol conformance test platform is embedded in the device certification process for every major smartphone chipmaker — a structural position that makes it the test infrastructure layer of the global 5G rollout, with optical OTDR providing a stable optical sector exposure.

## Investment Thesis

Anritsu has carved out a differentiated position in the $6B+ wireless test equipment market by focusing on protocol conformance testing — the process by which 5G base stations and devices must prove they correctly implement 3GPP standards before they can be deployed commercially. The ME7834NR 5G network emulator/callbox is one of only a few systems globally certified by 3GPP for device conformance testing, which means every Qualcomm, MediaTek, Samsung, and Apple chipset must run through Anritsu (or Keysight, R&S) test systems before it can receive carrier approval for commercial sale.

**The structural moat:** 3GPP certification is a mandatory regulatory process — there is no alternative path. While Keysight and R&S are larger competitors, Anritsu has maintained its position as a certified vendor in this market. Any 5G chip launch (Snapdragon X75, Dimensity 9300, etc.) requires Anritsu ME7834NR qualification runs. This creates recurring revenue from every chip generation cycle.

**Optical OTDR business:** Anritsu's MT9085 Access Master OTDR directly competes with Viavi's OneExpert platform for fiber network commissioning. The business is smaller than Viavi's but serves a similar function — fiber quality verification before network activation. As 5G fiber backhaul buildout accelerates, OTDR demand grows.
