## Company Overview

Coherent Corp (formerly II-VI, after 2022 merger with Finisar) is the broadest photonics company by product scope — spanning coherent optical transceivers (100G→800G), VCSEL arrays (LiDAR, 3D sensing, data center), SiC and compound semiconductor substrates, laser systems, and optical components. The merger created the only company capable of vertical integration from raw compound semiconductor wafers through finished optical modules. The primary growth driver is AI data center interconnect: 800G QSFP-DD transceivers connecting GPU clusters at NVIDIA, Meta, Google, and Amazon.

## Key Metrics

- Market cap: ~$15B
- Revenue FY2025: ~$5B (telecom + datacom + industrial + SiC)
- Gross margin: ~38–40% (improving as product mix shifts to higher-margin AI transceivers)
- Net debt: ~$4B (from II-VI/Finisar merger financing — primary financial constraint)
- 800G transceiver: ramping in volume for AI hyperscaler orders
- SiC substrates: growing business serving EV power modules and power electronics
- CEO Jim Anderson: mandate to rationalize portfolio and expand margins post-merger
