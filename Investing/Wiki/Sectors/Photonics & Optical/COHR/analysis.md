## One-Line Thesis

Coherent is the broadest photonics platform in the AI transceiver supercycle — vertical integration from compound semiconductor wafers to 800G modules creates margin expansion runway if Jim Anderson can clean up the merger debt overhang.

## Investment Thesis

The II-VI + Finisar merger created a photonics company with unprecedented scope: compound semiconductor substrates (InP, GaAs, SiC), VCSEL arrays, laser gain chips, coherent transceiver engines, and finished 800G modules — all vertically integrated. No other company matches this breadth. The strategic bet is that AI data center buildout requires 800G+ transceiver volumes at scale, and only Coherent can supply the complete technology stack from wafer to module.

**Why AI is a structural tailwind:** GPU clusters require optical interconnects at every distance — within racks (silicon photonics, VCSEL), between racks (800G QSFP-DD transceivers), and between data centers (coherent 400ZR/800ZR). Coherent participates at every layer. NVIDIA's rack-scale architecture (NVL72, GB200) requires Coherent-qualified optical modules for high-density interconnect.

**SiC substrate optionality:** Coherent is a top-3 SiC substrate supplier globally. As EV adoption and power electronics demand grows, SiC substrate revenue provides an orthogonal growth driver uncorrelated to telecom/datacom cycles.

**The margin recovery story:** Post-merger gross margins (~38%) are below the Finisar standalone level (~45%) because of integration disruption and telecom mix. Jim Anderson's mandate is to rationalize the product portfolio, cut lower-margin telecom commodity lines, and expand margins toward 42–45%. If successful, this is a compounding earnings story even without revenue growth.

**Risk — debt:** $4B+ net debt from the merger is the primary financial constraint. Interest burden limits financial flexibility and acquisition capability. The leverage ratio must fall before the full margin recovery story is credible.

**Risk — competition:** InnoLight, II-VI China operations, and Asian ODMs are aggressively pricing 800G transceivers. Coherent's vertical integration advantage must translate to better performance (reach, power, density) not just lower cost.
