## One-Line Thesis

nLIGHT is a US-made fiber and semiconductor laser company with an increasingly valuable defense franchise in directed energy weapons — the industrial business competes against IPG Photonics at a structural disadvantage, but the defense segment is growing with DoD laser weapon spending and carries DoD-style margins.

## Investment Thesis

nLIGHT operates in two laser markets that could not be more different in competitive dynamics:

**Industrial Lasers — the problem:** IPG Photonics is the Intel of industrial fiber lasers — it invented the modern high-power fiber laser, has 30+ years of manufacturing learning, vertically integrates from fiber preform to finished laser, and has roughly 4-5x nLIGHT's revenue. IPG can and does undercut on price to defend market share. Any nLIGHT growth in industrial is fought for against a much larger, more efficient competitor. Industrial margins are ~20-25%.

**Defense Lasers — the opportunity:** DoD directed energy weapons (DEW), laser rangefinders, missile defense laser countermeasures, and hypersonic test illumination lasers all require US-made, export-controlled, security-cleared high-power lasers. nLIGHT's Camas WA and Vancouver WA manufacturing facilities are cleared and US-domiciled — a mandatory prerequisite for these programs. IPG Photonics, despite being headquartered in the US, has significant Russian-heritage manufacturing history (founder Valentin Gapontsev was Russian; IPG has historically manufactured components in Russia) — creating defense program risk that benefits nLIGHT.

**The directed energy thesis:** DoD investment in laser weapons has grown substantially since 2020: high-energy laser (HEL) programs at Northrop Grumman, Raytheon, and L3Harris are scaling from demonstrations to fielded systems. Counter-drone laser systems are being deployed with US Army and USMC units. Each of these systems requires high-brightness semiconductor or fiber laser pump sources — nLIGHT's specialty. Defense contracts are often sole-source or limited-competition, carry higher margins (~35-40%), and have multi-year duration.

**The bear case:** Scott Keeney has been fighting IPG for 26 years and nLIGHT is still only $270M revenue vs. IPG at ~$1.2B. The defense thesis requires sustained DoD laser weapon budget allocation — any budget cycle reset or program cancellation hits nLIGHT disproportionately given the defense revenue concentration.
