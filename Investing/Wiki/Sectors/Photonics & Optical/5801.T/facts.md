## Company Overview

Furukawa Electric is a 150-year-old Japanese industrial conglomerate most notable in photonics for inventing the VAD (Vapor-phase Axial Deposition) optical fiber preform process — the foundational manufacturing technology that enabled mass-market optical fiber production. The optical communications division produces finished optical fiber cables, fiber optic splitters, WDM (wavelength division multiplexing) components, and EDFA optical amplifiers. The company operates OFS Fitel (a US subsidiary) giving it North American optical fiber manufacturing capacity. Furukawa is a conglomerate, with automotive copper wiring harnesses as its largest segment — a structure that limits the pure-play optical premium. The optical division benefits from data center fiber demand and FTTH buildout globally.
