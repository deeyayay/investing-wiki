# Photonics & Optical — Sector Framework

_Created: May 11, 2026 | Review quarterly or after major sector events_

---

## Why This Sector Exists in the Portfolio

AI data centers require optical interconnects because electrons can't move data fast enough or efficiently enough between GPUs at scale. Every AI cluster — Blackwell, Rubin, whatever follows — needs optical transceivers, lasers, modulators, and the test infrastructure to validate them. As GPU speeds increase, the optical layer has to keep up. That replacement cycle (400G → 800G → 1.6T → 3.2T) is not a one-time event — it's a recurring demand driver that forces capital reinvestment every 2–3 years. This makes the sector structurally different from most tech hardware: there is no "good enough" resting point.

The investment thesis is not "AI will succeed." It's narrower and more durable: **regardless of which AI model wins or which hyperscaler leads, optical interconnects are mandatory infrastructure**. COHR and LITE don't care if it's NVIDIA or Google or Meta building the cluster. That demand indifference is the foundation of the sector's investment appeal.

---

## How Value Is Created in This Sector

Three mechanisms drive value creation, in order of durability:

**1. Technological chokepoints.** The most valuable positions are supply constraints that cannot be quickly replicated — LITE's monopoly on 200G EML lasers at volume, AXTI's InP wafer substrate supply, COHR's multi-material (InP + GaAs + SiPh) transceiver platform. These positions command pricing power and order lead times that stretch years. The moat is not brand or distribution — it is IP, manufacturing process knowledge, and the time required to replicate it at volume. Identify these first and weight them most heavily.

**2. Architectural transitions.** Each speed generation is a re-procurement event. Customers cannot upgrade transceivers the way they upgrade software. A switch from 800G to 1.6T requires new components, new test equipment, and new validation cycles. Companies positioned at the frontier of each transition capture outsized revenue in the 18–24 month window when new supply is constrained and demand is acute.

**3. Scale and vertical integration.** At the contract manufacturing and systems layer, moat comes from manufacturing precision and customer switching costs rather than IP. FN's optical alignment processes, CIEN's WaveLogic DSP, and VIAV's 100+ year optical coating library are not easily replicated — but they're also not monopolies. These positions compound more slowly but more predictably.

## Industry Structure — Value Chain Map

|Layer|Role|Key Players|Moat Type|Margin Profile|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Substrate**|Raw material (InP, GaAs wafers)|AXTI, SIVE|Supply constraint + process IP|Low-medium, volatile|
|**Component**|Laser chips, EMLs, transceivers|LITE, COHR, AAOI|IP + manufacturing scale|High, expanding|
|**Systems**|Coherent optical networking platforms|CIEN|DSP IP + relationships|Medium, stable|
|**Contract Mfg**|Precision assembly + testing|FN|Process expertise + switching costs|Low-medium, consistent|
|**Test & Measurement**|Validating networks and components|VIAV|Domain expertise + installed base|Medium, cyclical|
|**Pre-Revenue**|Next-gen material/architecture bets|LWLG, POET|IP option value|None|
|**Defense Crossover**|High-power lasers for DEW + industrial|LASR|Defense relationships + process|Medium|

**Structural insight:** This is NOT a winner-take-most market at the top level. COHR and LITE can both succeed. However, within specific sub-markets (200G EML, InP substrates, CPO engines), monopoly or near-monopoly positions exist — and that's where the highest returns concentrate.

## Company Archetypes and How to Evaluate Them

### Archetype 1: Component Leaders (COHR, LITE)

_High IP, pricing power, direct AI capex leverage_

**What matters most:** Moat within specific product lines (which products have no second source?), gross margin trajectory (should expand as volumes scale), R&D as % of revenue (is the moat being defended?), customer concentration and contract structure.

**Valuation:** During hypergrowth, EV/Revenue is more useful than P/E. Compare EV/Revenue to revenue growth rate: a company growing at 70% YoY should trade at a meaningfully higher multiple than one growing at 20%. P/E normalizes later in the cycle (2028–2030) when growth moderates.

**Warning signs:** Gross margin compression, revenue deceleration before guidance acknowledges it, customer announcing in-sourcing of key components.

---

### Archetype 2: Systems Integrators (CIEN)

_Coherent networking platforms, sticky relationships, slower but durable growth_

**What matters most:** Backlog and book-to-bill ratio (leading indicator), deferred revenue build (customers paying forward = pricing power), WaveLogic DSP performance lead vs. Nokia/Infinera, hyperscaler DCI contract win rate.

**Valuation:** P/E or EV/EBITDA. Deferred revenue is an underappreciated quality signal — CIEN's $390.9M deferred position means customers are contractually committed forward. Don't pay more than 50x P/E for a systems integrator in mid-cycle.

**Warning signs:** Book-to-bill falling below 1.0, hyperscalers announcing proprietary optical networking, Nokia or Infinera winning flagship hyperscaler contracts.

---

### Archetype 3: Contract Manufacturers (FN)

_Volume leverage on the entire sector, no product risk, clean balance sheet_

**What matters most:** Revenue growth vs. industry unit volume (should track closely), gross margin stability, customer concentration risk (LITE + COHR), inventory build as leading indicator of demand.

**Valuation:** P/E with a premium for zero debt and capital efficiency. Rarely justify more than 35x P/E in mid-to-late cycle. Watch the new HPC revenue category as the growth optionality.

**Warning signs:** Gross margin declining below 10%, major customer announcing in-house manufacturing, inventory build without revenue follow-through within 2 quarters.

---

### Archetype 4: Test & Measurement (VIAV)

_Picks-and-shovels, sticky installed base, cyclical revenue_

**What matters most:** NSE segment revenue growth (tracks optical capex cycle), OSP segment as counter-cyclical buffer, acquisition strategy quality, recurring service revenue %.

**Valuation:** EV/EBITDA adjusted for acquisition noise. Fair value is 20–25x EBITDA. The undisclosed FY2026 acquisition (flagged in 10-Q note) needs to be identified before sizing up.

**Warning signs:** NSE revenue decelerating for 2+ quarters, goodwill impairment, hyperscalers internalizing test capability.

---

### Archetype 5: Pre-Revenue Bets (LWLG, POET)

_Binary outcomes, technology option value, size for total loss_

**What matters most:** Cash runway vs. time to first production commitment (must overlap), quality of design-win customers, manufacturing partnership credibility, quarterly burn rate.

**Valuation:** Probability-weighted outcome analysis. Assign probabilities to bull/base/bear cases. Position size such that the total loss in the bear case is tolerable as a % of portfolio.

**LWLG specific:** Cash is $34.9M (Sep 2025), not the $70M cited in some analyses. Runway is tight to the 2027 ramp — no margin for delay.

**Warning signs:** Cash burn accelerating without milestone progress, design-win customer going quiet, CEO/CTO departure.

---

### Archetype 6: Defense/Industrial Crossovers (LASR)

_Dual catalyst, non-correlated defense revenue, indirect AI exposure_

**What matters most:** Defense contract announcements (program of record = highest conviction signal), directed energy timeline vs. DoD budget, industrial laser market recovery, gross margin mix shift toward defense.

**Valuation:** Blend defense P/E (25–30x) with industrial P/E (15–20x) weighted by segment mix. Monitor for mix shift toward defense as the re-rating catalyst.

**Warning signs:** DoD budget cuts to directed energy, industrial market depressed for 2+ quarters, IPGP winning key defense contracts.

## Where We Are in the Industry Cycle

**Current phase: Early-to-mid hypergrowth (2025–2028)**

This is not the 2001 telecom bubble — that was speculative demand built on "if you build it they will come." This demand is cash-flow funded, contractually committed (NVIDIA's $4B in LITE + COHR), and tied to AI inference revenue hyperscalers are already capturing.

Phase characteristics right now:

- Supply is constrained (backlogs, multi-year lead times, capacity doubling programs)
- Pricing is favorable (LITE raising EML prices, COHR expanding non-GAAP margins 18.6% → 20.3%)
- New entrants emerging but not yet at scale
- Valuations elevated but earnable given growth rates

**Signals that this phase is ending — watch for:**

- Hyperscaler AI capex guidance deceleration (most important leading indicator)
- Inventory build at component makers that doesn't resolve within 2 quarters
- Chinese entrants (Accelink, InnoLight) reaching quality parity in Western markets
- Lead times collapsing from years to weeks (supply caught up to demand)
- 1.6T commoditizing before 3.2T reaches production

**The 2028–2030 transition:** Growth moderates, margins and FCF become primary valuation drivers. Right companies to hold through maturity: FN (clean balance sheet, volume leverage), COHR (multi-material moat), CIEN (sticky DSP relationships). Pre-revenue bets need to execute by 2027 or exit.

## Sector Bull / Base / Bear Cases

**Bull case (40%):** 1.6T adoption accelerates faster than supply can build, driving another leg of pricing power into 2027. CPO becomes the dominant architecture creating a new $20B+ sub-market where LITE and COHR have a 2–3 year head start. China restrictions on optical component imports remain in place. AXTI doubles InP capacity on time. LWLG lands a production commitment from a Tier 1 customer.

**Base case (45%):** Orderly 1.6T ramp through 2026–2027 with modestly constrained supply. COHR and LITE grow 20–30% annually. FN revenue tracks the industry. Pre-revenue names experience at least one delay and require small dilutive raises. Sector returns of 20–40%/year for 2–3 years before moderating.

**Bear case (15%):** Hyperscaler AI capex disappoints — macro recession, AI monetization miss, or regulatory action. Chinese suppliers reach quality parity faster than expected. 1.6T transition is slower than projected due to CPO delays. Sector de-rates 40–60% from peak. LWLG and POET run out of cash before production commitment.

## Ranking Criteria — Comparing Opportunities Within the Sector

When comparing any two photonics investments, score each on these five dimensions:

|Dimension|Weight|What a High Score Looks Like|
|---|---|---|
|**Chokepoint position**|30%|Monopoly or near-monopoly on a critical component with no near-term second source|
|**Financial quality**|25%|Net cash, expanding gross margins, strong operating cash flow, no impending debt maturity|
|**Catalyst visibility**|20%|Specific, near-term, high-probability events with confirmed customer orders|
|**Valuation vs. growth**|15%|PEG < 1.0 or EV/Revenue below peer median for equivalent growth rate|
|**Smart money conviction**|10%|Tier 1 investors (Druckenmiller, major funds) building positions in recent 13F filings|

**Important:** Smart money conviction is a confirmation signal, not a primary signal. Use it to validate a thesis you've already built — not as the reason to enter.

2. What are lead times for 800G and 1.6T transceivers? _(supply/demand balance)_
3. Are COHR and LITE gross margins expanding, stable, or compressing? _(pricing power)_
4. Is CIEN's deferred revenue building or depleting? _(forward demand visibility)_

6. Has LWLG received a production commitment from any Stage 3 customer? _(binary event)_
7. Are Chinese optical makers gaining share in Western markets? _(competitive threat)_
8. What are the most recent 13F filings saying about sector positioning? _(smart money)_
