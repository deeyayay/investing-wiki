## One-Line Thesis
Defense + AI laser crossover with dual catalyst structure — a new 70kW-class directed energy milestone drove +92% YTD alongside the NVIDIA photonics wave, making nLIGHT the most differentiated name in the folder by risk profile.

## Investment Thesis
nLIGHT makes high-power fiber lasers and semiconductor lasers for industrial, defense, and microfabrication markets. The dual-catalyst thesis is what makes LASR interesting: defense business is non-correlated to the AI transceiver/interconnect theme, providing a separate revenue driver. On defense: new 70kW-class directed energy laser system announced early 2026 — significant milestone in the DoD's accelerating directed energy weapons (DEW) program. On AI: high-power lasers are used in manufacturing photonic components (dicing, welding, ablation), giving indirect exposure to the photonics supply chain buildout.

**Key moat:** High-power fiber laser expertise, vertically integrated semiconductor laser chip manufacturing, defense customer relationships and security clearances, dual-market buffer.

**Key risks:** Defense contract timing can be lumpy and classified, industrial laser market is competitive (IPG Photonics is dominant), AI exposure is more indirect vs. COHR/LITE/FN, directed energy narrative may already be priced in.

*(Append notable developments: partnerships, product launches, supply chain signals)*

- **2026-05-07** — HADES directed energy product line launched: scalable 70kW-class coherently beam-combined HEL with integrated atmospheric correction; first named/branded nLIGHT effector platform.
  **Why it matters:** HADES is the transition from component supplier to system-level platform vendor — it can be sold into RFPs and FMS programs as a complete product, not just sourced as a subcomponent. Scalable architecture (tens of kW → 1 MW) expands the addressable market from C-UAS to advanced missile defense.

- **2026-05-07** — Q1 2026: $80.2M revenue (+55.2% YoY); A&D $55.1M (+69% YoY), A&D product revenue $33.1M (+98%); record adj. EBITDA $13.9M; EPS $0.20 vs $0.09 est; first GAAP net income ($0.6M); Q2 guided $75–81M.
  **Why it matters:** A&D product revenue nearly doubling (+98%) and GAAP profitability in the same quarter confirm the defense revenue is now both large enough and high-margin enough to carry the P&L — the industrial-to-defense transition is structurally complete.

- **2026-03-02** — New 70kW-class directed energy laser system announced. Coincided with NVIDIA $4B photonics investment; defense-adjacent laser names lifted alongside pure AI plays. +92% YTD through April 22.
  **Why it matters:** 70kW is above the 50kW DE M-SHORAD threshold — positions LASR for next-generation DEW programs (Golden Dome, naval DEW) where higher power class is required for longer-range threats.

- **2023-11-27** — $34.5M U.S. Army DE M-SHORAD contract awarded (subcontractor to KORD Technologies); 50kW-class HEL on Stryker vehicle; 18-month execution.
  **Why it matters:** First program-of-record DEW contract for LASR — establishes the company as a qualified DEW hardware supplier to Army RCCTO, which is the gating credential for larger follow-on programs.
