## One-Line Thesis
nLIGHT is a vertically integrated directed energy platform — chips to complete weapon systems — with 69% A&D revenue, a first-mover HADES HEL product line, and DoD budget tailwinds approaching $1B/year.

## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** May 14, 2026
> **Last validated:** May 19, 2026
> **Drift status:** On track — upgraded; nLIGHT has completed its transition to a directed energy platform company; thesis rewritten to reflect HADES launch and A&D majority-revenue status

nLIGHT designs and manufactures high-power fiber lasers and semiconductor lasers with a unique vertical integration spanning from raw semiconductor laser chips through complete high-energy laser (HEL) weapon systems. This full-stack capability — 25 years in the making — is now the primary investment thesis. As of Q1 2026, aerospace and defense revenue was $55.1M (+69% YoY), representing 69% of total company revenue. The industrial laser segment that originally defined nLIGHT is now secondary. Q1 2026 produced the company's first GAAP-profitable quarter ($0.6M net income) and a record $13.9M adjusted EBITDA — structural proof that the defense revenue base is large enough and high-margin enough to carry the P&L without industrial support.

The bull case rests on HADES and the expanding DoD directed energy budget. HADES (launched May 7, 2026) is nLIGHT's first branded, production-ready HEL effector family — scalable from tens of kilowatts to 1 MW, with integrated coherent beam combining and atmospheric correction. It competes directly for defense RFPs and foreign military sales (FMS) as a complete system, not just a subcomponent. This is a category shift: nLIGHT moves from being a laser supplier embedded inside a prime contractor's weapon system to being a system-level platform vendor with its own contract vehicle. The addressable market expands accordingly — from the component slice of DEW programs to the full program value. DoD laser weapon budgets are running approximately $1B/year, with FY2027–FY2028 DEW prototype and procurement budgets funded and accelerating. The Golden Dome missile defense initiative and naval DEW programs provide additional upside optionality above the current run-rate.

The moat is vertical integration and program-of-record qualification. nLIGHT's stack runs from III-V semiconductor wafer growth and epitaxy through fiber laser manufacturing, beam combining, atmospheric correction optics, and now complete weapon system integration. No other publicly traded company spans this full range. The DE M-SHORAD contract (Army RCCTO, Nov 2023) established nLIGHT's credentials as a qualified DEW hardware supplier — a certification that takes years to accumulate and gates participation in all follow-on programs. Security clearances and government program relationships are accumulated, not purchased. Competitors like IPG Photonics offer industrial-first laser technology with defense exposure; they do not have the program-of-record qualification stack or the weapon-system-level product (HADES) that nLIGHT now fields.

Key risks are structural to defense contracting. Program timelines are classified and inherently lumpy — revenue recognition can shift quarters without public disclosure. HADES is a new product line with no production contract yet; the transition from product launch to program of record takes 12–36 months and is not guaranteed. The February 2026 equity offering (~$191M at $44/share) diluted existing shareholders ~6% to fund capacity scale-up — necessary but confirms that production ramp requires significant capital before it returns cash. Industrial laser revenue (31% of Q1) remains cyclically exposed to China manufacturing demand, which introduces a macro variable that is out of management's control.

**Key moat:** 25-year vertical integration from semiconductor laser chips to complete HEL weapon systems (HADES); DE M-SHORAD program-of-record qualification gating follow-on defense programs; coherent beam-combining and atmospheric correction IP with no direct pure-play public competitor.

**Key risks:** Defense contract lumpiness and classification limiting revenue visibility; HADES product-to-program-of-record timeline (12–36 months); ~6% dilution from Feb 2026 offering with further raises possible if production ramp requires additional capital; industrial segment cyclically exposed to China manufacturing demand.
