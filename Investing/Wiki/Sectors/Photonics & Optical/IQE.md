# IQE — IQE plc
*Last updated: 2026-05-18*

## One-Line Thesis

The world's only pure-play, full-portfolio epiwafer foundry, supplying the compound semiconductor recipes that no Tier-1 chip designer can replicate in-house.

## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-05-18
> **Last validated:** 2026-05-18
> **Drift status:** On track — MACOM anchor deal provides visibility and balance-sheet runway into 2027

IQE is the dominant independent manufacturer of compound semiconductor epiwafers — the ultra-thin crystalline layers (GaAs, InP, GaN, GaSb) grown onto substrates that define the performance of RF chips, lasers, power devices, and photonic components. It is the only merchant supplier that covers the full materials portfolio across all major epitaxy platforms (MOCVD and MBE) while maintaining manufacturing in three geographies (UK, US, Taiwan). Fabless chip designers and IDMs use IQE because growing reproducible high-yield epitaxial layers requires decades of proprietary "black box" process recipes and capital-intensive reactor fleets that are uneconomical to build internally at scale.

The bull case rests on two converging demand vectors: AI-driven data centre interconnects and military/defence electronics. Indium phosphide (InP) lasers are essential for the high-speed optical transceivers inside AI clusters (Nvidia-adjacent demand), and GaN-on-SiC remains the preferred material for defence radar and electronic warfare. IQE is actively qualifying 8-inch GaN-on-Silicon to reduce cost per wafer for EV and power applications, which represents a third long-duration growth leg. The April 2026 MACOM strategic investment — £81m raised including £30m equity and £15m convertible notes plus long-term supply agreements (LTSAs) for InP and GaN — provides anchor revenue visibility and eliminates near-term balance-sheet risk.

The primary moat is the combination of breadth and process depth: IQE holds hundreds of patents but more critically holds decades of undocumented, customer-specific crystal-growth recipes that are impossible to transfer. Switching costs are very high — any customer seeking a second source must re-qualify the epiwafer with each end-device OEM, a 12–24 month process. Scale also matters: MOCVD reactor economics improve significantly at high utilisation, and IQE's reactor fleet is the largest in the independent market.

Key risks are near-term cyclicality in the handset/wireless market (wireless was 52% of H1 2025 revenue, down 52% YoY on inventory digestion), customer concentration (MACOM at 11.5% now has board representation and supply-agreement leverage), continued statutory net losses (FY 2024 loss of £38m on £118m revenue), and execution risk on the 8-inch GaN scale-up. Revenue guidance for FY 2025 was cut to £90–100m with EBITDA potentially negative, signalling that the wireless downcycle has not yet fully cleared.

**Key moat:** Proprietary epitaxial process recipes (not just patents), full compound-semiconductor materials portfolio, and multi-geography dual-sourcing capability that no pure-play peer can replicate.

**Key risks:** Wireless/handset end-market cyclicality; MACOM customer concentration now formalized with board seat; sustained statutory losses and negative free cash flow; 8-inch GaN qualification timeline slippage.
