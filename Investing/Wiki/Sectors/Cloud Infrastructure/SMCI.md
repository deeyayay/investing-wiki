# SMCI — Super Micro Computer
*Last updated: May 7, 2026*

---

## One-Line Thesis
The largest GPU server ODM (original design manufacturer) and NVIDIA's biggest rack-scale systems partner — extremely high AI revenue leverage, but with significant governance and accounting risk that requires a discounted valuation lens.

---

## Investment Thesis
Super Micro builds and ships the actual GPU servers that go into AI data centers — DGX-compatible rack systems, NVL72 GPU rack solutions, and high-density compute nodes. They are NVIDIA's closest hardware partner and have first-mover advantage in liquid-cooled AI server design. At peak AI buildout, SMCI captures enormous revenue leverage because every large GPU order tends to include SMCI rack infrastructure. The bull case is simple: AI server TAM grows from ~$30B to $300B+ and SMCI maintains a 10-15% share. However, SMCI has well-documented accounting issues — they delayed multiple 10-K filings in 2024, faced a Hindenburg short report, auditor resignations, and a DOJ investigation. A new auditor has since been engaged and filings are current, but the governance scar tissue remains. This is a high-risk/high-reward name that requires a wider margin of safety.

**Key moat:** NVDA preferred ODM status, liquid cooling design leadership, fastest time-to-market on new GPU architectures, modular "building block" server architecture.

**Key risks:** Accounting/governance history, customer concentration (NVDA ecosystem), competition from Dell/HPE, ongoing regulatory scrutiny, execution risk at scale.
