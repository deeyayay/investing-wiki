## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-16
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-16
> **Drift status:** On track — AWS $37.6B Q1 (+28%); CapEx $44B/quarter; $364B backlog

Amazon Web Services is the world's leading cloud provider with approximately 33% market share, a 200+ service portfolio, and a $150B annualized revenue run rate entering mid-2026. AWS growth reaccelerated to 28% in Q1 2026 — the fastest pace in 15 quarters — driven by both the migration of enterprise workloads and an AI-specific demand wave that is still in early innings. With a $364B contractual backlog (plus a $100B strategic investment in Anthropic), AWS has multi-year revenue visibility that few infrastructure businesses can match. The core thesis is that AWS is building the foundational rails for the next computing paradigm, and its Q1 capex of $44.2B (guided to ~$200B for full-year 2026) reflects a deliberate land-grab for AI capacity before supply constraints ease.

Amazon's custom AI chips — Trainium (training) and Inferentia (inference) — are increasingly central to the investment thesis. Management has guided that Trainium could save tens of billions of dollars in capex per year versus relying on third-party GPU supply, while also generating several hundred basis points of AWS operating margin advantage. This mirrors the Apple/TSMC dynamic: vertical integration of compute reduces input costs, tightens the feedback loop between software and hardware, and creates a lock-in surface that hyperscaler rivals without proprietary silicon cannot easily replicate. As AI inference workloads scale to millions of end-users, the cost advantage of custom silicon compounds.

AWS's moat is a Platform/Ecosystem moat operating across three interlocking dimensions: (1) 200+ deeply integrated services create high migration friction for enterprise customers; (2) global data center scale (33 regions, 105 availability zones) provides latency and compliance coverage no startup can replicate; and (3) the AI stack — from Bedrock (foundation model access), to SageMaker (ML ops), to Trainium/Inferentia chips — is deepening ecosystem lock-in at the AI layer specifically. Amazon's $244B total company backlog (up 40% YoY) confirms that enterprise commitment is accelerating, not plateauing.

The primary risk is capex overshoot: Amazon is spending at a pace ($44B/quarter) that has compressed free cash flow from $25.9B to $1.2B year-over-year, and a demand air pocket or capacity glut could make these investments look poorly timed. Competitive pressure from Microsoft Azure (with OpenAI exclusivity) and Google Cloud (with Gemini) is real; both rivals are growing faster in AI-native workloads by some measures. Regulatory risk is elevated — Amazon faces antitrust scrutiny in both the US and EU. Trainium's adoption curve and software ecosystem maturity vs. NVIDIA CUDA's entrenched developer base is a key variable to monitor.

**Key moat:** 200+ integrated cloud services, global scale, proprietary AI silicon, and enterprise switching costs create a compounding platform moat.

**Key risks:** Capex-driven FCF compression, Azure/GCP AI competition, regulatory antitrust exposure, and Trainium CUDA ecosystem adoption risk.
