## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-05-07
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-17
> **Drift status:** On track — hyperscaler AI capex cycle driving accelerating AI networking demand; Ethernet winning at scale

Arista sells high-speed Ethernet switches and its EOS (Extensible Operating System) software to the world's largest data centers. The AI angle is this: NVIDIA GPU clusters originally ran on InfiniBand (NVDA's Quantum networking), but hyperscalers at scale are increasingly moving to Ethernet because it's cheaper, more flexible, and their engineering teams already know it. Arista dominates high-speed Ethernet switching (400G/800G) and has deep relationships with all five hyperscalers. As AI cluster sizes balloon to hundreds of thousands of GPUs, the networking layer becomes as important as the compute layer.

Arista's software moat (EOS) means customers don't just buy switches — they build their entire network automation stack around Arista's APIs. Founder-era CEO Jayshree Ullal and co-founder/Chief Architect Andreas Bechtolsheim have run this company with exceptional discipline: FY2025 delivered 29% revenue growth at a 48% non-GAAP operating margin — among the best in enterprise tech. The company hit $1B quarterly net income for the first time in Q4 2025 and accelerated its $10B revenue target two years ahead of schedule.

**Key moat:** EOS software ecosystem lock-in, hyperscaler relationships (Meta, Microsoft, Google are top customers), 400G/800G switching leadership, founder-era management culture.

**Key risks:** Cisco competitive response, InfiniBand resurgence (NVDA Quantum-X), customer concentration (Meta ~15% of revenue), custom silicon from hyperscalers, co-packaged optics transition risk.
