## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-15 / **Last validated:** 2026-06-15 / **Drift status:** New

Mobileye is the most production-proven public bet on physical AI in vehicles. Its vertical stack — the EyeQ vision SoC, REM (Road Experience Management) maps crowdsourced from millions of equipped cars, and the RSS formal safety model — is a genuine data-and-integration moat: the installed ADAS base both generates the mapping data and funds the higher-autonomy roadmap. Unlike pre-revenue AV names, Mobileye ships EyeQ at scale today, giving it real unit economics while it climbs the autonomy ladder.

The investment debate is about the ladder's slope versus the base's erosion. Entry-level ADAS (EyeQ4/EyeQ6L) faces price and volume pressure and competitive encroachment (NVIDIA DRIVE, Qualcomm Snapdragon Ride, Tesla's vertically integrated FSD, and fast-moving Chinese ADAS suppliers). The bull case is mix-shift: SuperVision and Chauffeur ("eyes-off") at higher content-per-vehicle, plus the Drive robotaxi platform. This wires Mobileye to the Edge & Physical AI surface's "Autonomous Vehicles" box and anchors a new "Autonomous Vehicles & ADAS" tier. Intel's controlling stake is both a stability anchor and an overhang (potential supply/secondary sales).

**Key moat:** REM data flywheel + EyeQ SoC + RSS safety model, monetized across a large shipping ADAS base.
**Key risks:** ADAS price/volume erosion; intense competition (NVIDIA/Qualcomm/Tesla/China); Intel-stake overhang; timing of eyes-off/robotaxi monetization.
