## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-27
> **Last validated:** 2026-06-27
> **Drift status:** Material change — onsemi all-stock acquisition announced June 25, 2026; SYNA is now primarily a merger arbitrage situation pending mid-2027 close

Synaptics is a fabless semiconductor company that designs human interface ICs, IoT wireless connectivity chips, and AI edge processors. Its portfolio spans three segments: Enterprise & Automotive (~54% of revenue) — display drivers, video conferencing SoCs, docking silicon; Core IoT (~32%) — Wi-Fi 7/8 + Bluetooth combo SoCs, Zigbee/Z-Wave/Thread smart home chips; and Mobile (~14%) — touchpad controllers and touchscreen ICs. All manufacturing is outsourced to foundry partners (TSMC and others). The company completed a strategic pivot from legacy PC HMI toward IoT connectivity and edge AI between 2022–2026, culminating in the January 2025 acquisition of Broadcom's Wi-Fi assets to establish a Wi-Fi 7/8 leadership position in IoT.

The standalone bull case was Core IoT's hypergrowth trajectory — from ~19% of revenue in FY2024 to ~33% by FY2026, growing >40% YoY to over $385M annually. The Astra AI processor platform, designed for edge inference in robotics and IoT devices, was generating early design wins with expected production ramp in 2027. This thesis was effectively validated by strategic acquirers: on June 25, 2026, onsemi (ON) announced an all-stock acquisition of Synaptics at 1.35x exchange ratio per SYNA share (~19% premium to 10-day VWAP, ~$7B EV). onsemi's stated rationale is to acquire Synaptics' Astra AI processors and IoT connectivity portfolio to accelerate its "Physical AI" strategy — AI embedded in devices, robotics, and industrial machines.

The moat that made SYNA attractive was multi-layered: 2,000+ patents covering touch, display, wireless, and AI processing; #1 PC touchpad controller market share with deep OEM driver/software integration creating meaningful switching costs; and the Astra AI platform differentiated by combining Wi-Fi 7 connectivity with edge AI inference in a single SoC. Competition from Goodix (touch), MediaTek (connectivity), NXP, and Qualcomm is intense across the portfolio, which is why pricing power is rated medium rather than high.

As a standalone investment after the acquisition announcement, SYNA is primarily a merger arb play. The 1.35 shares of ON stock per SYNA share structure means SYNA holders bear ON stock price risk from now until close. Key risks: regulatory approval (China/EU antitrust given both companies have SiC exposure overlap via onsemi's existing SiC business + Synaptics' edge AI); SYNA shareholder vote; ON stock price volatility; deal timeline (mid-2027 close means ~12 months of exposure). Pro forma combined revenue would be ~$7.8B with $200M annual synergies targeted within 18 months post-close.

**Key moat:** 2,000+ patents + #1 PC touchpad with OEM integration switching costs; Astra AI edge processor platform with Wi-Fi 7 integration.

**Key risks:** Merger arb risk (ON stock price, regulatory clearance, 12-month timeline); pre-deal standalone risks include customer concentration, negative FCF (7 of 8 quarters), and high competition from Qualcomm/MediaTek in Wi-Fi/connectivity.
