## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** 2026-06-15 / **Last validated:** 2026-06-15 / **Drift status:** New

AeroVironment is the entrenched Western incumbent in small unmanned aircraft systems and loitering munitions. Its Puma/Raven/Wasp/JUMP 20 sUAS and the Switchblade family are embedded in US and allied programs of record and have been combat-validated at scale in Ukraine — incumbency, ITAR/classified barriers, and battlefield reputation are the moat. The 2025 BlueHalo combination transformed AV from a drone manufacturer into a multi-domain platform spanning space, counter-UAS, electronic warfare, and directed energy, enlarging the addressable defense budget it can capture.

The thesis is leveraged to a structural rearmament cycle: drone-centric warfare, attritable autonomous systems, and counter-UAS are among the fastest-growing defense priorities, and AV is the clearest pure-play public name. As an edge-robotics story it fits the Edge & Physical AI surface's "Drones and UAVs" box and anchors a new "UAV & Drone Systems" tier. The offsets are classic defense-name risks: revenue lumpiness tied to government budgets and continuing resolutions, program-timing volatility, integration of BlueHalo, and intensifying competition from low-cost drone entrants (including loitering-munition startups).

**Key moat:** Programs of record + battlefield-proven incumbency + ITAR/classified barriers (made-in-USA defense supplier).
**Key risks:** Budget/CR-driven lumpiness; BlueHalo integration; low-cost-drone competition; valuation sensitivity to conflict-demand expectations.
