## Investment Thesis

> **Thesis established:** May 19, 2026
> **Last validated:** May 19, 2026
> **Drift status:** On track — Stereolabs acquisition broadens platform into Physical AI; Q4 2025 hit 62% GM but Q1 2026 pulled back to 46% on integration costs — watch recovery

Ouster makes digital lidar sensors — the perception hardware that lets robots, autonomous vehicles, drones, and smart infrastructure "see" the physical world in 3D. Unlike analog lidar incumbents, Ouster built its sensors around a proprietary digital SoC (system-on-chip) that reduces the sensor to two custom semiconductor chips. This architecture compresses cost and complexity, and is designed to follow a Moore's Law-like cost reduction curve as volumes scale — a structural advantage in a market facing intense pricing pressure from radar and camera alternatives.

The bull case rests on two converging tailwinds. First, the industrial and robotics market is buying lidar at scale: FY2024 revenue reached $111M across four verticals (industrial, automotive, robotics, smart infrastructure), with software-attached bookings growing 60% YoY — signaling a shift from commodity sensor sales toward recurring software revenue. Second, the inclusion of Ouster's OS1 sensor in the Pentagon's Blue UAS Framework (approved systems for military drone operations) opens a high-margin defense segment that is largely inaccessible to foreign-made lidar competitors. This certification was the catalyst for a 61% YTD stock surge in 2025 and is not easily replicated.

Margin trajectory is the most important near-term signal: gross margins expanded from 22% in Q4 2023 to 45% by Q2 2025. If this trend continues toward 50–55%, it validates that the digital SoC architecture is delivering cost leverage at scale, re-rating the business from a hardware vendor to a platform company with defensible economics.

**Key moat:** Proprietary digital SoC architecture (two chips vs. dozens in analog lidar), Pentagon Blue UAS Framework certification (competitive barrier for defense), software/perception layer increasing switching costs, co-founder engineering culture with 10+ year tenure.

**Key risks:** Not yet profitable; high valuation relative to current earnings; competition from imaging radar and pure-vision systems potentially displacing lidar in automotive; slow pace of autonomous vehicle commercial deployment; customer concentration risk across four verticals; new CFO transition (Gianella, May 2025) adds near-term execution uncertainty.
