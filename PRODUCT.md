# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo investor (Alex D) conducting deep research sessions. Uses the dashboard to navigate sector supply chains, track tickers through a D1–D5 dimensional taxonomy, surface cross-ticker signals, and build conviction before investment decisions. Context is a desk or laptop, unhurried analysis mode, not a glanceable mobile screen.

## Product Purpose

Investment research knowledge base and interactive dashboard. Maps sector supply chains company-agnostically, then attaches specific companies to nodes in each supply chain tier. Surfaces chokepoints, catalysts, earnings, analyst coverage, and sentiment in one place. Success means faster research cycles, clearer conviction, and a systematic way to track how structural shifts propagate across sectors.

## Brand Personality

Curious, structured, precise. Rewards exploration without demanding it. The tool should feel like a well-organized research notebook that also knows how to become a map.

## Anti-references

- **Bloomberg Terminal**: too cryptic, maximally dense green-on-black, makes expertise a barrier rather than a reward.
- **Robinhood / retail trading apps**: gamified, candy-colored, emotionally manipulative — treats investing as entertainment.
- **Generic SaaS dashboards**: flat card grids, hero-metric layouts, cream backgrounds, teal accents, every section shaped the same.

## Design Principles

1. **Instrument, not trophy** — this is a working research tool used daily. No decorative flourishes that distract from the work. Every visual element earns its place by carrying signal.
2. **Structure before style** — the D1–D5 taxonomy and sector → tier → company hierarchy is the product. Make the hierarchy viscerally legible through space and weight before reaching for color.
3. **Surface the signal** — chokepoints, catalysts, and conviction markers should read immediately. Supporting detail recedes; critical information commands attention.
4. **Density with breathing room** — information should be packed, not cramped. Tight groupings within sections, generous separations between them. The rhythm of space teaches the user where one idea ends and another begins.
5. **Curious confidence** — discovering a cross-sector relationship or a new supply chain node should feel like a reward. Exploration is encouraged through clear wayfinding, not punished with cognitive overhead.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG requirements. Light theme is the primary experience. Aim for comfortable contrast ratios as a natural consequence of good design; don't rely on hue alone to communicate state (use shape, position, or weight as a secondary cue for critical indicators like chokepoints).
